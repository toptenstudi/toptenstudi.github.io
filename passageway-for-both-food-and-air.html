What is the throat? The throat (pharynx and larynx) is a ring-like muscular tube that acts as the passageway for air, food and liquid. 26. Which structure of the upper respiratory tract serves as the passageway for both food and air?


#BrainlyFast

Mouth and Pharynx

Mouth and Pharynx are the structures or passageway for both food and air in the upper respiratory tract. We break down food into tiny pieces in the mouth and we can also breath through our mouth making it also a common passageway for both air and food. Pharynx is another structure of the upper respiratory tract which serves as a common passageway of food and air.

Epiglottis is the structure that somehow separates food from air upon entering the lower respiratory tract. When you eat foods, the epiglottis close the larynx so that food will not go down to the lower respiratory tract and instead it goes to the esophagus down to the stomach.
