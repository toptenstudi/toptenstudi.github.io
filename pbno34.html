1. What is the molarity of a solution made by adding 1.565 moles of PbNO3 to 500 mL?


The molarity of solution: 3.13 M

Further explanation

Molarity is a way to express the concentration of the solution  

Molarity shows the number of moles of solute in every 1 liter of solute or mmol in each ml of solution  

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{M=\dfrac{n}{V}}}[/tex]  

Where  

M = Molarity  

n = number of moles of solute  

V = Volume of solution  

The molarity of PbNO₃

moles = 4.565

Volume = 500 ml = 0.5 L

then :

[tex]\rm M=\dfrac{1.565}{0.5}\\\\M=\boxed{\bold{3.13}}[/tex]

(a) K2C2O4(aq) + Ba(OH)2(aq) ⟶ 2KOH(aq) + BaC2O4(s)

Complete ionic equation:

K+ (aq) + 2C2O4^2- (aq) + Ba^2+ (aq) + 2OH- (aq) ⟶ 2K+ (aq) + 2OH- (aq) + Ba^2+ (aq) + 2C2O4^2- (aq)

Net ionic equation:

Ba^2+ (aq) + 2OH- (aq) ⟶ Ba^2+ (aq) + 2C2O4^2- (aq)

(b) Pb(NO3)2(aq) + H2SO4(aq) ⟶ PbSO4(s) + 2HNO3(aq)

Complete ionic equation:

Pb^2+ (aq) + 2NO3- (aq) + 2H+ (aq) + SO4^2- (aq) ⟶ PbSO4(s) + 2H+ (aq) + 2NO3- (aq)

Net ionic equation:

Pb^2+ (aq) + SO4^2- (aq) ⟶ PbSO4(s)

(c) CaCO3(s) + H2SO4(aq) ⟶ CaSO4(s) + CO2(g) + H2O(l)

Complete ionic equation:

Ca^2+ (aq) + CO3^2- (aq) + 2H+ (aq) + SO4^2- (aq) ⟶ Ca^2+ (aq) + SO4^2- (aq) + CO2(g) + 2H+ (aq) + 2H2O(l)

Net ionic equation:

CO3^2- (aq) + 2H+ (aq) + SO4^2- (aq) ⟶ CO2(g) + 2H+ (aq) + 2H2O(l)
