In what stages of development of the organisms above show similarities?

Best similarities appear in  stage 1 and stage 2.

The greatest similarity is found in the middle of embryonic development,  during the "phylotypic stage" species-specific variations predominate before and after this stage.

In embryonic development, STAGE 1 shows similarities as the structure of the  various animals, such as cats, birds, fish, lizards and humans, appeared the same.

In the first and second stages of development, species have typical patterns of development that display  similarity, but in stage 3 some structure that varies, such as their forelimbs, eyes, and body structure.

24. Questions;1. In what stage(s) of development do the organisms show similarities?2. What structures are similar in Stage 1 of embryonic development of theorganisms?3. In stage 3, identify the structures that make the organisms different from eachother?4. Explain how the embryonic development of organisms could be used asevidence to support evolution.​


Answer:

1. phylotypic stage

2. pharyngeal slits

Explanation:

1. The greatest similarity arises in the middle of embryonic development, during the "phylotypic stage"; species-specific differences predominate before and after this stage

2. Most of these similarities occur in the earliest stages of embryonic development. One similarity is pharyngeal slits, which develop into breathing structures, like the pharynx in humans and the gills in fish. Another is the notochord, a rod made of cartilage that supports the embryo's body.

3. An organism when viewed in an ecosystem is a community that develops together with the physical environment as a system. Organisms will always adapt to the physical environment, otherwise organisms also affect the physical environment for the purposes of life.

On the other hand, each organism may show similarities, for example certain plant or animal organisms such as the tiger will show similarities. But in a later stage two tigers will show differences even with the same sex. The difference is, for example, from the shape of the front / back legs, the size, the shape of the eyes, and even the running speed for each tiger

4.  Evidence of an evolutionary common ancestor is seen in the similarity of embryos in markedly different species. Darwin used the science of embryology to support his conclusions. Embryos and the development of embryos of various species within a class are similar even if their adult forms look nothing alike
