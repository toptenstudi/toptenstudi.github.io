Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

In a right triangle, side "c" will always be the hypotenuse and is always opposite the right angle.

Side A will always be opposite Angle A.

To find Side A, we should use one of the trigonometric functions, and we will use cosine since cos θ = [tex]\frac{opposite}{hypotenuse}[/tex]. (θ is the angle that we will use)

Plugging in the information that we know:

[tex]cos (40) = \frac{a}{39}[/tex] (We are looking for side "a")

[tex]39 cos(40) = a[/tex] (multiply both sides by 39 to isolate "a")

[tex]29.88 = a[/tex] (rounded off two decimal places)

8. the winds blow toward 40degree with avelocity of 50km/h.what is the vertical component of the winds velocity


Answer:

The vertical component of the wind's velocity is 32.14 km/h

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve only for the vertical component of the wind's velocity. To do this, we get the product of the sine of the given angle and the velocity of the wind.

For the formula, we use:

Vy = V sinθ

where

Vy     is the vertical velocity of the wind, unit is in km/h

V       is the velocity of the wind

θ       is the given angle

For the given information

θ = 40°

V = 50 km/h

Vy = ?

Solving the problem

Let us use the given formula to solve for the vertical component of the wind, we have:

Vy = V sinθ

Then substitute the given information, we have:

Vy = (50 km/h) sin 40°

Vy = 32.14 km/h

Therefore, the vertical component of the wind is 32.14 km/h

13. A ray of light is reflected from a plane mirror. The angle of incidence is 20degrees. The angle between the incident and the reflected ray is _______ a. 10degrees b. 30degrees c. 20degrees d. 40degrees


Explanation:

Since angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection, we can also say that angle between the incident ray and reflected ray is twice the angle of reflection or incidence.


Thus, as per above case the angle between the incident and the reflected ray is

= (20*2) 

= 40 degrees.

Therefore, d. 40 degrees is the angle between the incident and the reflected ray.
