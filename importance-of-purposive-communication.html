20. What is Purposive Communication? What is its importance​


Purposive communication is a type of communication that is intentionally designed to achieve specific goals or purposes. It involves identifying the intended audience, determining the message to be communicated, and selecting the appropriate channels and strategies to convey the message effectively.

The importance of purposive communication lies in its ability to help individuals and organizations achieve their goals and objectives. By using purposive communication, individuals can effectively persuade and influence others, convey information and ideas clearly and concisely, and build strong relationships and connections with their audience.

Purposive communication is essential in various settings, including business, politics, education, and personal relationships. In business, effective communication is critical to success, and purposive communication can help individuals and organizations to communicate their vision, goals, and values effectively to stakeholders, customers, and employees.

In politics, purposive communication is essential for politicians to communicate their policies and platforms to voters and to persuade them to support their candidacy. In education, purposive communication is used to help students learn and understand concepts effectively by using various instructional strategies and techniques.

Answer:

Purposive communication is a form of communication used to write, speak, and present to different audiences for various purposes. This aims for students to hone their communication effectively and efficiently to a variety of audiences either local or international to deliver the intended message.
