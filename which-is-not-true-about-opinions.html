2. which is true about fact and opinion​


Facts

What are facts? Facts are statements that are true and can be verified objectively or proven. In other words, a fact is true and correct no matter what. Here are some examples of facts:

- The sun is a star.

- Humans are mammals.

- Living things are made up of one or more cells.

Opinions

Opinions are statements that reflect the views or ideas that people have about subjects and topics. For example, your friend says that all ice cream is tasty. This is their opinion, because not everyone may think the same way, nor can it be proven to be true. Opinions may not be rooted in fact or be supported by strong evidence, though there are exceptions, such as in the case of expert opinions.

Sometimes, the language used in opinions can be deliberately emotional to mislead others. Therefore we need to know the purpose of the message or information. Is it to inform or persuade you to do something or buy a product.

9. Which is not true about opinions? Select one: a. Opinions are based on beliefs and feelings. b. They are statements of actuality and experience. c. They do not have to be based on logical reasoning


The answer is B. Options A and C are both true about opinions, which are statements of beliefs or feelings and are not necessarily based on logic. In contrast, option B refers to a fact, a statement of actuality or experience that is verifiable. This is why option B is NOT true about opinions.

Fact vs. Opinion

A lot can be said about the differences between these two words. However, they can also be connected. Let’s take a look at the definitions of fact and opinion:

Fact A statement of actuality or occurrence A statement that is known or proved to be true A statement that is based on evidence, observation, or actual experience A verifiable statement

Examples of Fact The Second World War ended in 1945.Dogs have fur.The capital of Brazil is Brasilia. The sun rises in the east.

Opinions A statement of belief or feeling about a subject A statement that can be changed anytime A judgment that can be based on facts (Note: different people draw different opinions on the same fact)

Examples of Opinions Italian food tastes better than French food. The legal age for driving should be lowered to 15. Manila is the best city in the world. Dogs are man’s best friend.

Be careful not to consider opinions as facts and vice-versa.
