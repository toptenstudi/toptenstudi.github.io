22. in this age, the invention of the transistor ushered in the electronic age.a.pre-industrial ageb.industrial agec.electronic aged.information age​


In this age, the invention of the transistor ushered in the electronic age.

c. electronic age

Explanation:

A transistor is a semiconductor device used in electronic devices such as computers to amplify, control and generate electrical signals. The word transistor comes from the word trans for transmitter or transmitter and sistor for resistor or resistor. Before using transistors, the world's first computer called ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator And Computer) required 18,000 vacuum tubes weighing 30 tons which were used to regulate the electric current so that a computer could work to perform its functions. To store that many vacuum tubes, a room of 167m2 is required. Not only does it require a large room, but for just one computer unit it requires a very large amount of electricity (174 kilowatts) and the replacement of many damaged vacuum tubes every day.

The invention of this transistor is very important in the modernization of the electronics industry and supports the development of small electronic devices such as laptops to smartphones. The transistor was first discovered in 1947 after the start of the project in 1945 at the largest industrial laboratory at that time called Bell Laboratories. The laboratory is owned by a giant telephone company called American Telephone and Telegraph (AT&T).

The inventors of the transistor are John Barden, William Shockley, and Walter Brattain. After experiencing several failed experiments, they found a bright spot when the collaboration of Walter Brattain and John Barden succeeded in producing the first transistor called the point-contact transistor on December 16, 1947. This transistor is made of a combination of gold foil and germanium plates which is capable of producing a higher electric current. bigger than received.

28. DescribeNeolithicmetal agethe copper agethe bronze agethe iron age​


Neolithic – Neolithic, also called New Stone Age, final stage of cultural evolution or technological development among prehistoric humans. ... The Neolithic followed the Paleolithic Period, or age of chipped-stone tools, and preceded the Bronze Age, or early period of metal tools.

Metal age – The Metal Ages began about 6,000 years ago. During the Metal Ages people started using metals, like copper, tin, bronze and. iron, to make tools and weapons. They heated and shaped the metals in hot furnaces. People often used precious metals, like gold and silver, to make jewellery.

The copper age – The Chalcolithic period, or Copper Age, was an era of transition between the stone tool-using farmers of the Neolithic and the metal-obsessed civilizations of the Bronze Age. The Copper Age was really a phenomenon of the eastern Mediterranean regions, and occurred from roughly 3500 to 2300 BCE.

The bronze age – The Bronze Age spanned from 3,300 to 1,200 BCE and is characterized by the use of copper and its alloy bronze as the chief hard materials in the manufacturing of implements and weapons. This period ended with further advancements in metallurgy , such as the ability to smelt iron ore.

The iron age – The Iron Age was a period in human history that started between 1200 B.C. and 600 B.C., depending on the region, and followed the Stone Age and Bronze Age. During the Iron Age, people across much of Europe, Asia and parts of Africa began making tools and weapons from iron and steel.

Answer:

METAL AGE : THE PERIOD OF PREHISTORY WHEN WEAPONS AND TOOLS WERE COMMONLY MADE OF COPPER. BRONZE AND IRON.

THE COPPER AGE : THE CHALCOLITHIC OR COPPER AGE IS THE TRANSITIONAL PERIOD BETWEEN THE NEOLITHIC AND BRONZE AGE. IT IS TAKEN TO BEGIN AROUND THE MID 5TH MILLENNIUM BC. AND ENDS WITH THE BEGINNING OF THE AGE BRONZE PROPER. IN THE LATE 4TH TO 3RD MILLENNIUM BC. DEPENDING ON THE REGION.

THE BRONZE AGE : IS A PREHISTORIC PERIOD THAT WAS CHARACTERIZED BY THE USE OF BRONZE. IN SOME AREAS PROTO WRITING. AND OTHER EARLY FEAUTURES OF URBAN CIVILIZATION. THE BRONZE AGE IS THE SECOND PRINCIPAL PERIOD OF THE THREE AGE. STONE. BRONZE. IRON SYSTEM. AS PROPOSED IN MODERN TIMES BY CHRISTIAN JÜRGENSEN THOMSEN. FOR CLASSIFYING AND STUDYING ANCIENT SOCITIES.

THE IRON AGE : THE FINAL EPOCH OF THE THREE AGE DIVISION OF THE PREHISTORY AND PROTOHISTORY OF HUMANITY. IT WAS PRECEDED BY THE BRONZE AGE AND THE STONE AGE (PALEOLITHIC. MESOLITHIC. NEOLITHIC.
