17. Values of giving; Respect Justice Social Justice Kindness Friendship Love Loyalty Define each and example each


Answer:

Values of Giving:

Respect - Respect is the act of showing consideration and honor towards others, regardless of their age, gender, race, or religion. An example of respect is listening attentively to someone who is speaking, even if you don't agree with their opinions.

Justice - Justice is the quality of being fair and impartial, ensuring that everyone is treated equally under the law. An example of justice is punishing someone who has committed a crime, regardless of their social status or connections.

Social Justice - Social justice is the concept of promoting fairness, equity, and equality for all members of society, particularly those who are marginalized or disadvantaged. An example of social justice is fighting for the rights of people who have been discriminated against because of their race, gender, or sexual orientation.

Kindness - Kindness is the act of showing compassion, empathy, and goodwill towards others. An example of kindness is helping a stranger carry their groceries, or comforting a friend who is going through a difficult time.

Friendship - Friendship is the relationship between people who share common interests, values, and experiences. An example of friendship is spending time with someone you enjoy being around, sharing your thoughts and feelings with them, and supporting each other through good times and bad.

Love - Love is the intense feeling of affection and care towards someone or something. An example of love is the love between parents and their children, or between romantic partners who share a deep emotional connection.

Loyalty - Loyalty is the act of remaining committed and faithful to someone or something, even in difficult times. An example of loyalty is standing by a friend or family member who has made a mistake, and supporting them as they try to make things right.

23. explain Intersubjectivity by using love and justice​


Answer:

Intersubjectivity refers to the way that individuals understand and relate to each other through their shared subjective experiences and perspectives. It involves a recognition that other people have their own thoughts, feelings, and experiences, and that these can be different from one's own.

In the context of love and justice, intersubjectivity can play a role in how we understand and approach these concepts. For example, in a loving relationship, both parties may need to understand and recognize the other's feelings and perspectives in order to build a strong and healthy connection. Similarly, in a justice system, judges and jurors may need to consider the subjective experiences and perspectives of the people involved in order to make fair and impartial decisions.

Overall, intersubjectivity is an important aspect of human relationships and can help us to understand and empathize with others, leading to greater understanding, compassion, and cooperation.

Answer/Explanation:

Love and justice can exist in society today, but achieving these values can be a challenge due to the complexities and flaws of human nature and the systems that govern our community. Love and justice require effort, compassion, and a willingness to confront and address injustice and inequality.

The wisdom of the prophet Amos can help guide us in the pursuit of love and justice. Amos was a prophet in the Hebrew Bible who spoke out against social injustice, corruption, and oppression in ancient Israel. His message was of righteousness and justice, and he called on the people to repent of their wrongdoing and seek a closer relationship with God.

Some key themes from the book of Amos that can help us live out the values of love and justice include:

Social justice: Amos spoke out against the exploitation and oppression of the poor and marginalized and called for fair treatment and equality for all people.

Righteousness: Amos emphasized the importance of living a righteous and moral life and called on the people to turn away from their sins and seek reconciliation with God.

Mercy and compassion: Amos called on the people to show mercy and compassion to those in need and to act with kindness and compassion towards others.

Repentance: Amos called on the people to confess their sins, seek forgiveness, turn away from their wrongdoing and seek a closer relationship with God.

By following the wisdom of Amos and seeking to embody these values in our lives, we can work towards creating a more loving and just society.

29. Why is love and justice important in scripture? ​


Answer:

Love and justice are two of the most important themes in scripture because they are essential aspects of God's character and his relationship with humanity.

Love is often described in scripture as the defining characteristic of God (1 John 4:8), and is demonstrated through his actions towards humanity, including the sending of his son, Jesus Christ, to die for the sins of the world (John 3:16). Scripture also teaches that love is the foundation of all human relationships and that we are called to love others as ourselves (Matthew 22:39).

Justice is also a central theme in scripture, and is closely linked to love. God is described as a God of justice (Isaiah 30:18) who cares for the oppressed and marginalized (Psalm 103:6). Throughout scripture, God commands his people to seek justice and to care for the poor and vulnerable (Isaiah 1:17, James 1:27). This emphasis on justice is also reflected in the teachings of Jesus, who consistently spoke out against injustice and oppression.

Overall, love and justice are important themes in scripture because they reflect God's character and his relationship with humanity. They provide a framework for how we should relate to one another and the world around us, and inspire us to seek a better world through acts of compassion and justice.
