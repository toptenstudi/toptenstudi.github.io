❑In this discussion, we will discuss Kinetic Energy and the lessons to be learned from it.

What is kinetic energy?

Kinetic energy is a energy on an object due to motion. The kinetic energy examples is given as follows:

Running WalkingMoving carCycling Windmills Flying airplane

How can we calculate kinetic energy?

To calculate kinetic energy, we need to follow the formula above:

[tex]\huge \quad \: KE= \frac{1}{2} mv ^{2} [/tex]

m = mass (kg)

v = velocity (m/s)

KE = Kinetic energy (J)
