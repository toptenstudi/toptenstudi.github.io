12. tell whether the following plants are aerial, terrestrial or acquatic 1. water lily 2. orchids 3. santan 4. gumamela 5. lotus​


Answer:

1. water lily -Acquatic2. orchids- Aerial 3. santan- Terrestrial 4. gumamela- Terrestrial 5. lotus-acquatic

Explanation:

Aerial plants

 are plants that do not have underground root systems; instead, they are located in areas above the ground.

Terrestrial 

refers to things related to land or the planet Earth. Terrestrial may also refer to: Terrestrial animal, an animal that lives on land opposed to living in water, or sometimes an animal that lives on or near the ground, as opposed to arboreal life (in trees)

Aquatic plants 

are plants that have adapted to living in aquatic environments (saltwater or freshwater). They are also referred to as hydrophytes or macrophytes to distinguish them from algae and other microphytes. ...Aquatic plants require special adaptations for living submerged in water, or at the water's surface.

Answer:

Plastic pollution affects marine and aquatic lives in numerous ways.

First, plastic pollution damages the marine ecosystems by causing a decline in the amount of sunlight that reaches the deeper parts of the ocean. This reduced amount of light causes aquatic plants to dwindle in number. Algae and other marine plants which is the primary source of food for most of the aquatic organisms become scarce due to plastic pollution. As a consequence, the reproduction and survival rate of these organisms is reduced to the extent that some of them are now critically endangered or even driven to extinction.

Second, there is the presence of microplastics which gets ingested by organims causing them adverse health effects including poisoning and cancer.

Lastly, plastic waste build-ups in the breeding grounds of organisms causes a decline in the susceptibility of these locations for nesting. The reduced amount of oxygen and sunlight in these areas may cause eggs and fingerlings not to fully develop.
