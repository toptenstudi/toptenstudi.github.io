12. angler4. A 40-m electric wire is to be cut into two segments whose ratio is 3:5. What arethe lengths of the two cut electric wires?​


Answer:

15 m and 25 m

#NO TO COPY PASTE SOLUTION!

2 Original Solutions Provided:

Algebraic Solution:

Ratio = 3:5

Wire1 = 3x

Wire2 = 5x

Total Wire = 40m

so we can say,

3x + 5x = 40

8x = 40

x = 40/8

x = 5

solve for the lengths of the 2 wires,

wire1 = 3x = 3(5) = 15 m

wire2 = 5x = 5(5) = 25 m

Logic Solution:

Ratios are simply fractions showing part of a whole.

So for 3:5 ratio, we can say that the 2 ratio in fraction form is,

3/8 and 5/8, since 8 is the whole of 3 and 5.

Now we simply multiply the fractions to the total Length of wire of 40 m.

Wire1 = 3/8 (40) = 15 m

Wire2 = 5/8 (40) = 25 m

Therefore the lengths of the two cut electric wires are 15 m and 25 m respectively.

Answer:

B. 100°

Since the supplement of an angle is the angle that, when added to the given angle, forms a straight line (180°), we can solve for the measure of the angle by subtracting the given supplement from 180°:

180° - 79° = 101°

Therefore, the measure of the angle is 101°.

A. Angler and angle h have a sum that is equal to 90°.

Since angle r and angle h are supplementary, their sum is equal to 180°. Therefore, statement B is true.

Vertical angles are opposite angles that are formed by the intersection of two lines. Since angles r and h are supplementary and not opposite angles, statement C is false.

Statement A is also false since if angle r and angle h are supplementary, their sum is equal to 180°, not 90°. Therefore, the correct answer is A.

Angle 1 and angle 2 are complementary angles.

Complementary angles are two angles whose sum is equal to 90°. Since angle 1 and angle 2 form a right angle when combined, their sum is equal to 90°, and thus they are complementary angles. Therefore, the correct answer is that angle 1 and angle 2 are complementary angles.
