Tala

Tala (Sanskrit for "clap") is a metric cycle with a specified number of beats—from 3 to 128—that repeat in the same manner throughout a musical performance in India, Bangladesh, and Pakistan.

Goblet

Goblet percussion (plural goblet drums) (music) A goblet-shaped drum (membranophone) with a single head. (music) A darbuka is a goblet-shaped drum from the Middle East and North Africa.

Oud

The oud is a stringed musical instrument popular in medieval and modern Islamic music. It's the ancestor of the European lute. The oud is distinguished by its deep pear-shaped body, fretless fingerboard, shorter neck, and less severely bent-back pegbox than the European lute.

Hora

The word comes from Rumanian. It was an early term for a round folk dance. Later, it came to represent a specific dance as well. In Rumanian and Moldavian folklore, the phrase also refers to two types of dancing melodies: sluggish with a triple meter and quick with a duple meter.

Strings, woodwinds, percussion instruments, and vocalists are commonly used in West Asian music. These characteristics all work together to create an intricate and dramatic musical style that builds tension and leads to huge musical climaxes.
