Answer:

To calculate the soil texture using a soil texture triangle, you need to plot the percentage of sand, silt, and clay on the triangle and then determine which texture the point falls within. For example, for the combination 25% sand, 30% silt, and 45% clay, you would plot the point on the triangle at the 25% sand, 30% silt location, and then draw a line to the 45% clay location. The point at which this line intersects with one of the texture boundaries on the triangle will determine the soil texture.

For the combination of 25% sand, 30% silt, and 45% clay, the point would fall within the clay texture area.

For the combination of 40% sand, 30% silt, and 30% clay, the point would fall within the loam texture area.

For the combination of 60% sand, 10% silt, and 30% clay, the point would fall within the sandy loam texture area.

For the combination of 70% sand, 12% silt, and 18% clay, the point would fall within the loamy sand texture area.

For the combination of 90% sand, 5% silt, and 5% clay, the point would fall within the sandy texture area.

For the combination of 80% sand, 15% silt, and 5% clay, the point would fall within the sandy loam texture area.

For the combination of 10% sand, 85% silt, and 5% clay, the point would fall within the silt texture area.

24. briefly explain how you will heat a sample with these materials: wire gauze, bunsen burner, crucible, iron stand, iron ring and clay triangle​ pls maayos na sagot naman po.


Answer:

Hi. The best way to prepare a laboratory set-up is to know each of the equipment's purpose or use. Let me explain the function of each laboratory equipment to better your understanding for your future encounters in the laboratory.

1. Wire gauze

The wire gauze is a metal mesh with a circular white coating of ceramic in the middle. Laboratory glasswares are not built to have direct contact on an open fire as it may cause breakage. Furthermore,  direct fire on glasswares limits the fire and heat in one location which is not ideal when performing experiments. A wire gauze solves these problems by serving as a heat distributor and regulator of heat.

2. Bunsen burner

Some of the laboratory experiments require the heating of samples. A bunsen burner is a heat source for small-scale experiments that require an open and direct heat source. Depending on the type of unit, the intensity of the fire is adjustable. The source of gas is usually either butane or liquified petroleum gas.

3. Crucible

Crucibles are ceramic bowls with very high heat resistance. Due to this characteristic, this is often used as a heating container for a direct open heat source for intentions of heating high melting point samples or metals, evaporation of specific solutions among others.

4. Iron Stand

An iron stand has two distinguishable parts. The iron platform on its base provides support to the stand and serves as a platform heating source such as a bunsen burner or an alcohol lamp, glassware such as beakers and flasks. A circular metal iron sticks out from this platform and this serves as a stand for iron rings and clamps to clamp on.

5. Iron ring

An iron ring is a metal ring with a rod sticking out of the ring similar to the shape of a magnifying glass. At the end of the rod is a clamp that is used to adjust and clamp the iron ring onto the iron stand at the desired height. An iron ring is used as a platform for glassware which needs to stand at an elevation from the laboratory table such as in the case when heating a sample in a beaker on an open fire.  

6. Clay Triangle

Due to the size and shape of a regular crucible, a clay triangle aids in providing support. A crucible could be placed on a clay triangle on an iron ring when heated with an open flame.
