A peripheral device is any hardware device that is not a part of the computer’s central processing unit (CPU) or motherboard. It does not contribute to the primary function of a computer system but instead help users access and use the additional functionalities of that device associating with the computer.

Peripheral devices are connected to the motherboard through various expansion slots or via serial ports on the back or front of the chassis. Since they are not essential to the function of the computer, it can still operate without them.

5. if a pc does not possess a graphics card adapter how would that impact the computer?​


Answer:

A graphics card, also known as a video card or GPU, is a dedicated piece of hardware that is responsible for rendering and displaying images on a computer's monitor. It is responsible for performing complex calculations to create the images that we see on the screen.

Without a graphics card, a computer would rely on its CPU (central processing unit) to handle the rendering and display of images. While the CPU is capable of handling these tasks, it is not designed specifically for this purpose and may not be able to perform them as efficiently or effectively as a dedicated graphics card. This can result in lower performance, lower quality images, and slower load times for graphics-intensive applications such as video games and video editing software.

Additionally, a computer without a graphics card may not be able to support multiple monitors or high-resolution displays. It may also be limited in its ability to run certain graphics-intensive applications or games.

In summary, a computer can work without a graphics card, but it may not perform as well in terms of rendering and displaying images, and may be limited in its ability to run certain graphics-intensive applications or support multiple monitors or high-resolution displays.

16. What do you think is the advantage of using removable adapter cards instead of using built-in adapter cards?


AnswerWhat do you think is the advantage of using removable adapter cards instead of using built-in adapter cards?

Removable Adapter Cards are individual parts. Therefore, when an adapter card is broken and needs replacements, it doesn't affect other computer parts. Particularly the Motherboard. In other words, a Removable Adapter Card is easier to replace, making it a practical choice for gaming.

Why Removable Adapter Cards are more practical

When a build-in Adapter Card is broken, it requires the Motherboard to be replaced as well. This is why it is cheaper to repair desktop computers than old laptop versions. However, the latest version of gaming laptops has separated cards from the motherboard.
