9. The length of the sides of a triangle are in the ratio 4 : 7 : 9.( a ) If the length of the longest side is 45cm., find the length of the shortest side.( b ) If the total length of all the sides is 200 cm., find the length of the longest side.( c ) If the difference between the length of the longest side and the length of the shortest side is 35cm, find the length of the shortest side.​


Answer:

a. 20cm
b. 90cm
c. 28cm

Step-by-step explanation:

a. 4:7:9
4/n = 9/45, where n is the shortest side

Then cross multiply 4 and 45 then divide the product by 9
4 x 45 = 180
180 / 9 = 20

20 is the shortest side if the longest side is 45cm with a ratio of 4:7:9

b. If the total length of all sides is 200cm and the ratio is still 4:7:9 then,

4n+7n+9n = 200
20n = 200
n = 10, then we multiply n to the ratios
n x 4: n x 7: n x 9, where the answer in n x 9 is the longest side

n x 9 =?
10 x 9 = 90

90cm is the length of the longest side if all sides sum up to 200cm with a ratio of 4:7:9

c. If 9n - 4n = 35, then

9n - 4n = 35 (subtract 4n from 9n)
5n = 35 (then divide both sides by 5 to cancel out 5n)
n = 7

then we just multiply the n to their ratios 4:7:9

4 x 7 = 28
7 x 7 = 35
9 x 7 = 63

The longest side is the 4 x 7, which is 28, therefore 28 is the answer.
To confirm the answer is right, their difference should be 35 as per the question so we need to subtract the shortest side to the longest side and it must result to 35.

63 - 28 = 35, therefore the answer is correct.

16. which of the following primitive units has the longest length?a. palm b. Hand c. cubit d. digit​


Answer:

C. Cubit

Step-by-step explanation:

Cubit, unit of linear measure used by many ancient and medieval peoples. It may have originated in Egypt about 3000 BC; it thereafter became ubiquitous in the ancient world. The cubit, generally taken as equal to 18 inches (457 mm), was based on the length of the arm from the elbow to the tip of the middle finger and was considered the equivalent of 6 palms or 2 spans. In some ancient cultures it was as long as 21 inches (531 mm).

WINTER SOLTICE

Which of the following will be experienced during winter solstice.

A. Longest days and shortest nights

B. Longest nights and shortest days

C. Equal lengths of days and nights

D. Colder days and hotter nights

ANSWER

Amongst the given choices above the correct answer is letter B. Longest nights and shortest days.

Winter usually begins during winter solstice (one of the two times of the year when the sun is farthest north or south of the equator takes place) this is around December in the Northern Hemisphere.

Our hemisphere is tilted away from the Sun, so the sun's rays reach us less directly. While days get a bit longer during winter, they are still shorter compared in spring and summer, that's why it's cold.
