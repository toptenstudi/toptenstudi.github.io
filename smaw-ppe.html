12. if you don't use a leggings what can happen to you?(PPE/SMAW)​


Answer:

It is important to wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) when engaging in welding using the Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW) process. Leggings, also known as leg guards or spats, are one of the PPE recommended for welders to wear. Leggings are designed to protect the legs and feet from sparks, spatter, and heat generated during the welding process.

If a welder does not wear leggings while performing SMAW welding, they may be at risk of sustaining burns, cuts, or other injuries to their legs and feet. Welding sparks and spatter can cause serious burns if they come into contact with unprotected skin. Additionally, hot metal particles can get trapped inside the shoes or socks, causing further damage or injury.

Therefore, it is highly recommended that welders wear appropriate PPE including leggings to ensure their safety while performing SMAW welding.

15. broad topic for smaw​


Answer:

Shielded metal arc welding (SMAW) process is one of the most commonly employed material joining processes utilized in the various industrial sectors such as marine, ship-building, automotive, aerospace, construction and petrochemicals etc.

The purpose of SMAW (also known as stick welding) is to perform arc welding using a covered metal electrode to shield the weld. Welding is one of the most popular ways to combine two metal parts into one. The process is simple: apply heat to each metal component.

Since the flux-covered electrode generates its own protective gas, no additional exterior shielding gas is needed, so SMAW can be done outdoors. Versatile: SMAW can be used on a variety of different alloys and metals, as well as done in numerous positions.

16. if you don't use a welding spats what can happen to you?(PPE/SMAW)​


Answer:

If you do not use welding spats while welding, you are at risk of getting burned by hot sparks and slag which can cause serious injuries to your feet and legs. Welding spats are a type of personal protective equipment (PPE) that are designed to protect your feet and legs from these hazards.

Welding generates extremely high temperatures that can produce sparks and molten metal which can fly off and land on your feet or legs. In addition, welding produces slag which is a byproduct of the welding process and can also fly off and land on your feet or legs.

When you wear welding spats, they cover your shoes and lower legs which can prevent sparks, molten metal, and slag from burning your skin. The spats are made of flame-resistant materials that can withstand the high temperatures associated with welding.

Overall, wearing welding spats is essential for protecting yourself from the hazards associated with welding. It is important to wear the appropriate PPE for the job to ensure your safety.
