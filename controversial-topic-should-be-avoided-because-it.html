6. Instruction: Read each statement carefully. Place a T on the line if you think the statement is TRUE. Place an F on the line if you think the statement is FALSE. (2 pts. each) ________ 1. Cooperation is the absence of conflict. ________ 2. Debating controversial issues should not be encouraged because it is competitive. ________ 3. The competing (power-forcing) conflict management style should always be avoided. ________ 4. Accommodating is often the conflict management style of less powerful. ________ 5. Avoiding is always an ineffective conflict management style because it ignores the conflict instead of confronting. ​


Answer:

1. FALSE: Cooperation is a process in which individuals or groups work together in order to achieve a common goal or solve a problem. It is not the absence of conflict, as conflict can often arise when people are working together.

2. FALSE: Debating controversial issues can be an important and productive way to explore different perspectives and ideas, as long as it is done in a respectful and constructive manner. While it can be competitive, it does not have to be, and can be a valuable tool for understanding and finding solutions to complex issues.

3. TRUE: The competing (power-forcing) conflict management style involves trying to assert one's own interests or needs over those of others, and can often result in a win-lose outcome. While it may be effective in some situations, it is generally not recommended as a long-term strategy, as it can lead to damaged relationships and negative outcomes.

4. TRUE: The accommodating conflict management style involves prioritizing the needs or interests of others over one's own. It is often adopted by individuals who have less power or influence in a situation, as they may feel that they have little choice but to go along with the wishes of others.

5. FALSE: While avoiding can be an ineffective conflict management style in some situations, it is not always the case. In some circumstances, it may be necessary or appropriate to delay addressing a conflict until a more appropriate time or to seek mediation or other forms of outside assistance. It is important to consider the specific context and the needs of all parties involved when deciding on the most appropriate conflict management strategy.
