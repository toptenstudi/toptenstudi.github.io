23. Write a 5 sentence paragraph with affixed words and blended words about the changes that happen in materials under the presence or lack of oxygen.​


Answer:

Simply put: oxygen (O₂) is a very reactive guy and, sooner or later, jumps on everybody.

Perhaps at room temperature you can still slow it down, but an oxygen-driven Redox reaction is one of the commonest chemical processes you can encounter wherever you go. In a nutshell, oxygen is very fond of picking up electrons from many other chemical species and thus convert itself into an oxide anion (O²¯), which is for example found in many Oxide compounds. This, along with more specific reaction conditions, is the important part of many chemical pathways O₂ is involved in:

O₂ + 4e¯ → 2O²¯

The two electrons come from the material/molecule/compound being oxidised (4e¯ stands for “4 electrons” picked up by one oxygen molecule). By doing so, O₂ is said to become reduced to, indeed, an oxide anion.

Think about, for example, Rust formation, Cellular respiration, rock formation, etc. They are all oxygen-based processes. Perhaps the equation above is an oversimplification of what actually happens, but is fairly accurate on a descriptive level nonetheless.


24. Learning Task No. 1: Presence and Absence of OxygenIn investigating the changes that happen in materials under the condition that there is presence or absence of oxygen, you will need the following materials : ​


The following is a list of what we'll need:

- 1 piece of apple 

- any vitamin C tablet

- 3 clear glasses/ bottles

- 500 ml, or around 2 glasses, of water

- cutting board

- knife

Steps:

- Keep the experimental area neat, secure, and easy to handle by performing the test in the kitchen or any other suitable location. Do as I say.

- Firstly, get your supplies in order.

- Mark or write the contents of each glass. Put a glass of water (labelled "Glass A") and a glass of vitamin C (labelled "Glass B". This is the way to do it if you want to keep an eye on which glasses change color when exposed to oxygen.

Analyze the result:

- Glass A:

The oxidation process causes the apple to turn brown when it is chopped and exposed to oxygen (When an apple is cut open, oxygen is pumped into the wound, which prompts enzymes to quickly oxidize phenolic chemicals already present in the apple tissues to create melanin, a brown pigment). The first apple glass submerged in water has this state. Therefore, the apple's oxidation rate is moderately slowed by being submerged in water. That's why you need that glass of ordinary water with the apple as a control. Otherwise, the evaluation would be skewed.

- Glass B:

The oxidation rate of the apple in glass B, which contains the vitamin C tablet (antioxidants), is much lower than in glass A. This is due to vitamin C's antioxidant properties, which cause it to combine with highly reactive oxygen-centred radicals to produce less reactive species, alleviating the condition. As a result, the apple's flesh suffers less from oxidation. For this reason, the apple in the vitamin C solution won't oxidize and will keep its bright green colour. Results suggest that the vitamin C tablets are likely free of 'heavy metal' and 'crude oil' residues, despite also containing fillers and sweeteners.

However, we are known as we have shown that vitamin C tablets are the most effective at decreasing oxidative damage and providing the healthiest-looking apple.
