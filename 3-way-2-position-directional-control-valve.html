4. Blood coming from all parts of the body is received by the (1)___ of the heart by the way of the blood vessel called (2)___. Contraction of the right atrium sends blood to the (3)___. At this point, the (4)___ valves are open. Contraction of the right ventricle forces blood to the (5)___ for oxygenation by the way of the (6)___. At this point, the (7___ valves are open, while the (8)___ valves are closed to prevent the back flow of blood in the right atrium


Answer:

Blood coming from all parts of the body is received by the (1)right atrium of the heart by the way of the blood vessel called (2)superior and inferior  vena cava. Contraction of the right atrium sends blood to the (3)ventricle. At this point, the (4)tricuspid valves are open. Contraction of the right ventricle forces blood to the (5)Lungs for oxygenation by the way of the (6)pulmonary artery. At this point, the (7. pulmonary valves are open, while the (8)tricuspid valves are closed to prevent the back flow of blood in the right atrium.

The pulmonary arteries deliver deoxygenated blood to the lungs where blood is oxygenated and cleansed from carbon dioxide content. This part of the circulation is called the pulmonary circulation. Air exchange happens through alveolar cells in the alveolar sacs of the lungs and the endothelial cells of the capillary bed. The exchange of gases is primarily due to the differences in the partial pressures of the oxygen gas extracellularly in the air sacs and within the blood, and differences in the partial pressures of the carbon dioxide gas extracellularly in the air sacs and within the blood. The flow of gas is from a greater pressure to a lower pressure, thus the flow of oxygen from the lungs into the blood and the flow of carbon dioxide from the blood into the lungs to be exhaled. After oxygenation of blood in the lungs, the oxygenated blood would then flow back into the left atrium through the pulmonary vein. It should be noted that major vasculature that brings blood towards the heart is called a vein and vasculature that brings blood away from the heart is an artery. This is why even if it carries oxygenated blood from the lungs, the pulmonary vein is called a vein because it brings blood towards the heart. The blood from the left atrium would then passively enter the left ventricle through the mitral valve and the left atrium would contract to empty the remaining volume into the left ventricle. the left ventricle (and right ventricle) would then contract and push the oxygenated blood into the aorta. The pressure from the ventricular contraction closes the mitral valve preventing backflow and opens the aortic valve to allow blood to flow into the aorta and towards the limbs, thorax, abdomen, and head where oxygen will be utilized and metabolic wastes from the cells such as carbon dioxide are collected by the blood. Deoxygenated blood would then return to the heart. This is called the systemic circulation.

25. The gain of a control valve should never be less than ___​


Answer:

0.5

Explanation:

Gain is the ratio of the percentage change in a process variable to the percentage change of valve travel, and depends on the valve characteristics and process conditions. The gain should never be less than 0.5. The ability of a valve to control flow depends upon the quality of its actuator.


26. what should be the rangeability of the control valve?A.GainB.CvC.TurndownD.Stroke%​


Answer:

Turndown

Explanation:

Rangeability of a control valve is defined as the ratio of maximum to minimum flow that can be acted upon by a control valve after receiving a signal from a controller. Today's control valve applications often require a degree of high rangeability, i.e. require a valve to control a flow from small to large flow.
