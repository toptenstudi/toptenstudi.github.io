4. The equation of motion is given by s(t) = 6t2 + t + 8 at 1:05 P.M. What is the average velocity from 1:07 P.M. to 1:09 P.M. in meters/second?


Answer:

The average speed is 2161 meters/second.

Further Explanation:

One of the ways to solve this problem is by applying the concepts in calculus.

Take note that the first derivative of the function is actually the velocity of the function and the second derivative is the acceleration.

Some Major Topics in Calculus

1. Differential Calculus

2. Integral Calculus

3. Advanced Calculus

Let's assume that the function is in the form [tex]s(t)=6t^2+t+8[/tex].

Note that the initial time or when the motion is at rest the time is 1:05 PM. The object then moves accordingly. It takes 2 minutes from the initial time to 1:07 PM and 4 minutes from the initial time to 1:09 PM.

Since the problem is asking for the average velocity in meters per second, we need to convert first the minutes which the object takes to move.

Remember the conversion factor.

              1 minute = 60 seconds

Conversion of 2 minutes to Seconds:

               [tex]2\times{60}=120[/tex]

Conversion of 4 minutes to Seconds:

                 [tex]4\times{60}=240[/tex]

Let's solve for the first derivative of the function to get the velocity of the motion.

                                      [tex]s'(t)=12t+1[/tex]

Evaluate the derivative by substitution. Substitute the values 120 and 240 into the derivative function, then simplify.

                                  [tex]\begin{aligned}s'(120)&=12(120)+1\\&=1441\:\text{m/s}\end{aligned}[/tex]

                                  [tex]\begin{aligned}s'(240)&=12(240)+1\\&=2881\:\text{m/s}\end{aligned}[/tex]

We have two exact velocity values from 1:05 PM to 1:07 PM and from 1:05 PM to 1:09 PM.

Take the sum of the two velocities and then divide by two to get the average velocity.

                                 [tex]\frac{1441+2881}{2}=\frac{4322}{2}=2161[/tex]

From the solution above, the average speed is 2161 meters/second.

13. 2.) x2 - 6x + 10 = 07, Quadratic or not Quadratic?Pa explain po Kung Not quadratic​


Answer and step-by-step explanation:

To determine if an equation is quadratic or not, we just have to look if the highest exponent is 2.

[tex] {x}^{2} - 6x + 10 = 7[/tex]

The term "x²" has an exponent of 2.

The term "-6x" has an exponent of 1.

The term "10" has an exponent of 0.

Thus, the greatest exponent is 2.

Therefore, it is quadratic.
