→ A parallelogram is a quadrilateral that:

a. A quadrilateral's diagonals are bisected by each other.

b. The opposite sides of both pairs of opposite sides are congruent.

c. Both opposite angle pairs are congruent.

d. Both pairs of sides are congruent and parallel.

e. Both successive angles are supplementary to a quadrilateral's angle.

→ Because all parallelograms have four sides, they are always quadrilaterals. While a quadrilateral isn't necessarily a parallelogram because it doesn't always have two pairs of opposite sides that are parallel.

22. GIVEN: Quadrilateral WISH is a parallelogram. If a m ∠W = x + 15 and m ∠S = 2x + 5, what is m ∠W?


Answer:

m∠W = 25°

Step-by-step explanation:

(Please click my illustration below of WISH with properties of a parallelogram.)

Opposite angles of parallelogram are congruent. They have the same measure.

Adjacent angles (sharing the same side) are supplementary.  The sum of their measure is 180 degrees.

Given that quadrilateral WISH is a parallelogram: (m here means measure)

m ∠W = x + 15m ∠S = 2x + 5∠W and ∠S are opposite angles, then they are congruent.

To solve for value of x, equate the expressions because the measurements are equal.

Equation:

m∠W = m∠S

x + 15 = 2x + 5

15 - 5 = 2x - x

10 = x

or

x = 10

Substitute 10 to x:

m∠W = x + 15 = (10) + 15 = 25°

m∠S  = 2x + 5 = 2(10) + 5 = 25°

What is m∠W? 25°

24. quadrilateral wish is a parallelogram if the measure of the angle w = x + 15 what is the measure of angle W ? illustrate and show your solution​


Answer:

We are given that quadrilateral WISH is a parallelogram. Therefore, opposite angles of this quadrilateral are congruent. Let's denote the measure of angle W as "w". Then, we know that angle S has the same measure as angle W since they are opposite angles in a parallelogram. Similarly, angle I has the same measure as angle H.

Now, we are given that the measure of angle w is x + 15. Since angle W and angle S are congruent, we know that the measure of angle S is also x + 15. Similarly, the measure of angle I is x + 15, and the measure of angle H is also x + 15.

The sum of the interior angles of a quadrilateral is always 360 degrees. Therefore, we can write an equation based on the angles in quadrilateral WISH:

w + x + 15 + x + 15 + w = 360

Simplifying this equation, we get:

2w + 2x + 30 = 360

2w + 2x = 330

w + x = 165

We are asked to find the measure of angle W, which is "w". We can solve for "w" by substituting the value of "x + 15" into the equation we just found:

w + (x + 15) = 165

w + x + 15 = 165

w + 15 = 165

w = 150

Therefore, the measure of angle W is 150 degrees.
