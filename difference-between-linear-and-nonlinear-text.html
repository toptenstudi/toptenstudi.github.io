Answer:

The key difference between linear and nonlinear text is their reading path. In a linear text, a reader can make sense of the text by reading sequentially, from beginning to the end. However, in a nonlinear text, the reading path is nonlinear and non-sequential; thus, the reader can choose his own reading path.

What is the relationship between linear and nonlinear text?

The reading route distinguishes linear and nonlinear material. A reader can make sense of a linear text by reading it from beginning to conclusion. The reading path in a nonlinear book, on the other hand, is nonlinear and non-sequential, allowing the reader to choose his reading path. The approach or route through a book is a reading path. Two options depending on the reading path: linear or nonlinear literature. This article discusses the differences between linear and nonlinear text by presenting examples. Traditional material that must be read from beginning to end is linear text. The reader deduces meaning from the text based on the grammatical and syntactic structure of the words. Furthermore, this form of writing has an order or sequence; the order of the text, or its reading path, is usually determined by the book's author. Texts printed on paper are typically thought of as linear texts. Novels, poems, short stories, letters, and educational texts are all linear works read from beginning to end. The antithesis of linear text is nonlinear text. It is nonlinear and non-sequential, as the name implies. In other words, readers do not need to read the content in sequential order to comprehend it. Because the readers decide the order of reading, not the creator of the text, this form of the book has various reading pathways. The phrase "nonlinear text" has several definitions. Most people think of nonlinear texts as texts that include images or graphs. Flowcharts, charts, and graphs (ex: pie chart, bar graphs), as well as graphical organizers like knowledge maps and story maps, are some examples.
