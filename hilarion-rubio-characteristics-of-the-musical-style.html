3. What is the music style of hilarion rubio


Hilarion Rubio y Francesco was a musician and composer of classical type of music. He started playing clarinet (his first instrument) at an early age because of his father, Armando Buencamino who taught him how to play the instrument then eventually excelled on playing clarinet that’s why he became a clarinetist in the Harmony of Orchestra in Bacoor. He became a conductor of several orchestra band and became one of the board members of the Federation of Filipino Composers.

8. What is the musical contribution pf hilarion rubio


Answer:

It is clearly that Hilarion Rubio is a singer/composer in blood and mind. You can see his interest and love into music from his early teens wherein he is laready playing some instruments and composing his original song. After the Second World War, he was appointed for a position to some organizations. One of these is the Philippine Bandmaster’s Association as the first President. He introduced the love of our nation through his compositions that include: bulaklaken, theme and variations for band, dance of the nymphs rondo, florente at laura (overture), National Heroes, Halik, Unang katas, Two part Invention (piano), Ang Konsyerto (ballet), Light, Narra, Mutya ng Silangan, Ang Magsasaka, Bukang liwayway, Concertino in C (marimba and piano), Filipinas kong Mahal, Hatulan Mo Ako, Ginintuang araw, danza, In a Tropical Sea, To the Filipino Youth, Nela.

12. point out the characteristics of the musical style of each of the abovementioned composers. a. Francisco buencamino Sr. b. Francisco Santiago c. Nicanor Abelardo d. Antonio Molina e. Hilarion Rubio f. Col. Antonio Buenaventura g. Rodolfo Cornejo h. Felipe Padilla de Leon Sr. i. Lucio San Pedro j. Rosendo Santos Jr. k. Alfredo Buenaventura l. Ryan Cayabyab


Answer:

Explanation:

The musical style of the following composers are:

a. Francisco Buencamino Sr.  

Buencamino's style are either Zarzuela or Kundiman

b. Francisco Santiago

His style is Kundiman. He is also called as the Father of Kundiman.

c. Nicanor Abelardo

Abelardo is also known for his Kundiman songs.

d. Antonio Molina

Molina used impressionism in his musical style. Impressionism gives you that atmospheric vibe and conveys feelings through music.

e. Hilarion Rubio

Hilarion Rubio y Francesco's style leaned towards the classical type of music. He also became one of the board members of the Federation of Filipino Composers during his time.

f. Col. Antonino Buenaventura

I think you meant Antonino and not Antonio. Col. Buenaventura was also into classical music then.

g. Rodolfo Cornejo

Cornejo who had over 300 compositions which ranged from classical to pop music. He even wrote music for the ballets of Ibong Adarna in 1970.

h. Felipe Padilla de Leon Sr.

De Leon Sr., one of our National Artist for music. One of his discoveries includes the establishment of a classical tradition which is based on folk musical forms like the Kundiman.  

i. Lucio San Pedro

San Pedro's musical pieces are more into the classical and lullaby pieces. Some of his works are "Sa Ugoy ng Duyan" and "In the Silence of the Night"

j. Rosendo Santos Jr.

Santos Jr., style is on contemporary music with influence from the 20th century music from the West. But for the most of it, his style is neoclassicism.

k. Alfredo Buenaventura

His styles includes operas, symphonic poems. He was also once the Dean of Music conservatory.

l. Ryan Cayabyab

Cayabyab is considered one of the living music legends in the Philippines. He is known for his eclectic, modern and even pop music. Some of his famous songs are "Kay Ganda ng Ating Musika" and "Tuwing Umuulan at Kapiling Ka".
