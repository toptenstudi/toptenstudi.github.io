1. what is the product of transition reaction​


Answer:

The transition reaction is the transition between glycolysis and the citric acid cycle. The transition reaction converts pyruvate (3 carbons) to acetyl CoA (2 carbons), producing carbon dioxide (CO2) and a NADH . The figure shows the transition reaction with CoA and NAD entering, and acetyl-CoA, CO2, and NADH being produced.

The acetyl is combined with coenzyme A (CoA) to form acetyl-CoA.

Thus, for one molecule of glucose, the transition reaction produces 2 acetyl-CoAs, 2 molecules of CO2, and 2 NADHs.

1.The term reaction rate refers to the speed at which a chemical reaction occurs, measured by the change in concentration of reactants or products per unit time.

2.The factors that can affect the rate of a chemical reaction include:

Temperature

Concentration of reactants

Surface area of reactants

Pressure (for reactions involving gases)

Presence of a catalyst

Nature of reactants and their chemical properties

3The graph is not provided, so it cannot be labeled or described.

4.False. The activation energy is the minimum energy required for a reaction to occur, and a higher activation energy means that more energy is required for a reaction to proceed. Therefore, the reactant with the higher activation energy will be slower.

5.True. Increasing the reaction temperature increases the kinetic energy of reactant molecules, leading to more frequent and energetic collisions, and thus increasing the percentage of successful collisions.

25. Titanium is a transition metal used in many alloys because it is extremely strong and lightweight. Titanium tetrachloride (TiCl4) is extracted from titanium oxide using chlorine and carbon. Determine the chemical equation involved in the reaction. If you begin with 1.25 TiO2, what mass of Cl2 gas is needed?


Answer:

TiO2 + 2Cl2 + C → TiCl4 + CO2

177g

Explanation:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

TiO2 + 2Cl2 + C → TiCl4 + CO2

Step 2: Compute for the mass of Cl2 gas needed.

mass Cl2 = 1.25 mol TiO2 × (2 mol Cl2 / 1 mol TiO2) × (70.90 g Cl2 / 1 mol Cl2)

mass Cl2 = 177 g
