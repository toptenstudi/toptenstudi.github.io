3. how to multiply binomial by binomial


Answer:

FOIL Method

Step-by-step explanation:

FOIL (First, Outer, Inner and Last)

Example

(2x+1)(3x-2)

First, multiply both first term.

2x * 3x = 6x^2

Outer, multiply the outer term, in this case that's 2x and -2 so..

2x * -2 = -4x

Inner, multiply the inner term, in this case that's 1 and 3x so...

1 * 3x = 3x

Last, multiply all last term, 1 and -2 from out example so...

1 * -2 = -2

Then add like terms

(2x^2) + 3x - 4x -2

(2x^2) - x - 2


4. example of multiply binomials​


Step-by-step explanation:

A binomial is an algebraic expression with two terms separated by either the addition sign (+) or subtraction sign (-). Examples of binomial expressions are 2x + 3, 3x – 1, 2x+5y, 6x−3y, etc. Binomial expressions are multiplied using the FOIL method. F-O-I- L is the short form of ‘first, outer, inner and last.’ The general formula of foil method is; (a + b) × (m + n) = am + an + bm + bn.

Let’s take a look at the example below.

Example 1

Multiply (x – 3) (2x – 9)

Solution

Multiply the first terms together;

= (x) * (2x) = 2x 2

Multiply the outermost terms of each binomial;

= (x) *(–9) = –9x

Multiply the inner terms of the binomials;

= (–3) * (2x) = –6x

Multiply the last terms of each binomial;

= (–3) * (–9) = 27

Sum up the products following the foil order and collect the like terms;

= 2x 2 – 9x -6x + 27

= 2x 2 – 15x +27

16. how to multiply two binomials


In multiplying two binomials we just perform FOIL method, it is simply like distribution so for example:
(x+1)(x+2) we do First, Outer, Inner, Last

First:  (x+1)(x+2)  : x²
Outer: (x+1)(x+2)  : 2x
Inner: (x+1)(x+2)  : x
Last: (x+1)(x+2)  : 2

We add all so:
(x+1)(x+2) 
= x²+2x+x+2
= x²+3x+2
