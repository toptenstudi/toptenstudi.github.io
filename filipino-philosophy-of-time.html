8. discuss the philosophy of human values in the Filipino context​


Answer:

Filipino values are, for the most part, centered at maintaining social harmony, motivated primarily by the desire to be accepted within a group. The main sanction against diverging from these values are the concepts of "Hiya", roughly translated as 'a sense of shame', and "Amor propio" or 'self-esteem'.

20. explain the following filipino philosophy in your own words and understanding provide example 1.Bahala Na 2.Filipino Time​


Answer:

1.Bahala Na

- Bahala na is a socio-cultural value in the Philippines as well as a phrasing in Filipino language that is either said as an expression of a fatalistic attitude towards life or as a determined one in a challenging situation where things are risky and uncertain.  

Example: When going home late, we fear that our parents will scold us but we have to choice because we need to go home still so we say “ bahala na”.

2.Filipino Time

- Filipino time is the attitude of Filipinos who starts everything late. When meeting people, we believe we must come a little later after the given exact time because we think that we can save time and when we get there the event already started. We think that, if we come too early, we have to still wait for the others, so why not come together with the others?

Example: When deciding for a trip with friends we set our time earlier that what it’s supposed to be because we know that they will be there a little late.
