1/7 of sister's friends became brother's friends, so brother gained (1/7)*15 = 2 friends from sister's friends.

Sister gained 35 more than brother's friends, so sister has (x - 40) + 35 = x - 5 friends.

2/9 of sister's friends are also brother's friends, so the number of friends they have in common is (2/9)*(x - 5).

4/6 of brother's friends became brother's enemies, so brother has (2/6)*8 = 16 friends left.

15 + 6 + (2/9)*(x - 5) = 8 + 16

2(x - 5) = 27

x = 19

25. enrique spends 2/5 of his monthly salary, saves 1/3 of the remainder and shares the rest equally to his 2 sisters and 1 brother. If his monthly salary is 60000, how much does he gave to his brother?


2/5 of monthly salary is spent so subtract:
[tex]1 - \frac{2}{5} = \frac{5 - 2}{5} = \frac{3}{5} [/tex]
1/3 of 3/5(the remainder) will go to his savings so multiply first both terms and subtract the product to 3/5:
[tex] \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{3}{5} = \frac{3}{15} = \frac{1}{5} \\ \\ \frac{3}{5} - \frac{1}{5} = \frac{3 - 1}{5} = \frac{2}{5} [/tex]
He has 3 siblings(2 sisters and 1 brother) to equally share 2/5 of his salary(the rest) so divide 2/5 by 3:
[tex] \frac{2}{5} \div 3 = \frac{2}{5} \times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{2 \times 1}{5 \times 3} = \frac{2}{15} [/tex]
So the brother will get 2/15 of the total salary of Enrique:
[tex]60000 \times \frac{2}{15} = 4000 \times 2 = 8000[/tex]
So the brother will get 8000.
