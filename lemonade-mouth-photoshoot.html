6. 5) A 5-gallon jar is to be filled with lemonade. If a 6-ounce can of frozen concentrated makes one quart of lemonade, how many cans will it take to make the 5 gallons of lemonade? lemonade​


Answer Step-by-step explanation:

There are 4 quarts in 1 gallon, so 5 gallons is equal to 5 x 4 = 20 quarts of lemonade.

Since one 6-ounce can of frozen concentrated lemonade makes one quart of lemonade, we can use this information to determine how many cans we need to make 20 quarts.

One quart is equivalent to 32 ounces (4 quarts x 8 ounces per cup). Therefore, each 6-ounce can of frozen concentrated lemonade will make 32/6 = 5.33 quarts of lemonade.

To make 20 quarts, we need 20/5.33 = 3.75 cans of frozen concentrated lemonade. Since we can't use a fraction of a can, we need to round up to the next whole number of cans.

Therefore, it will take 4 cans of frozen concentrated lemonade to make 5 gallons of lemonade.
