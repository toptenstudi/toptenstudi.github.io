3. 3 real life word problem in geometric sequence


1. Applying geometric sequences to radioactive decay is a very usual application. It works in the way that, say you start with 100g of element X, then if you have the half-life of say, 1 day, after 1 day, you will be left with 50g. After 2 days, you will have 25g, and so on.

An example problem for this would be: We have 2000g of Element X, which has the half-life of 3 months. When will we run out of Element X?

2. Another example we can use would be the bouncing ball. Usually, a ball bounces high for the first bounce, and then reaches only a part of that for the next bounce, and a part of that for the next as well.

For example: A bouncing ball reaches a height of 60 inches on the first bounce. If on each succeeding bounce, the height is 80% of the previous one, at which bounce will the height be approximately 10 inches?


3. Another usual example is compound interest. Say, for example you deposited 100 today, at an interest rate of 6% per annum. Then at the end of the year you would have 100(1.06). At the end of 2 years, you would have 100(1.06)^2.

For example: You deposit 10,000 in an investment which gives an interest of 3% per annum. How much would you earn from this after 10 years?

Hope this helps!

8. Give an example about geometric sequence word problem


Answer:

A geometric sequence is a sequence of numbers in which the ratio between consecutive terms is constant. where r is the common ratio between successive terms. Example 1: {2,6,18,54,162,486,1458,...}.

Definition of Geometric Sequences

For example, the sequence 2,6,18,54,⋯ 2 , 6 , 18 , 54 , ⋯ is a geometric progression with common ratio 3 . Similarly 10,5,2.5,1.25,⋯ 10 , 5 , 2.5 , 1.25 , ⋯ is a geometric sequence with common ratio 12 . for every integer n≥1.

A man joined a company as Assistant Manager. The company gave him a starting salary of ₹60,000 and agreed to increase his salary 5% annually. What will be his salary after 5 years?

solution

Starting salary = 60,000

Every year 5% of annual salary is increasing.

Second year salary = 60000 + 5% of 60000

= 60000(1 + 5%)

Third year salary = 60000 + 5% of 60000(1 + 5%)

= 60000(1 + 5%)(1 + 5%)

= 60000(1 + 5%)2

By continuing in this way, salary after 5 years is

= 60000(1 + 5%)5

= 60000(105/100)5

= 60000(1.05)5

You were hired as an errand boy at your uncle's shop. You were tasked to do little errands that help the store.At first, you were given P4000.00 for your first month. However, a month passed, your uncle noticed your visible hardwork so he decided to give you a salary rise and you're now getting P4500.00 for your monthly salary. You keep doing your best in working for the next month so your uncle once again raised your salary to P5000.00. How much salary will you gain if you keep working hard for the next 4 months?

Answer to the made problem:

FORMULA:

[tex]an \: = \: {a}^{n} \: + \: (n \: - \: 1) \: d[/tex]

Given:

a1 = P4000

n = 7

d = P500

Solution:

an = a1 + (n-1)d

a7 = 4000 + (7-1)500

a7 = 4000 + (6)500

a7 = 4000 + (3000)

a7 = 7000

Checking:

4000 + 500 = 4500 (a2)

4500 + 500 = 5000 (a3)

5000 + 500 = 5500 (a4)

5500 + 500 = 6000 (a5)

6000 + 500 = 6500 (a6)

6500 + 500 = 7000 (a7)

Final Answer :

If you keep doing your best in working for the next four months, your monthly salary might go up to P7000.00
