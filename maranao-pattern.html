16. weaving is one of the finest works of maranao people. one or their finest works in weaving is malong. if you look at the design of their malong, it has a combination of different colors and pattern called_______.​


Answer:

The malong is a traditional Filipino rectangular or tube-like wraparound skirt bearing a variety of geometric or okir designs. The malong is traditionally used as a garment by both men and women of the numerous ethnic groups in the mainland Mindanao and parts of the Sulu Archipelago. They are wrapped around at waist or chest-height and secured by tucked ends, with belts of braided material or other pieces of cloth, or are knotted over one shoulder. They were traditionally hand-woven, with the patterns usually distinctive to a particular ethnic group. However, modern malong are usually machine-made or even imported, with patterns that mimic the traditional local designs.

23. meaning of maranao?​


Answer:

Maranao people

Explanation:

The Maranao people (Maranao: ['mәranaw]; Filipino: Maranaw), also spelled Meranao, Maranaw, and Mëranaw, is the term used by the Philippine government to refer to the southern indigenous people who are the "people of the lake", a predominantly-Muslim Lanao province region of the Philippine island of Mindanao


24. It is a wooden box with intricate patterns and designs, and is where the maranaos usually keep their personal belongings and treasures.


Answer:

Baur or Baor

Explanation:

Baur of the Maranaos

The Baur of the Maranaos - Baor to some - is a wooden box with intricate patterns and designs that the Maranaos usually used to keep their personal belongings. Baur is a hand-carved chest with elaborate okir motifs and inlaid with polished shells or tipay. Also used as a treasure chest, first-class products are usually made from Narra while second-class are from Basudan.

Additional Information About the Baur of the Maranaos

For further knowledge about the Baur of the Maranaos, here are a few additional facts:

An average-sized baur takes up to three months to build.

Baur can also be used as a chair, a center table, or a side table.

The okir - or okkil - motif is a pattern of geometric and flowing plant-based designs.

26. ilan ang mga lalaki sa maranao/bilang ng lalaki sa maranao?​


Answer:

There is no definitive answer to this question as the Maranao people do not have a centralized system of record-keeping. However, based on various estimates, it is believed that there are approximately 1.5 million Maranao people living in the Philippines.


27. differentiate the attires (color, pattern, and accessories) of the following ethnic groups with one another • Tausug • Yakan • Maranao​


Tausug- They use Muslim motifs called ukkil or okir, which are made up of geometric and flowing arabesque designs.

Male Tausugs wear badju lapi (jacket), sawwal (pants), kandit (sash), and pis siabit (sash) (square piece of cloth tied around the head).

Biyatawi (top), tambuku (gold buttons), and habul tiyahian (bottom) for females (a rectangular cloth draped across the shoulder).

Yakan- Yakans are known for their amazing technicolor geometric weaves and distinctive face decorations, which are used in traditional ceremonies.

Maranao- Tribal and Muslim influences can be seen in the designs and curvilinear motifs, such as stylized vines and the sarimanok.
