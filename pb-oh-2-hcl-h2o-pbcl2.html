2. AlCl3 + H2O arrow Al(OH)3 + HCl


Explanation:

The chemical equation you provided represents a reaction between aluminum chloride (AlCl3) and water (H2O) to form aluminum hydroxide (Al(OH)3) and hydrochloric acid (HCl).

This is a double displacement reaction where the aluminum chloride and water molecules exchange their ions to form new compounds. The aluminum chloride dissociates in water to form Al3+ ions and Cl- ions, while water dissociates into H+ ions and OH- ions.

When the Al3+ ions react with the OH- ions, they form aluminum hydroxide, which is a white precipitate. At the same time, the H+ ions react with the Cl- ions to form hydrochloric acid, which is a strong acid.

Overall, this reaction represents a neutralization reaction where an acid (HCl) and a base (Al(OH)3) react to form a salt (AlCl3) and water (H2O).

Chemical Reaction:

2 HCl + Ca(OH)2 --> CaCl2 + 2H2O

M= 0.150 M Ca(OH)2

V = 30 mL Ca(OH)2

M= 0.250 M HCl

V = ? mL HCl

m = M x V = 0.15 x 30

m = 4.5 mol Ca(OH)2

Find mole in terms of HCl:

m = 4.5mol Ca(OH)2 x 2mol HCl / 1 mol Ca(OH)2

m = 9.0 mol HCl

Calculate the number of grams of HCl that can react with 0.500 g of Al(OH)₃ c. Calculate the number of grams of AlCl3 and the number of grams of H2O formed when 0.500 g of Al(OH)₃ reacts. d. Show that your calculations in parts (b) and (c) are consistent with the law of conservation of mass.


a. ANSWER:

Al(OH)₃ + 3HCl → AlCl₃ + 3H₂O

b. SOLUTION:

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of Al(OH)₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 3 moles of HCl.

The molar masses of Al(OH)₃ and HCl are 78.00 g/mol and 36.46 g/mol, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of HCl} & = \text{0.500 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}{\text{78.00 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3} \times \frac{\text{3 mol HCl}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3} \times \frac{\text{36.46 g HCl}}{\text{1 mol HCl}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.701 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

c. SOLUTION:

• For grams of AlCl₃

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of Al(OH)₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of AlCl₃.

The molar mass of AlCl₃ is 133.3 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{AlCl}_3 & = \text{0.500 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}{\text{78.00 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{AlCl}_3}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3} \times \frac{\text{133.3 g} \: \text{AlCl}_3}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{AlCl}_3} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.854 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For grams of H₂O

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of Al(OH)₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 3 moles of H₂O.

The molar mass of H₂O is 18.02 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{H}_2\text{O} & = \text{0.500 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}{\text{78.00 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3} \times \frac{\text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_2\text{O}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3} \times \frac{\text{18.02 g} \: \text{H}_2\text{O}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{H}_2\text{O}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.347 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

d. SOLUTION:

[tex]\begin{aligned} \sum mass_{\text{reactants}} & = \sum mass_{\text{products}} \\ \text{mass of} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3 + \text{mass of HCl} & = \text{mass of} \: \text{AlCl}_3 + \text{mass of} \: \text{H}_2\text{O} \\ \text{0.500 g + 0.701 g} & = \text{0.854 g + 0.347 g} \\ \text{1.201 g} & = \text{1.201 g} \: \checkmark \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the calculations in (b) and (c) are consistent with the law of conservation of mass.

Chemical Reactions

Involves rearrangement of the molecular or ionic structure of a substance. Process in which one or more reactants are converted to one or more different products. Can be classified into five basic types. Analyzing reactants and products will allow placing it into one of these categories. Some reactions will fit into more than one category.

Types of Chemical Reactions:combination/synthesisdecompositionsingle replacement/single displacementdouble replacement/double displacementcombustion

A combination reaction, also known as a synthesis reaction, is a reaction in which two or more substances combine to form a single new substance. Combination reactions can also be called synthesis reactions. One combination reaction is two elements combining to form a compound.

A decomposition reaction is a reaction in which a compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances. Most decomposition reactions require an input of energy in the form of heat, light, or electricity.

A single replacement reaction is a reaction in which one element replaces a similar element in a compound. A metal must be replaced by another metal while a non - metal must be replaced also by a non - metal.

A double replacement reaction is a reaction in which the positive and negative ions of two ionic compounds exchange places to form two new compounds. Double-replacement reactions generally occur between substances in aqueous solution. In order for a reaction to occur, one of the products is usually a solid precipitate, a gas, or a molecular compound such as water.

A combustion reaction is a reaction in which a substance reacts with oxygen gas, releasing energy in the form of light and heat. Combustion reactions must involve  O₂  as one reactant.
