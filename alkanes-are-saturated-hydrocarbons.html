1. why alkanes called saturated hydrocarbons?​


Answer:

The alkanes are a homologous series of hydrocarbon molecules (made of carbon and hydrogen atoms). Alkanes are referred to as 'saturated' hydrocarbons because they do NOT contain a carbon C=C carbon double bond and other atoms cannot add to alkane molecules.


2. Which of the following explains why alkanes are called saturated hydrocarbons?


Answer:

Saturated hydrocarbons are hydrocarbons that contain only single bonds between carbon atoms. They are the simplest class of hydrocarbons. They are called saturated because each carbon atom is bonded to as many hydrogen atoms as possible.

10. Why are unsaturated hydrocarbons more reactive than saturated hydrocarbons?


Answer:

In unsaturated hydrocarbons, there are two types of bonds: C=C and C=C. These two types of bonds are weaker than the single bond in saturated hydrocarbons. These double and triple bonds are the places in the hydrocarbons that aren't saturated that are most likely to have addition reactions.

The definition of alkanes

Alkanes are organic molecules that are made of hydrogen and carbon atoms bound together only once. Alkanes have the formula CnH2n+2, and they can be divided into 3 groups: chain alkanes, cycloalkanes, and branched alkanes.

Alkanes are also referred to saturate hydrocarbons. Alkanes are a class of compounds that have only one covalent link between a carbon atom and a hydrogen atom. The term "saturated hydrocarbons" refers to these. Carbon and hydrogen atoms are bonded together in this series of molecules by a single covalent bond. Additionally includes an analogous series with the chemical formula CnH2n+2.

The alkanes is the simple family of hydrocarbons. They only contain hydrogen and carbon. Each hydrogen atom forms one link, but each carbon atom forms four. The simple alkane methane has one carbon atom and the chemical formula CH4.  Because they are quicker and easier to draw than condensed structural formulations, chemists prefer to utilize line-angle formulas. Alkane structural formulas can also be written in a condensed form.

17. Define the following termsHydrocarbonsAlkanesAlkenesAlkynes​


Answer:

Hydrocarbons — a compound of hydrogen and carbon, such as any of those which are the chief components of petroleum and natural gas.

Alkanes — any of the series of saturated hydrocarbons including methane, ethane, propane, and higher members.

Alkenes — any of the series of unsaturated hydrocarbons containing a double bond, including ethylene and propylene.

Alkynes — any of the series of unsaturated hydrocarbons containing a triple bond, including acetylene.
