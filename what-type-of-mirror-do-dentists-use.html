15. what type of mirror do dentists usually use to clearly see the images of our teeth?


A mouth mirror, concave mirror or dentist's mirror is an instrument used in dentistry. The head of the mirror is usually round. Its most important functions are allowing indirect vision by the dentist, reflecting light onto desired surfaces, and retraction of soft tissues. Indirect vision is needed in certain locations of the mouth where visibility is difficult or impossible. The mouth mirror is used to retract tissues, such as the tongue or cheeks, to gain better visualization of the teeth. Concave mirrors are used by the dentists to see the large images of the teeth of patient. Answer:

The location of the object affects the characteristics and location of the image formed in a concave mirror by determining the size, orientation, and position of the image. Objects placed closer to the mirror will produce a larger, upright image that is farther away from the mirror. Objects placed farther away from the mirror will produce a smaller, inverted image that is closer to the mirror. For a convex mirror, the image is always smaller, inverted, and located behind the mirror regardless of the object's location.

Dentists usually use a concave mirror to clearly see the images of our teeth. Concave mirrors have the ability to magnify objects, making them more visible. This is useful for dentists as they need to be able to see our teeth clearly in order to diagnose and treat any problems.

Most department stores use a convex mirror. Convex mirrors provide a wider field of view, allowing customers to see a larger area of the store from the same spot. This is useful for security purposes, as it allows store staff to have a better view of the store and any potential shoplifters.
