24. Modal verbs and fanboy sentences


Answer:

hope it helps:)

Explanation:

modal verbs are a type of auxiliary verb that are used to indicate possibility, necessity, or permission. Some common modal verbs include "can," "could," "may," "might," "must," "shall," "should," "will," and "would."

FANBOYS is an acronym that stands for "For," "And," "Nor," "But," "Or," "Yet," and "So." These are coordinating conjunctions that can be used to connect clauses in a sentence. Here are some examples of sentences using modal verbs and FANBOYS:

"I can come to the party if you want me to." (using "can" as a modal verb)

"I could finish this project by the end of the day, or I might need a little more time." (using "could" and "might" as modal verbs)

"You must be home by 10:00 PM, or you'll be grounded." (using "must" as a modal verb)

"We should go to the museum, but we might not have enough time." (using "should" and "but" as a modal verb and a coordinating conjunction)

"She will be here soon, so we should get started." (using "will" as a modal verb and "so" as a coordinating conjunction
