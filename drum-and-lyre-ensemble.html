4. What are the differences between rondalla, drum and lyre band, and philippine indigenous ensembles? Patulong po please. Tama=Brainliest Nonsense=Report


Answer:

The rondalla is an ensemble of stringed instruments played with the plectrum or pick and generally known as plectrum instruments. It originated in Medieval Spain, especially in the ancient Crown of Aragon: Catalonia,[1] Aragon, Murcia, and Valencia. The tradition was later taken to Spanish America and the Philippines. The word rondalla is from the Spanish ronda, meaning "serenade."

And The three main forms introduced to the Filipinos were the harana, the kundiman, and the rondalla. Most of these forms were developed as a result of the fusion between tribal music styles and traditional Spanish and Mexican music.Dec 29, 2012

Answer:

D. Drum and Bugle Corps.

Explanation:

A Drum and Bugle Corps is a musical ensemble that typically consists of brass instruments, percussion instruments (including snare drums, tenor drums, bass drums, and sometimes a glockenspiel), and a color guard. The color guard is a group of performers who dance and spin flags, rifles, and other equipment to enhance the visual aspect of the performance.
