14. give at least one senario exemplifies the theory of mans thancedence 1.)power ethical theory 2.) Principles of university ethical theory 3.) Principles or humanity ethnical theory (Confucuis approach) 4.) Principles of humanity ethnical theory (christ Approach)


Answer:

1) Power Ethics Theory: The power ethics theory states that the powerful members of society have the right to use their power to influence the behavior of others. For example, a CEO of a company may use their position to dictate the rules and policies that all employees must follow, or a government leader may use their power to pass laws that all citizens must abide by.

2) Principles of University Ethics Theory: The principles of university ethics theory states that universities should not only teach students about ethics but also hold universities accountable for their actions. This theory suggests that universities should include a set of ethical guidelines in their policies and procedures to ensure that all staff and faculty members understand and follow ethical standards.

3) Principles of Humanity Ethical Theory (Confucian Approach): The principles of humanity ethical theory (Confucian approach) states that all individuals should practice benevolence and righteousness in their daily lives. This theory proposes that individuals should treat others with respect, show compassion and kindness, and work together in order to create a harmonious society.

4) Principles of Humanity Ethical Theory (Christian Approach): The principles of humanity ethical theory (Christian approach) states that all individuals should follow the teachings of Jesus Christ, which emphasize the importance of showing love to others, being truthful and honest, and treating others with respect. This theory also encourages individuals to strive for spiritual growth and to live a life that is guided by Christian values.

Answer:

Ethical theories are often broadly divided into three types: i) Consequentialist theories, which are primarily concerned with the ethical consequences of particular actions; ii) Non-consequentialist theories, which tend to be broadly concerned with the intentions of the person making ethical decisions about particular actions; and iii) Agent-centered theories, which, unlike consequentialist and non-consequentialist theories, are more concerned with the overall ethical status of individuals, or agents, and are less concerned to identify the morality of particular actions. Each of these three broad categories contains varieties of approaches to ethics, some of which share characteristics across the categories. 26. Ethical theories utilitarianism and natural law


Answer:Utilitarianism and natural law theory, though from different schools of philosophical thought, have some structural similarities. The first overarching parallel is that both ethical theories assume an objective moral order, so that in every situation there is a universal right and a universal wrong course of action. It is possible, therefore, that a man living by utilitarianism and a man living by the natural law may come to choose the same course of moral action in a certain situation. For example, given Mrs. Jones, an elderly woman in obvious need of help crossing the street, the utilitarian would see it to be the greatest good to help her across, and the natural law theorist would see goodness and the preservation of life as reasons to offer his assistance. Furthermore, both utilitarianism and natural law theory involve some sort of calculation. Utilitarianism’s felicific calculus is the concrete extreme of weighing a moral decision, but a natural law theorist goes through the same process based on his conscience. Note, as opposed to the pro/con calculus of the utilitarian, dependence on one’s conscience is a process of reason
