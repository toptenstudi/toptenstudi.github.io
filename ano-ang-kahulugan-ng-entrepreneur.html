4. Ano-ano ang kahulugan ng entrepreneur at entrepreneurship​


An entrepreneur is a person who starts a new business, bearing the majority of the risks and reaping the majority of the rewards. The entrepreneur is frequently regarded as an innovator, a provider of new ideas, items, services, and business/or operations. Because of their capacity and initiative to foresee needs and provide useful new ideas to the market, entrepreneurs are crucial to the success of every economy.

Different entrepreneurs have different personalities and objectives. Here are a few examples of different types of entrepreneurs:

Opportunist. Opportunistic entrepreneurs are optimistic individuals who can identify financial possibilities, engage at the proper time, stay on board during growth, and exit when a business reaches its pinnacle. These entrepreneurs are focused on profits and the wealth they will create. As a result, they are drawn to initiatives that have the potential to produce residual or renewal money. Opportunistic entrepreneurs can be passionate since they are seeking for well-timed possibilities.Innovator. Innovators are remarkable people who come up with a brilliant notion or product that no one else has thought of. Rather than focusing on money, innovators are concerned with the social impact of their products and services. These people are not the best at operating a business because they are idea generators.

Entrepreneurship is the ability and willingness to design, plan, and run a company operation—complete with all of its unknowns. The establishment of new businesses is the most apparent manifestation of entrepreneurship. Entrepreneurship can deal with anything that can be profitable.

Similar with entrepreneur, entrepreneurship also has some different types. Here are a few examples of different types of entrepreneurship:

Entrepreneurship in Scalable Startups. This entrepreneur launches a business with the belief that their concept has the potential to affect the world. They attract thinkers and urge individuals to think creatively. More capital investment is required to fuel and support their business.Entrepreneurship for Social Good. This form of entrepreneurship is concerned with creating products and services that address social needs and challenges. Their primary mission and objective are to work for society rather than for profit.

Answer:

An entrepreneur is an individual who creates a new business, bearing most of the risks and enjoying most of the rewards. The entrepreneur is commonly seen as an innovator, a source of new ideas, goods, services, and business/or procedures.

Entepreneurship is the activity of setting up a business or businesses, taking on financial risks in the hope of profit.

Ang entrepreneur ay isang tao o indibidwal na namamahala at nagpapatakbo ng isang negosyo sa madaling salita, sila ang tinatawag na negosyante. Kahalagahan ng Entrepreneur

Ang mga entrepreneur ay nakakalikha ng mga bagong hanapbuhay at trabaho na makatutulong sa tao Ang mga entrepreneur ay nakatutulong sa paglaho ng ekonomiya ng bansa. Ang mga entrepreneur ang nagpapakilala ng mga bagong produkto, kalakal at serbisyon sa mga pamilihan. Ang mga entrepreneur ay nakakahanap ng mga makabagong paraan na magpahusay sa mga kasanayan ng bawat indibidwal. Ang mga entrepreneur ay makapag hahatid ng bagong teknolohiya, industriya, at produkto sa pamilihan na makatutulong sa pag-unlad ng bansa. Ang entrepreneur ang nangunguna upang pagsamahin ang mga salik ng produksyon tulad ng lupa, paggawa, at puhunan upang makalikha ng produkto at serbisyo na kailangan sa ekonomiya ng bansa. Ang mga entrepreneur ang tumutulong sa bawat indibidwal upang mas mapalago ang mga kakayanan at kakayahan tungo sa pansariling pag-unlad.

Entrepreneurship

Ang entrepreneurship ay tumutukoy sa kakayahan ng isang indibidwal na mabatid ang mga kalakal at serbisyo na kailangan ng tao at maihatid ang mga ito sa tamang panahon, tamang lugar, at tamang madla at maibenta sa tamang halaga.
