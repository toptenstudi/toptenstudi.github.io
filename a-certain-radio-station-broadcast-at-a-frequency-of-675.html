26. A certain radio station broadcasts on a frequency of 100000 Hz. What is the wavelength of the radio waves?​


ELECTROMAGNETIC WAVES

A certain radio station broadcasts on a frequency of 100000 Hz. What is the wavelength of the radio waves?

Solution :-

Given :

Velocity or Speed of Wave (v) = 3.0 × 10⁸ m/sFrequency (f) = 100000 Hz

According to the question by using the formula we get,

[tex] \qquad \qquad\begin{gathered}\implies \sf\boxed{\bold{\lambda =\: \dfrac{v}{f}}}\\\end{gathered} [/tex]

where,

λ = Wavelengthv = Wave Speed (Velocity)f = Frequency

So, by putting those values we get,

[tex] \qquad \qquad\begin{gathered}\implies \sf \lambda =\: \dfrac{3.0 \times 10^8}{100000}\\\end{gathered} [/tex]

We can write as :

[tex] \qquad \qquad\begin{gathered}\implies \sf \lambda =\: \dfrac{3.0 \times 10^8}{1 \times 10^5}\\\end{gathered} [/tex]

As we know that :

[tex] \qquad \qquad\begin{gathered}\bigstar \: \: \boxed{ \bold{ \: \: \dfrac{a^m}{a^n} =\: a^{m - n} \: \: }}\: \: \: \bigstar\\\end{gathered} [/tex]

So, by using this identities we get,

[tex]\begin{gathered}\implies \sf \lambda =\: \dfrac{3.0 \times 10^{(8 - 5)}}{1}\\\end{gathered} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered}\implies \sf \lambda =\: \dfrac{3.0 \times 10^3}{1}\\\end{gathered} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered}\implies \sf \lambda =\: \dfrac{3.0 \times 1000}{1}\\\end{gathered} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered}\implies \sf \lambda =\: \dfrac{3000}{1}\\\end{gathered} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \qquad\begin{gathered}\implies \sf\bold{\underline{\lambda =\: 3000\: m}}\\\end{gathered} [/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The wavelength of the radio waves is 3000 m .

Answer:

3000 m

Explanation:

Radio waves are electromagnetic waves.

All electromagnetic waves travel at the same speed through a vacuum or the air. The speed is equal to the speed of light (c):

v = c = 300,000,000 meter per second (m/s).

Wave speed equation:

[tex] \it{v= f \lambda}[/tex]

where:

v = wave speed in meters per second (m/s)f = frequency in Hertz (Hz)λ = wavelength in meters (m)

Given:

v = 300 000 000 m/sf = 100 000 Hz

Substitute the given values into the formula and solve for λ:

[tex]\begin{gathered}\begin{aligned}v &= f \lambda\\ \qquad 300\;000\;000 &= 100\;000 \lambda\\ \lambda &=\dfrac{300\;000\;000}{100\;000}\\ \lambda &=3000\; \sf m\end{aligned}\end{gathered} [/tex]

Therefore, the wavelength of the radio waves is 3000 m.
