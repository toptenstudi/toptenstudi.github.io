12. give a short description of piko game​


Answer:

patayan game sali ka sa laro ng brutal

Answer:

Piko or hopscotch is one of the most famous Filipino games. It is a group game and can be played with as many people as you want. Unlike games nowadays, piko is more physical and requires a lot of jumping.

Growing up, many of us has played piko. You can still see children playing piko on the streets today. It may have simple mechanics, it is still a fun game to play.

Playing “piko” is very simple. All you need to have is marker usually chalk or charcoal or anything than can be used to draw lines in the ground. You also need a “pamato” for this game, this is usually a stone or part of a broken pot.stone or part of a broken pot.

How to play the game:

Throw the stone on the marked area. Try to aim for number five. The player whose stone is nearest to the number five is the first to play and the stone furthest is the last one.

The first player will throw the stone at the first box. Then, he will jump across the area without stepping in the first box.

Before he returns, he should pick up his stone, still, without jumping on the first box.

It’s the next player’s turn. He will only do what the first player did.

After all players get to turn. The first player will now throw his stone on the second box and repeat the procedure, now not stepping on the second box.

If a player steps on a line, they stop and let the next player try. They have to wait until their next turn to try again.

The first player to reach the last box wins.

25. What is the background of the game PIKO?


Answer:

HOPE THIS HELP!!

(PA BRAINLIEST)

ANSWER:

PIKO was created by the Japanese game development studio Nurijoy, which was founded in 2013 by former Arc System Works employee and game designer Keisuke Kikuchi. The studio is best known for their work on the popular rhythm games Deemo, Cytus and VOEZ. PIKO is the studio’s first venture into the puzzle game genre. The game was released on iOS and Android in August 2018.

In PIKO, players must match colored blocks on a grid-based board to form a continuous path. The path must lead from a starting node to an end node, while also touching all other nodes on the board. As the game progresses, the boards become more complex and require more strategic planning. The game features a variety of modes, including a single-player campaign, a versus mode, and a time attack.
