8. What are the contributions of South and West Asia in: Literature : Painting : Sculpting/architecture : dance/music: sports/math/science :


Explanation:

Here are some contributions of South and West Asia in various fields:

Literature:

South and West Asia has a rich literary heritage, with famous epics like the Ramayana and the Mahabharata originating from the region.

Persian and Urdu poetry is also highly regarded, with poets like Rumi, Hafiz, and Mirza Ghalib having a significant impact on literature.

Painting:

The art of miniature painting is an important contribution of South and West Asia. The region is known for its intricate and detailed paintings, often featuring mythical and religious figures.

Persian, Mughal, and Rajasthani paintings are some of the most notable styles from the region.

Sculpting/Architecture:

South and West Asia has a rich history of architectural masterpieces, including the Taj Mahal, the Great Mosque of Mecca, and the Alhambra Palace in Spain.

Sculpting in the region has been heavily influenced by religious and spiritual themes, with intricate carvings and statues often found in temples, mosques, and other holy sites.

Dance/Music:

South and West Asia is known for its diverse range of music and dance styles, including classical Indian dance forms like Bharatanatyam and Kathak, as well as Qawwali music from Pakistan.

Classical music from the region, including Hindustani and Carnatic music, has also had a significant impact on the world music scene.

Sports/Math/Science:

The ancient game of chess is believed to have originated in India, and many other popular games like polo and kabaddi have roots in South and West Asia.

Scholars from the region have made significant contributions to the fields of mathematics and science, with notable figures like Al-Khwarizmi, Ibn al-Haytham, and Aryabhata making important discoveries and advancements.
