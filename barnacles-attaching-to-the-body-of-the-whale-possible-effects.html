1. 3. barnacles attaching to the bodyof the whale​


Barnacles attach to the whale's skin and benefit from an easy source of planktonic food drifting into their feathery filters as the whale swims. The whale is not harmed or helped by the barnacles that are permanently attached to their thick, tough skin.

ECOLOGICAL RELATIONSHIPS

Answers with Explanation:

1. elephants fighting each other

Possible Effect: The weaker elephant will lose the battle, and it will get harmed.

Explanation: The elephants fight each other either because of territory or they fight for a female elephant. This ecological relationship is called competition because two organisms compete each other for limited resources like food, water and territory.

2. barnacles attaching to the body of the whale

Possible Effect: The whale will be unaffected. The barnacles can have transport and enjoy plenty foods when they attached to the whale.

Explanation: This ecological relationship is referred as commensalism because one organism benefits from the interaction while another is neither harmed nor benefited.

3. the pine beetle laying eggs in the pine trees

Possible Effect: The pine beetles serve as parasite for pine trees. Pine trees are harmed because pine beetles dwell in them. Although, thriving in the pine trees gives pine beetles protection, it is not good for pine trees to have them.

Explanation: This ecological relationship is referred to as parasitism because one organism benefits while another one is being harmed.

4. the lichen consists of a fungus and an algae growing together

Possible Effect: The algae provides carbon dioxide for the fungus which it uses for photosynthesis.  In return, the fungus gives protection to it.

Explanation: This ecological relationship is referred to as mutualism because both organisms benefit from each other.

5. pitcher plant catching insects

Possible Effect: The pitcher catches insects and kill them.

Explanation: This ecological relationship is called predation because one organism benefits while the other one is being killed.

16. what type of ecological interaction exists between the whale and barnacles​


Answer:

In the case of barnacles and whales, only the barnacles benefit from attaching to the whales, but at no biological cost to the whale. This type of symbiotic relationship is known as commensalism. In this case, attaching to the whales gives the barnacles a stable place to live, a free ride, and access to plenty of food.
