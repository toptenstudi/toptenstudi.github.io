4. a group of children playing"the boat is sinking"draw a circle with 2m in diameter,circle B with 1.4m radius,and circle C with 3m in diameter. what is the total area of the circles​


Answer:

The total area of the circles is 16.3594 square meters.

The area of circle A is 3.14 square meters. The area of circle B id 6.1544 square meters. The area of circle C is 7.065 square meters. Putting them all together we get 16.3594 square meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

Circle A

To get the area of circle A, we need to find first the radius. Since we are given the diameter which is 2 m, we can half of it by dividing it by 2.

2 ÷ 2 = 1

It means that the radius of circle A is 1 m. Using the formula for finding the area of a circle, we can get:

Area = π(pi or 3.14) × radius^{2}  ⇒ 3.14 × 1^{2} = 3.14

The area of circle A is 3.14 square meters.

Circle B

To get the area of circle B, we use the given radius which is 1.4 m to immediately solve for the area.

Area = π(pi or 3.14) × radius^{2}  ⇒ 3.14 × 1.4^{2} = 6.1544

The area of circle B is 6.1544 square meters.

Circle C

To get the area of circle C, we need to find first the radius. Since we are given the diameter which is 3 m, we can half of it by dividing it by 2.

3 ÷ 2 = 1.5

It means that the radius of circle C is 1.5 m. Using the formula for finding the area of a circle, we can get:

Area = π(pi or 3.14) × radius^{2}  ⇒ 3.14 × 1.5^{2} = 7.065

The area of circle C is 7.065 square meters.

Total area of the circles A, B, and C

In finding the total area, just add the areas of the three circles:

3.14 + 6.1544 + 7.065 = 16.3594

The total area of the three circles is 16.3594 square meters.

9. How does boat float in water and how does boat sink pahelp po at sana po with explination​


If the downward gravitational force is less than the upward buoyancy force then the object floats, otherwise it sinks. That is, if an object weighs less than the amount of water it displaces then it floats otherwise it sinks. A boat floats because it displaces water that weighs more than its own weight.

An object will float if it weighs less than the amount of water it displaces. So here the weight of the ship is less than that the amount of water it displaces. Hence it floats on water.

The air that is inside a ship is much less dense than water. That's what keeps it floating! The average density of the total volume of the ship and everything inside of it (including the air) must be less than the same volume of water.
