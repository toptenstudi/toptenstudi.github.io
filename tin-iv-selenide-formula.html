8. If 21 tins of milk cost $63,What will be the cost of 16 tins


Answer:

The cost of 16 tins is $48.

Step-by-step explanation:

Ratio:

Tins of milk : Cost

Let x be the cost of milk for 16 tins:

Direct Proportion:

In direct proportion, the product of means is equal to the product of extremes.

21 : 63 = 16 : x

Means of proportion: 63 and 16

Extremes of proportion: 21 and x

Solve for x:

21x = (63)(16)

21x/21 = 1,008/21

x = 48

The cost of 16 tins is $48.

11. In an experiment, 0.500 g of tin reacted with nitric acid to give tin oxide. If the oxide had a mass of 0.635 g, what is the emperical formula of the tin oxide?please show your complete solution. ​


Answer:

empirical formula = SnO2

Explanation:

Calculating the mass of O

mass O = mass oxide - mass Sn

mass O = 0.635 g - 0.500 g

mass O = 0.135 g

Calculating the number of moles of each element

n Sn = 0.500 g × (1 mol / 118.7 g) = 0.00421 mol

n O = 0.135 g × (1 mol / 16.00 g) = 0.00844 mol

Determining the empirical formula

empirical formula = SnxOy

Dividing the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles

x = 0.00421 mol / 0.00421 mol = 1

y = 0.00844 mol / 0.00421 mol = 2

empirical formula = SnO2

Answer:

$48

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Write it in a proportion form.

21/63 = 16/n

Step 2: Cross multiply. Numerator to Numerator. Denominator to Denominator.

21 x n = 16 x 63

21n = 1008

Step 3: Solve for the value of n by dividing 21 on both sides of the equation.

21n/21 = 1008/21      (21n divided by 21 = 1008 divided by 21)

n = 48
