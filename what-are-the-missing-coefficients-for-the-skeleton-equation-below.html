1. What are the missing coefficients for the skeleton equation below? NH3 (g) + O2(g) → N2(g) + H2O(l)​


The balanced equation :

4NH₃ (g) + 3O₂(g) → 2N₂(g) + 6H₂O(l)​

Further explanation

A chemical reaction can be expressed in terms of:  

words equation the skeleton equation  balanced equation

The skeleton equation is still in unbalanced form  

so only chemical formulas of reactants and products so that the relative amounts of the reactants and products are not included

Reaction

NH₃ (g) + O₂(g) → N₂(g) + H₂O(l)​

Give a coefficient to balance equation :

NH₃ (g) + aO₂(g) → bN₂(g) + cH₂O(l)​

Make a equation :

N, left=1, right=2b⇒2b=1⇒b=1/2

H, left=3, right=2c⇒2c=3⇒c=3/2

O, left=2a, right=c⇒2a=c⇒2a=3/2⇒a=3/4

The equation becomes :

NH₃ (g) + 3/4O₂(g) → 1/2N₂(g) + 3/2H₂O(l) ​x4

4NH₃ (g) + 3O₂(g) → 2N₂(g) + 6H₂O(l)​

7. Find the general equation of the line tangent to the equation below at the given point. What is the coefficient of y in the equation of the tangent line? y=9-x^2 ; at (2,5)


Solution:

Step 1: Get the derivative of the equation of the curve.

[tex]y = 9 - x^{2}[/tex]

[tex]y' = -2x[/tex]

Step 2: Find the slope of the curve at (2,3).

Note: slope = m = y'

Since x = 2

[tex]m = y' = -2(2)[/tex]

[tex]m = -4[/tex]

Step 3: Find the general equation of the tangent line by using the point-slope form.

[tex]y - y_{1} = m(x - x_{1})[/tex]

[tex]y - 3 = -4(x - 2)[/tex]

[tex]y - 3 = -4x + 8[/tex]

[tex]4x + y - 3 - 8 = 0[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{4x + y - 11 = 0}[/tex]

The coefficient of y in the equation of the tangent line is 1.

Notes:

The general form of the equation of a line is Ax + By + C = 0.The standard form of the equation of a line is Ax + By = C.

24. 40. In the reacton, equation? KCIO3 ->__ KCl +___02, write the missing coefficients to balance the chemical equation?​


Answer:

Here's the balanced equation for the decomposition of KClO3

2KClO3 ---> 2KCl + 3O2

Steps to balance a chemical equation:

1. Count the number of atoms in reactants and products. If the number of atoms in reactants and the products are not equal the equation is not balanced. This is where you need to balance the equation.

2. to balance the equation you need to place coefficients, before the symbols or formulas to increase the number of atoms or molecules of the substances. Use the smallest coefficients possible. Never change the subscripts in chemical formulas. Changing subscripts changes the substances involved in the reaction. Change only the coefficients.

Repeat steps 1 and 2 until the equation is balanced
