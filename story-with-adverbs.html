2. write a short story with five adverbs of manner and five adverbs of time and five adverbs of place​


Answer:

Once upon a time, there was a young girl named Lily who lived in a small town. She had always dreamed of exploring the world, but she was too afraid to leave the safety of her home.

One day, as she was walking through the forest, she heard a rustling sound. Curiosity getting the best of her, she cautiously walked towards the sound. Suddenly, she saw a brightly colored bird fly past her with incredible speed.

Amazed by the bird's speed, she ran after it as quickly as she could, jumping over logs and dodging trees. Eventually, the bird led her to a beautiful waterfall, where it perched on a nearby rock. Lily stood there, awestruck by the natural beauty of the place.

As the sun began to set, she realized that she had been out for hours and it was starting to get dark. Quickly, she gathered her things and started to make her way back home, using the light of the moon to guide her.

Breathlessly, she arrived home just as the clock struck midnight. Exhausted, but filled with a sense of adventure and wonder, she fell asleep dreaming of all the incredible places she would explore in the future.

Adverbs of manner: cautiously, quickly, awestruck, breathlessly, exhausted

Adverbs of time: once, always, eventually, hours, midnight

Adverbs of place: small town, forest, waterfall, nearby rock, home

Once upon a time, in a quaint little village nestled deep in the forest, there lived a young girl named Emily. She was a curious and adventurous child, always eager to explore the world around her.

One sunny morning, Emily woke up early with excitement twinkling in her eyes. She put on her sturdy boots, packed a small bag with snacks and water, and set off on an adventure into the woods. As she walked along the narrow path, the birds chirped merrily overhead, and the gentle breeze whispered through the trees.

Emily walked for hours, taking in the sights and sounds of the forest. She spotted a deer gracefully leaping through the undergrowth and heard the rustling of leaves as a squirrel scampered by. She marveled at the vibrant colors of the flowers and the dappled sunlight filtering through the canopy.

After a while, Emily came across a rickety old bridge spanning a gushing stream. She hesitated for a moment but then bravely crossed it, holding her breath as she carefully stepped on each plank. When she reached the other side, she let out a sigh of relief and continued her journey.

As the day wore on, Emily found herself in a clearing with a tall tree in the center. She gazed up at its majestic branches, reaching out to the sky like a giant umbrella. She felt a sense of awe and wonder, and without thinking, she exclaimed, "Wow!" Her voice echoed in the quiet forest, and she laughed with delight.

Feeling a little tired, Emily decided to rest under the tree. She spread out her blanket and sat down, savoring the peacefulness of the woods. She nibbled on her snacks and took a sip of water, feeling grateful for the adventure and the beauty of nature.

As the sun began to set, Emily packed up her things and started her journey back home. She retraced her steps, crossed the bridge once again, and walked along the familiar path. She reached her village just as the stars were coming out, feeling accomplished and happy.

Emily's parents welcomed her home with open arms, and she eagerly shared her adventure with them. She regaled them with tales of her encounters with wildlife, the breathtaking scenery, and the thrill of crossing the old bridge. Her parents listened intently, smiling at their daughter's enthusiasm.

That night, as Emily lay in bed, she reflected on her day. She realized how much she had learned and experienced during her forest adventure. She felt grateful for the beauty of nature, the joy of exploration, and the sense of accomplishment. With a contented sigh, she drifted off to sleep, eagerly anticipating her next adventure.

Adverbs: early, merrily, carefully, bravely, quietly, little

Prepositions: in, along, through, across, under, with, like, in, on, without, feeling, for, of, back, as

Interjection: "Wow!"
