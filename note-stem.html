notation that has a shaded head, stem and tail is called block notation

Explanation:

Notation is one of the musical elements that make up a song. Block notation is a symbol or sign to express the high and low voice that is manifested by pictures. Beam notation is also called absolute notation, because it has a fixed pitch (a = 440 Hz) so it is very effective in playing music. Block notation is a system of writing songs or other musical works that are poured in the form of symbols or images. Block notation is written on a stick, five parallel lines of the same length. Each note and beat has a different notation symbol.

The parts of the block notation are divided into three, namely the flag, stem and head.

Block Notation Type

Block notation is divided into several types, namely:

1. Notation full (whole)

Full notation is denoted by an empty circle. This notation is a notation that has a four-beat tone.

2. Half note (1/2)

This type of notation is denoted by an empty circle and a pole at the top. This type of notation has a total of two notes only.

3. One-quarter (1/4) note

One-quarter notation is represented by a black circle and a pole at the top. This notation has only one note number.

4. One-eighth note (1/8)

One-eighth notes is a type of notation with a note count of 1/2 beats. This type of note is represented by a black circle with a pole and a flag on it.

5. One-sixteenth (1/16)

This type of notation has a note count of 1/4 beats and is represented by a black circle with a pole and two flags at the top.

6. One-thirty-two (1/32)

This type of block notation has a pitch of 1/8 beat and is depicted by a black circle with a pole and three flags on it.
