11. compare and contrast the active listening and listining​


Answer:

In active listening, the listener concentrates, understands, responds and remembers the statement spoken by the speaker. In contrast, in passive listening, the listener is usually engrossed in his/her own thoughts and does not pays attention to the speaker but only pretends that he/she is listening.

Explanation:

What is the difference between listening and active listening?

Passive listening is one-way communication where the receiver doesn't provide feedback or ask questions and may or may not understand the sender's message. Active listening includes responses that demonstrate that you understand what the other person is trying to tell you about his or her experience.
