3. . What is the maximum number of electrons that can be placed in the P orbitals of an atom.


✒️ATOMIC ORBITALS

[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Solution:}}}[/tex]

The problem asks to find the maximum number of electrons in the p-orbitals of an atom. First, we need to find the number of p-orbitals. The quantum number l for a p-orbital is 1. The number of p-orbitals is

no. of orbitals = 2l + 1

no. of orbitals = 2(1) + 1

no. of orbitals = 3

Finally, we can now solve for the maximum number of electrons in a p-orbital. Therefore, the maximum number of electrons in a p-orbital is

max. no. of electrons = 2 × no. of orbitals

max. no. of electrons = 2 × 3

[tex]\boxed{\text{max. no. of electrons = 6}}[/tex]
