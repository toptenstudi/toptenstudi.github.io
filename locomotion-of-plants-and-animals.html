3. what does animal locomotion mean?​


Answer:

Animal locomotion, in ethology, is any of a variety of methods that animals use to move from one place to another. Some modes of locomotion are self-propelled, running, swimming, jumping, flying, hopping, soaring, and gliding.

What does animal locomotion mean?​

In ethology, animal locomotion refers to a number of strategies that animals employ to get from one location to another.[1] Some means of locomotion, such as running, swimming, jumping, flying, hopping, soaring, and gliding, are (originally) self-propelled. Many animal species rely on their surroundings for transportation, a sort of mobility known as passive locomotion, which includes activities such as sailing (certain jellyfish), kiting (spiders), rolling (some beetles and spiders), and riding other creatures (phoresis).

Animals travel for a number of reasons, including finding food, finding a mate, finding a suitable microhabitat, and avoiding predators. Natural selection has altered the locomotion strategies and mechanisms utilized by moving species since the capacity to move is crucial for survival for many animals. For example, migratory animals that travel long distances (such as the Arctic tern) have a locomotion mechanism that uses very little energy per unit distance, whereas non-migratory animals that must move quickly to avoid predators are likely to have a locomotion mechanism that uses a lot of energy but moves very quickly.
