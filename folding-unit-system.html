11. Starts with the big square from a piece of paper. Assume the area of the square is 64 square units. Fold the four corners to the center of the square and find the area of the resulting square. Repeat the process three times. 1) What is the area of the square formed after the first fold? Second fold? Third fold? 2) Is there a pattern in the areas obtained after 3 folds? 3) You have generated a sequence of areas. What are the first 3 terms of the sequence? 4) Is the sequence an arithmetic sequence? Why? 5) Using the pattern in the areas, what would be the 6th term of the sequence?​


HINTS:

Recall the area of a square having the side s.

[tex]\sf A=s^2[/tex]

Also, recall the Pythagorean theorem to determine the side of a triangle.

[tex]\sf c^2=a^2+b^2[/tex]

where c is the hypotenuse and a and b are other legs of the triangle.

ANALYSIS: (See the visual for your guide.)

Step 1: We know that the area of original size of the square is 64 square units. Determine its side using the formula of a square.

[tex]\sf A_1=(s_1)^2\\s_1=\sqrt{A_1}= \sqrt{64}=8\;\;units[/tex]

Step 2: Fold the four corners to the center of the square. After folding, we see that the four (isosceles) triangles are inscribed and fit in a new square. One triangle has two equal sides of 4 units, which comes from the half of the original side. So, we need to determine the other side of the triangle, or we call it as a side of the new square after the first fold. Using the Pythagorean theorem, we get

[tex]\sf s_2=\sqrt{(s_1/2)^2+(s_1/2)^2}\\\text{}\hspace{8}= \sqrt{4^2+4^2}\\\text{}\hspace{8}= \sqrt{32}\\\text{}\hspace{8}= 4\sqrt{2}\;\;units[/tex]

and the area after the first fold is

[tex]\sf A_2=(s_2)^2=(4\sqrt{2} )^2=32\;\;square\;units[/tex]

Step 3: For the second fold, it is same pattern from Step 2. Each triangle has two equal sides of 2 square root of 2 units, which is the half of the side from first fold. Using again the Pythagorean theorem to find the other side,

[tex]\sf s_3=\sqrt{(s_2/2)^2+(s_2/2)^2}\\\text{}\hspace{8}= \sqrt{(2\sqrt{2} )^2+(2\sqrt{2} )^2}\\\text{}\hspace{8}=\sqrt{8+8}\\\text{}\hspace{8}=\sqrt{16}\\\text{}\hspace{8}=4\;\;units[/tex]

and the area after the second fold is

[tex]\sf A_3=(s_3)^2=4^2=16\;\;square\;units[/tex]

Step 4: For the third fold, this is the same process from Step 3. Each triangle has two equal sides of 2 units, which is the half of the side from second fold. So, the side of a new square after folding by using again the Pythagorean is

[tex]\sf s_4=\sqrt{(s_3/2)^2+(s_3/2)^2}\\\text{}\hspace{8}= \sqrt{2^2+2^2}\\\text{}\hspace{8}=\sqrt{4+4}\\\text{}\hspace{8}=\sqrt{8}\\\text{}\hspace{8}=2\sqrt{2} \;\;units[/tex]

and the area after the third fold is

[tex]\sf A_4=(s_4)^2=(2\sqrt{2} )^2=8\;\;square\;units[/tex]

QUESTION AND ANSWER:

1. What is the area of the square formed after the first fold? Second fold? Third fold?

The area after the first fold is 32 square units. The area after the second fold is 16 square units. The area after the third fold is 8 square units.

2. Is there a pattern in the areas obtained after 3 folds?

Yes, every area of the resulting square is reduced by 1/2.

3. You have generated a sequence of areas. What are the first 3 terms of the sequence?

The first term is 64 before the folding happens. Then, the second term is 32 and the third term is 16. By writing the sequence: 64, 32, 16.

4. Is the sequence an arithmetic sequence? Why?

No, because the difference between the two consecutive terms of the sequence is NOT constant.

5. Using the pattern in the areas, what would be the 6th term of the sequence?​

To continue the sequence since we know that every area of the resulting square is reduced by 1/2. The fourth term is 8,  the fifth term is 4, and the sixth term is 2.
