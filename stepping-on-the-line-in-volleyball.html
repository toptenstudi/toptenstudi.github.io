4. if you are to organize a volleyball event what are the steps that you will prepare​


Answer:

The anticipation of a tournament may be intense. They probably can't wait to get out there and compete. So how do you prepare your little athlete for the big day? That's exactly what we'll be discussing today on the Rocky Mount Event Center blog.

Always PRACTICE  

The team will have many practices leading up to the event, so make sure they attend every one and follow their coach's instructions. While this may seem simple, practice is key to success.

NUTRITION

You should help your child eat healthy all year, but prior to a tournament, specific nutrition planning can improve athletic performance. First, keep them hydrated by drinking plenty of water. You'll want them to eat lots of carbs in the days leading up to the competition. Avoid sugary and fatty foods

If they are playing early games on the day of the tournament, keep their breakfast light. If they have till the afternoon, a larger breakfast with modest snacks should be plenty. SLEEP

Assure them of a good night's sleep. Sleeping 8 hours is great. Too little sleep and they'll be tired for the game, too much sleep and they'll be foggy.

MENTAL PREP

The buildup to a game can be nerve-wracking. Relax and get into the correct mindset. Encourage them to dance, walk, or picture their triumph. These strategies can help your athlete focus and prepare for the game.

DEWARMER

Get them to the game when their coach requests it. They'll adequately warm them up before the game.

11. identifying the different lines of volleyball?​


Answer:

The volleyball court lines include four sidelines, two endlines, two attack lines and a centerline which mark the boundaries of a volleyball court playing area.

Sideline – The two lines that run the length of the court are called “sidelines”. They are a total of 60 feet long and line up with the antennas that designate the side boundaries of the court. A server must serve from between these lines.

Two endlines that are perpendicular to these sidelines that run parallel to each other, while also serving as the two service lines.

The Attack line is the line that separates the front court (the area near the net) and the back court (the area between the attack line and end line). The Service area is the area beyond the service line where players serve the ball.

The center line separates one volleyball team's court from the other. The centerline measures two inches wide and goes from sideline to sideline which divides the two playing courts into two 29 foot 6 inch halves where the teams play, one on one side and the opposing team on the other.

19. As a future PE educator, what are the steps to be followed in order for your learners to perform better and improve their volleyball skills? And how can you address those steps, especially to those students who are not into volleyball?​

Explanation:

Well, after 34 years of coaching, playing and refereeing VB you might say I am kind of deep in the culture ;-)

Values (outlooks, viewpoints…whatever you want to call them):

Teamwork. Teams always defeat individuals.

Take responsibility for your own mistakes. When you whack it into the net, shank it into the bleachers or watch it drop in front of you because you didn’t take charge, don’t be looking around for someone to blame.

Hard work precedes all success in sports. VB is a game of repetition. Moving your feet so every pass is perfect. Hustling to defensive position so you are waiting for the ball to arrive. Getting a good approach. All these things take thousands of reps before they start happening automatically. Put in the time if you plan to be great.

Perfection is a wonderful goal and a horrible expectation. Learn to give yourself a break.

You can control the future but you can’t control the past. Let go of your mistakes because the ball is coming to you again and it needs your full attention.

When your coach coaches you, don’t give an excuse why you did something wrong…Listen and then ask a question if you don’t understand what your coach wants you to do right.

Treat your teammates the same way you would want them to treat you after committing an error.

When you are tired at practice, that’s when you learn the most about yourself as an athlete.
