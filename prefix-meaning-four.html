3. example of prefixes and the meaning of the prefix​


Answer:

A short list of prefixes:

Prefix\Examples

de-  decode, decrease

dis-  disagree, disappear

ex-  exhale, explosion

il-  illegal, illogical

im-  impossible, improper

in-  inaction, invisible

mis-  mislead, misplace

non- nonfiction, nonsense

pre- prefix, prehistory

pro- proactive, profess, program

re- react, reappear

un- undo, unequal, unusual

Explanation:

A prefix is a group of letters placed before the root of a word. For example, the word “unhappy” consists of the prefix “un-” [which means “not”] combined with the root (or stem) word “happy”; the word “unhappy” means “not happy.”

SUFFIX

A suffix is a letter or group of letters, for example '-ly' or '- ness', which is added to the end of a word in order to form a different word, often of a different word class. For example, the suffix '-ly' is added to ' quick' to form 'quickly'. Compare affix and , prefix.

PREFIX

A prefix is a letter or group of letters, for example 'un-' or 'multi-', which is added to the beginning of a word in order to form a different word. For example, the prefix 'un-' is added to ' happy' to form 'unhappy'. Compare affix and , suffix.

26. the prefix exo-means "out of" and the prefix endo-means "taking in"? how do these meaning relate to the meaning of the endocytosis and exocytosis?​


Answer:

The prefix "exo" which means "out of" can be related to the meaning of exocytosis, because exocytosis is the process where the vesicles fuse with the plasma membrane to draw out large molecules from the cell; Thus means that they transports substances outside of the cell.

The prefix "endo" which means "taking in" can be related to the meaning of endocytosis, because endocytosis is the process where the cell membrane either engulfs the substance or creates a pathway for dissolved nutrients to enter the cell; Thus means that they bring substances in the cell.

Explanation:

Endocytosis and exocytosis are two types of bulk transportation mechanisms, which transport big particles through the plasma membrane, either from cell to the external environment or from the external environment to the cell. Both these mechanisms form membrane-bound vesicles as the mean of transportation.
