1. What is meaning tangkulu?


Answer

The Tangkulu is a cloth worn as a headkerchief by a prominent person in the Bagobo tribe. It is a status symbol that represents distinction and power that is only worn by the Bagani, Datu, and the Mabalian. A Tangkulu is decorated and dye-colored by the Mabalian of the village. The colors used in a Tangkulu varies from chocolate brown to blood-red and usually represent the numbers of enemies slain in battle.

Additional Information

Bagani - is the warrior class of the Bagobos. They need to kill at least two enemies before they can wear the Tangkulu

Datu -  is a leader of the village and is the judge when he is settling disputes within the settlement. A Datu may be a former Bagani Warrior that has achieved plenty of enemy kills.

Mabalian - Are the spiritual leaders of the Bagobos. They are mostly females and are skilled weavers of the village. Hence, the role of making the Tangkulu is given to them.
