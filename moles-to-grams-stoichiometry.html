1. Stoichiometry: How many moles of Fe2O3 are needed to make 6.4 moles of H2O?​


StoichiometryDiscussion:

Stoichiometry is the quantitative study of reactants and products in a chemical reaction.

Whether the units given for the reactants (or products) are moles, grams, liters (for gases), or some other units, we use moles to calculate the amount of product formed in a reaction. This approach is called the mole method, which means simply that the stoichiometric coefficients in a chemical equation can be interpreted as the number of moles of each substance. For example, industrial ammonia is synthesized from hydrogen and nitrogen as follows:

[tex]\text{N}_{2}(g) + 3\text{H}_{2}(g) → 2\text{NH}_{3}(g)[/tex]

The stoichiometric coefficients show that one molecule of [tex]\text{N}_{2}[/tex] reacts with three molecules of [tex]\text{H}_{2}[/tex] to form two molecules of [tex]\text{NH}_{3}[/tex]. It follows that the relative numbers of moles are the same as the relative number of molecules:

[tex]\begin{array}{ccccc} \text{N}_{2} & + & 3\text{H}_{2} & → & 2\text{NH}_{3} \\ \text{1 molecule} & & \text{3 molecules} & & \text{2 molecules} \\ 6.022 × 10^{23} \: \text{molecules} & & 3(6.022 × 10^{23}) \: \text{molecules} & & 2(6.022 × 10^{23}) \: \text{molecules} \\ \text{1 mol} & & \text{3 mol} & & \text{2 mol} \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Thus, this equation can also be read as "1 mole of [tex]\text{N}_{2}[/tex] gas combines with 3 moles of [tex]\text{H}_{2}[/tex] gas to form 2 moles of [tex]\text{NH}_{3}[/tex] gas." In stoichiometric calculations, we say that three moles of [tex]\text{H}_{2}[/tex] are equivalent to two moles of [tex]\text{NH}_{3}[/tex], that is,

[tex]\text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_{2} \bumpeq \text{2 mol} \: \text{NH}_{3}[/tex]

where the symbol [tex]\bumpeq[/tex] means "stoichiometrically equivalent to" or simply "equivalent to." This relationship enables us to write the conversion factors

[tex]\frac{\text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_{2}}{\text{2 mol} \: \text{NH}_{3}} \: \text{and} \: \frac{\text{2 mol} \: \text{NH}_{3}}{\text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_{2}}[/tex]

Similarly, we have [tex]\text{1 mol} \: \text{N}_{2} \bumpeq \text{2 mol} \: \text{NH}_{3} \: \text{and} \: \text{1 mol} \: \text{N}_{2} \bumpeq \text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_{2}[/tex].

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

How many moles of Fe2O3 are needed to make 6.4 moles of H2O?

Given:

[tex]\text{moles of} \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O} = \text{6.4 mol}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\text{moles of} \: \text{Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

Since the balanced chemical equation is not given, we will assume the chemical equation involving Fe₂O₃ and H₂O. The assumed balanced chemical equation is

Fe₂O₃ + 3H₂ → 2Fe + 3H₂O

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of Fe₂O₃ needed by using dimensional analysis (factor-label method).

Since [tex]\text{1 mol} \: \text{Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} \bumpeq \text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}[/tex], the number of moles of Fe₂O₃ needed is

[tex]\text{moles of} \: \text{Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} = \text{6.4 mol} \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O} × \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3}}{\text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of} \: \text{Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} = \text{2.13 mol}}[/tex]

6. Using of mole fraction calculate the mole fraction of alcohol C2H5OH and water in mixture of 9.2 grams of alcohol in 18 grams of water. ​


Solution:

First, we calculate the number of moles of C₂H₅OH and H₂O. The number of moles of C₂H₅OH is

[tex]n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = 9.20 \: g \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH} \times \frac{1 \: mol \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}}{46.068 \: g \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = 0.19970 \: mol[/tex]

and the number of moles of H₂O is

[tex]n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 18.0 \: g \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O} \times \frac{1 \: mol \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}{18.016 \: g \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 0.99911 \: mol[/tex]

Next, we calculate the total number of moles. We write

[tex]n_{T} = n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} + n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{T} = 0.19970 \: mol + 0.99911 \: mol[/tex]

[tex]n_{T} = 1.19881 \: mol[/tex]

Finally, we can calculate the mole fractions of C₂H₅OH and H₂O. The mole fraction of C₂H₅OH is

[tex]X_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = \frac{n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}}}{n_{T}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = \frac{0.19970 \: mol}{1.19881 \: mol}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{X_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = 0.167}[/tex]

and the mole fraction of H₂O is

[tex]X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \frac{n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}}{n_{T}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \frac{0.99911 \: mol}{1.19881 \: mol}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 0.833}[/tex]

8. Mole gram Find the mass of 2.5 moles of caffeine (C8H10N4O2)​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of caffeine.

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 8) + (1.008 g/mol × 10) + (14.01 g/mol × 4) + (16.00 g/mol × 2)

molar mass = 194.2 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of caffeine.

[tex]\text{mass of caffeine = 2.5 mol caffeine} × \frac{\text{194.2 g caffeine}}{\text{1 mol caffeine}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of caffeine = 490 g}}[/tex]

28. Directions:Define the following terms.1. Solution2. Solubility3. Stoichiometry4. Moles5. Mole Fraction6. Molality​


Answer:

In chemistry, a solution is a special type of homogeneous mixture composed of two or more substances. In such a mixture, a solute is a substance dissolved in another substance, known as a solvent.

Solubility, degree to which a substance dissolves in a solvent to make a solution (usually expressed as grams of solute per litre of solvent). Solubility of one fluid (liquid or gas) in another may be complete (totally miscible; e.g., methanol and water) or partial (oil and water dissolve only slightly).

Stoichiometry is a section of chemistry that involves using relationships between reactants and/or products in a chemical reaction to determine desired quantitative data. In Greek, stoikhein means element and metron means measure, so stoichiometry literally translated means the measure of elements

Mole, also spelled mol, in chemistry, a standard scientific unit for measuring large quantities of very small entities such as atoms, molecules, or other specified particles.

In chemistry, the mole fraction or molar fraction is defined as unit of the amount of a constituent, nᵢ divided by the total amount of all constituents in a mixture, nₜₒₜ:.This expression is given

Molality is a measure of number of moles of solute present in 1 kg of solvent. This contrasts with the definition of molarity which is based on a specified volume of solution. A commonly used unit for molality in chemistry is mol/kg.
