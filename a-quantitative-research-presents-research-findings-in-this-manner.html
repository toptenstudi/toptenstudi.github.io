12. presenting the results in quantitative research, researchers commonly use numbers and statistics.​


Presenting the results in quantitative research, researchers commonly use numbers and statistics.​ The results of quantitative research are presented in the form of mathematical calculation results. The result of the calculation is considered as a confirmed fact.

EXPLANATION:

Quantitative research is usually widely used in psychology, economics, demography, sociology, marketing, health, society and human development and others. Less commonly used in anthropology and history, research in mathematical sciences such as physics is also included in quantitative research although the use of the term differs in context. This research method is defined as part of a series of systematic investigations of phenomena by collecting data to then be measured by mathematical or computational statistical techniques. This research is mostly carried out using statistical methods in collecting quantitative data through research studies.

Advantages of Quantitative Method

Similar to other research methods, quantitative research methods also have advantages and disadvantages. Below will be explained further about the advantages and disadvantages of quantitative research methods.

Data Type

Numerical and statistical

Data Representation of Quantitative Research

The results of quantitative research are presented in the form of mathematical calculation results. The result of the calculation is considered as a confirmed fact. The validity of quantitative research is largely determined by the validity and reliability of the instruments used.

Implications of Research Results

The results of quantitative research are in the form of generalized facts or theories. Whenever and wherever, that fact applies.

This is why quantitative research is described as objective research in contrast to qualitative research that is subjective.
