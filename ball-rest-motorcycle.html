Let’s begin with a particle with an acceleration a(t) is a known function of time. Since the time derivative of the velocity function is acceleration,

\[\frac{d}{dt}v(t)=a(t),\]

we can take the indefinite integral of both sides, finding

\[\int \frac{d}{dt}v(t)dt=\int a(t)dt+{C}_{1},\]

where C1 is a constant of integration. Since

11. A motorcycle at rest can reach 50 km/h in just 10s . Find its acceleration ​


The formula for acceleration is:

a = (v_f - v_i) / t

where:

a = acceleration

v_f = final velocity

v_i = initial velocity

t = time

In this problem, the motorcycle is initially at rest, so v_i = 0 km/h. The final velocity is 50 km/h, and the time it takes to reach that velocity is 10 seconds. Substituting these values into the formula, we get:

a = (50 km/h - 0 km/h) / 10 s

a = 5 km/h/s

Therefore, the acceleration of the motorcycle is 5 km/h/s.

27. A motorcycle accelerates from rest at 12 m/s in 3 sec on the level road. if the force is arround 4000N what is the total mass of the cyclist and motorcycle - With given and solution plsss​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The force acting on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F = ma}[/tex]

where:

F = force

m = mass

a = acceleration

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for a

[tex]a = \dfrac{v_{f} - v_{i}}{t}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{\text{12 m/s} - 0}{\text{3 s}}[/tex]

a = 4 m/s²

Solving for m

F = ma

[tex]\dfrac{F}{a} = \dfrac{ma}{a}[/tex]

[tex]m = \dfrac{F}{a}[/tex]

[tex]m = \dfrac{\text{4,000 N}}{\text{4 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{m = \text{1,000 kg}}[/tex]

Therefore, the total mass of the cyclist and motorcycle is 1,000 kg.
