The possessive of a plural noun is formed by adding only an apostrophe when the noun ends in s, and by adding both an apostrophe and s when it ends in a letter other than s.

26. what is the connection of plural noun to possessive noun​


Answer:

A noun is a part of speech that names a person, place, thing, action, feeling or ideal. Possessive nouns show ownership. Plural nouns show a quantity of more than one. Plural possessive nouns incorporate the concepts of ownership and "more than one."

28. write the plural possessive for the singula​


Answer:

Words with irregular plurals that don't end in "s" are made possessive as if they were singular; that is, by adding an apostrophe-S. "There was a line to the men's room at the children's choral concert." Both "men" and "children" are plural, but they take an apostrophe-S as if they were singular.
