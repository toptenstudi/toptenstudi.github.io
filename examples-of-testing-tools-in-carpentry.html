2. what is the purpose of testing tools in carpentry​


Answer:

Testing tools in carpentry are used to ensure the accuracy, precision, and quality of the finished product. These tools are designed to help carpenters measure, mark, cut, and join pieces of wood or other materials with the utmost precision.

Answer:

Testing tools are used in carpentry to ensure that the work is accurate after sawing, cutting, and smoothing of carpentry projects. Some examples of testing tools used in carpentry include straight edge, winding sticks, plumb bob, spirit level, try square, tape measure, framing square, level, short rule, and the try square.

13. give 5 examples of carpentry tools which are wedges​


Here are five examples of carpentry tools that are wedges:

1. Axe - The blade of an axe is a wedge shape, designed to efficiently split wood.

2. Chisel - A chisel is a tool with a sharp edge used for carving or cutting wood. The blade of a chisel is wedge-shaped to allow for precise cutting.

3. Splitting Maul - A splitting maul is a heavy, long-handled hammer used for splitting wood. The head of the splitting maul is wedge-shaped, designed to force wood apart.

4. Pry Bar - A pry bar is used for prying apart or lifting objects. The end of a pry bar is often wedge-shaped to allow for easy insertion and maximum leverage.

5. Shims - Shims are thin, wedge-shaped pieces of wood or plastic used to fill gaps or level surfaces. They are commonly used in carpentry to ensure that surfaces are level and flush.

If you only plan to use a tool once or twice, you might consider renting, or buying a less expensive tool. Harbor Freight is a great place to find inexpensive tools. If you know that you will be using a tool for a long time and on multiple projects, purchasing a high quality professional grade tool probably makes the most sense.
