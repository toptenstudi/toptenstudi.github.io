2. definition on festival dances


Answer:

Festival dance is a cultural dance performed to the strong beats of percussion instruments by a community of people who share the same culture, generally performed to honor the patron saint or as a thank-you for the rich harvest. Festival dance can be religious or secular in nature.

Explanation:

Festival dances are a type of dance that are typically performed during annual festivals or celebrations. These dances often have their roots in traditional cultural practices and beliefs, and they often involve synchronized movements and complex choreography. Festival dances can be performed solo or in groups, and they can range from simple steps to intricate sequences that incorporate elaborate costumes, props, and music. Some examples of festival dances include the Bhangra of Punjab, the Laona of Assam, and the Ghumar of Rajasthan in India, and the Carnival celebrations in Venice, Italy. These dances play an important role in documenting and preserving local cultural heritage and tradition, and they also serve as a means of social integration and community building.

DEFINITION:Abando Fertility Rite - The Obando Fertility Rites are a dance ritual, initially an Anitist ritual, and later also became a Catholic festival celebrated every May in Obando, Bulacan, Philippines.

Ati-Atihan Festival - The Ati-Atihan Festival is a Philippine festival held annually in January in honor of the Santo Niño in several towns of the province of Aklan, Panay Island.

Dinagyang Festival - Dinagyang is a Hiligaynon word that means “merrymaking.” Just like the Sinulog and Ati-Atihan Festivals, Dinagyang Festival is one of the religious festivities in the Philippines that celebrates the feast of the Santo Niño and the pact between the Datus and locals.

Santacruzan - Flores de Mayo is a festival held in the Philippines in the month of May. It is one of the May devotions to the Blessed Virgin Mary and lasts for the entire year. The Santacruzan is the ritual pageant held on the last day of the Flores de Mayo

Sinulog Festival - The Sinulog-Santo Niño Festival is an annual cultural and religious festival held on the third Sunday of January in Cebu City, and on the fourth Sunday of January in Carmen, Cebu, and is the centre of the Santo Niño Catholic celebrations in the Philippines
