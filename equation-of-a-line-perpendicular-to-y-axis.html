14. Find the equation of the line passing through the point (2, 3) and perpendicular to the line y = 2x + 1.


Answer:

Answer: y = (-1/2)x + 4

Explanation: We can use the slope formula to find the slope of the line and then use the point-slope form to write the equation of the line.

Answer:

[tex]\rm{y=-\dfrac{1}{2}x+4}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Hi there!

Given information:

Point (2,3)Equation y=2x+1The given line and the unknown one are perpendicular to each other

Note(s):

If two lines are perpendicular to one another, then their slopes are negative inverses of each other.

Since the slope of the line y = 2x + 1 is 2, the slope of the line that's perpendicular to it would be -1/2.

Now we have both the slope & the point, so we substitute the values into a formula that is commonly known as Point-Slope:

[tex]\rm{y-y_1=m(x-x_1)}[/tex]

Substitute the givens:

[tex]\rm{y-3=-1/2(x-2)}[/tex]

That would be our equation in Point-Slope form. But what about Slope-Intercept?

This equation form looks like this:

[tex]\rm{y=mx+b}[/tex]

So how do we simplify the equation [tex]\rm{y-3=-1/2(x-2)}[/tex] so that it matches the form [tex]\rm{y=mx+b}[/tex]? Well first, why not use the distributive property on the right side of the equation? That operation would result in:

[tex]\rm{y-3=-1/2x+1}[/tex]

Next I add 3 to both sides.

[tex]\rm{y=-1/2x+1+3}[/tex][tex]\rm{y=-1/2x+4}[/tex]

Therefore the result is as follows:

[tex]\rm{y=-\displaystyle\frac{1}{2} x+4}[/tex]
