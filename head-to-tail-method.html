Answer:

The head-to-tail method of adding vectors involves drawing the first vector on a graph and then placing the tail of each subsequent vector at the head of the previous vector. The resultant vector is then drawn from the tail of the first vector to the head of the final vector.

12. The head of a crocodile is half a meter; the tail is as long as the head plus 1/2 the body; the body is as long as the head and tail combined. Find the length of the crocodile.


The length of the head (h) = 0.5 m.

The tail (t) = h + (1/2)b = 0.5 + (1/2)b.

The body (b) = h + t = 0.5 + t.

We have two equations, t = 0.5 + (1/2)b and b = 0.5 + t. We substitute the first equation to the second to find b, b = 0.5 + 0.5 + (1/2)b = 1 + (1/2)b --> b - (1/2)b = 1 --> (1/2)b = 1 --> b = 2. For t, we have: t = 0.5 + (1/2)(2) = 0.5 + 1 = 1.5.
