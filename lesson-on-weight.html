1. It is a force exerted by a massive object on another nea pulling the other object toward its center.

The force being described is gravity. Gravity is a force of attraction that exists between any two objects in the universe. It is a force exerted by a massive object on another object, pulling the other object toward its center. The strength of gravity depends on the masses of the objects and the distance between them. The greater the mass of an object, the greater its gravitational force, and the greater the distance between two objects, the weaker their gravitational force.

2. An intrinsic property of a body that tells the amount of present in the body.

The intrinsic property of a body that tells the amount of matter present in the body is called its mass. Mass is a fundamental property of matter and is defined as the amount of matter in an object. It is an intrinsic property because it does not depend on the object's location or the forces acting on it. Mass is usually measured in units of kilograms (kg) or grams (g).

3. A factor of weight that varies from location to location.

The factor of weight that varies from location to location is the gravitational acceleration, which is denoted by the letter "g". Gravitational acceleration is a measure of the acceleration due to gravity that is experienced by an object in a particular location. It varies depending on the location, as it is dependent on the mass and distance of the objects that are attracting each other. On the surface of the Earth, the standard value for gravitational acceleration is approximately 9.81 meters per second squared (m/s^2). However, this value may vary slightly depending on the altitude, latitude, and local geological conditions of a particular location.

4.A factor of weight that is independent of location.

The factor of weight that is independent of location is the mass of an object. Mass is a fundamental property of matter that tells us the amount of matter present in an object, and it is an intrinsic property that is independent of location or environment. The weight of an object, on the other hand, is the force exerted on the object due to gravity and is dependent on the gravitational field strength of the location where the object is situated. Therefore, while the weight of an object may vary depending on its location, the mass of the object remains the same regardless of where it is located. Mass is usually measured in units of kilograms (kg) or grams (g).
