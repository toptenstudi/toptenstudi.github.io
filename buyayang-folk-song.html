1. Costumes: The dancers wear colorful and intricate traditional Kalinga attire, which includes a headgear, a sleeveless top, a skirt, and leg wraps. The costumes are usually adorned with beads, shells, and other indigenous materials.

2. Music: The dance is accompanied by indigenous Kalinga music, which features the sounds of bamboo instruments, such as the tongatong and the kulkul.

3. Movements: The dance involves a series of intricate and synchronized movements, which are characterized by graceful arm and hand gestures, fluid hip movements, and intricate footwork. The dancers move in a circular formation, imitating the swaying movements of rice stalks in the field.

4. Storytelling: Bayayang Buyayang is also a storytelling dance, which depicts the daily life and traditions of the Kalinga people. The dance tells the story of the rice cycle, from planting to harvesting, and celebrates the importance of rice in the Kalinga culture.

5. Community spirit: The dance is usually performed by a group of dancers, emphasizing the importance of community spirit and cooperation in the Kalinga culture. The dancers move in unison, symbolizing the unity and harmony of the community.
