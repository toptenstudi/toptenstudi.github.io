15. 1. What is the meaning of PAGASA? 2. How many typhoon signal numbers are there in total? 3. What is the speed of the lowest signal number? Speed of the highest signal? 4. What is the category (name) of the storm if its: a. Signal #1 b. Signal #2 c. Signal #3 d. Signal #4 e. Signal #5


Answer:

PAGASA stands for "Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical, and Astronomical Services Administration." It is the national meteorological and hydrological agency of the Philippines.

There are four typhoon signal numbers in total, which are issued by PAGASA to warn the public of incoming typhoons or tropical cyclones.

The speed of the lowest signal number, which is Signal No. 1, is 30-60 kilometers per hour (kph). The speed of the highest signal number, which is Signal No. 4, is 171-220 kph.

The category (name) of the storm depends on its maximum sustained winds, as follows:

a. Signal #1: Tropical depression (maximum sustained winds of up to 61 kph)

b. Signal #2: Tropical storm (maximum sustained winds of 62-88 kph)

c. Signal #3: Typhoon (maximum sustained winds of 89-117 kph)

d. Signal #4: Severe typhoon (maximum sustained winds of 118-220 kph)

e. Signal #5: Super typhoon (maximum sustained winds of more than 220 kph)
