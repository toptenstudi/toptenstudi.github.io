3. Livin A. What is a Ecosystem?​


Question:

What is a Ecosystem?

Answer:Ecosystem

An ecosystem is a geographic area where plants, animals, and other organisms, as well as weather and landscape, work together to form a bubble of life. Ecosystems contain biotic or living, parts, as well as abiotic factors, or nonliving parts.

a biological community of interacting organisms and their physical environment."the marine ecosystem of the northern Gulf had suffered irreparable damage"(in general use) a complex network or interconnected system."Silicon Valley's entrepreneurial ecosystem"

#CarryOnLearning

ECOSYSTEM

Ecosystem  a group or gathering of living organic entities that live in and collaborate with one another in a particular climate.

example of Ecosystem, tropical backwoods are environments comprised of living creatures, for example, trees, plants, creatures, bugs and miniature organic entities that are in consistent connection among themselves and that are impacted by other physical (sun, temperature) or synthetic (oxygen or supplements) parts.

Ecosystem can be just about as little as a desert spring in a desert, or as large as a sea, traversing huge number of miles. There are two sorts of environment:

1. Earthbound Ecosystem

2. Sea-going Ecosystem

ANSWERS

Planet Earth is very unique compared to any other planets in the Solar system, this is because it has the ability to cater and support life.

∆ The Earth has a very ideal distance from the Sun, not too near and not too far. It also has a hydrosphere and cryosphere, that keeps the planet drenched and wet, provides water for all living organisms and helps the weather systems around the planet working.

∆ Earth's subsystems, enable the planet to work effectively for all its living organims and planetary mechanisms, it has a thick atmosphere than can protect us from large chunks of interstellar bodies and dangerous cosmic and solar rays that can harm humans.
