The Genesis creation narrative is the creation myth[a] of both Judaism and Christianity.[1] The narrative is made up of two stories, roughly equivalent to the first two chapters of the Book of Genesis. 18. Why is the seventh day or creation for rest and worship important to us as children of God?


IMPORTANCE OF THE SEVENTH DAY CREATION

The seventh day or creation for rest and worship is important to us as a children of God because it is the time for God. It is the time that all people will worship him.   During the time of God in this earth. God rested not on the grounds that he was worn out, but since he had "finished His work" (Genesis 2:2). God, who is endless in power, scope, information, and so on, doesn't have to rest from weakness. Being worn out is a component of natural organic entities, and God is certainly not an organic living being. God is portrayed in the Bible as being soul (John 4:24), and soul doesn't have tissue and bones (Luke 24:39). Accordingly, God isn't naturally based. Moreover, God is interminable in both the past and the future (Psalm 90:2), and he is the lone God (Isaiah 43:10; 44:6, 8; 45:5). This implies that God is unceasingly existent in an independent way.

19. what is sabbath day and why is it important to us as a creation of GOD​


Answer:

The sabbath day is a day dedicated solely for rest. It is a commemoration of the rest God did on the seventh day when He created the world. It is important to us because it is also the day that we should set for worshipping God as our Father and Creator.

Answer:

The Sabbath is the seventh day of the week (Saturday) as what the scriptures said.

"Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy. Six days shalt thou labor and do all thy work. But the seventh day is the sabbath of the Lord thy God..."

(Exodus 20:8-10)

"Lembra-te do da do sabado, para o santificar." (Portuguese Bible, Exodus 20:8)

The sabbath is important to us because it reminds us that we are a product of God's creation. God created the world and everything on it including us, and on the seventh day, He rested, He blessed the seventh day and sanctified it because it is the day that he rested from all his work. (Genesis 2:1-3)

The Sabbath is the fourth commandment of God in the Ten Commandments. He wants us to remember the day He rested when He created the world. As God's creation, we should obey His commandments since it is the whole duty of man.

"Let us hear the conclusion of the whole matter: Fear God, and keep His commandments: for this is the whole duty of man." (Ecc. 12:13)
