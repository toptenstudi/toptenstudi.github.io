27. How many different committees with 4 members can be formed from a group with 7 seniors and 6 juniors if: a. they are all seniors? b. there are 3 seniors and 1 junior? c. there are equal number of seniors and juniors in each committee?​


✒️COMBINATIONS

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWERS}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad\Large a) \ \rm 35 \ ways [/tex]

[tex] \qquad\Large b) \ \rm 210 \ ways [/tex]

[tex] \qquad\Large c) \ \rm 315 \ ways [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTIONS}:} [/tex]

LETTER A:

» Find the number of combinations of 7 seniors taken 4 at a time to be the members in a committee.

[tex] \begin{aligned} & \bold{\color{lightblue}Formula:} \\ &\quad\boxed{\: \rm _nC_r = \frac{n! }{r!(n-r)! } \:} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex] _nC_r = \frac{7! }{4!(7-4)! } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_4 = \frac{7! }{4! \ 3! } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_4 = \frac{7 \cdot 6 \cdot 5 \cdot \cancel{4!}}{\cancel{4!} \cdot 3 \cdot 2 } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_4= \frac{7 \cdot \cancel6 \cdot 5}{\cancel3 \cdot \cancel2 } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_4 = 7\cdot 5[/tex]

[tex] _7C_4 = 35[/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] There are 35 ways in which all senior the member of the committee.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

LETTER B.

» First, find the number of combinations of 7 seniors taken 3 at a time to be the members in a committee

[tex] _7C_3 = \frac{7! }{3!(7-3)! } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_3 = \frac{7! }{3! \ 4! } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_3 = \frac{7 \cdot 6 \cdot 5 \cdot \cancel{4!}}{3 \cdot 2 \cdot \cancel{4!} } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_3 = \frac{7 \cdot \cancel6 \cdot 5}{\cancel3 \cdot \cancel2 } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_3 = 7 \cdot 5[/tex]

[tex] _7C_3 = 35[/tex]

» Then, find the number of combinations of 6 juniors taken 1 at a time to be the members in a committee.

[tex] _6C_1 = \frac{6! }{1!(6-1)! } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _6C_1 = \frac{6! }{1! \ 5! } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _6C_1 = \frac{6 \cdot \cancel{5!} }{1 \cdot \cancel {5!} } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _6C_1 = \frac{6}{1}\\ [/tex]

[tex] _6C_1 = 6 \\ [/tex]

» By applying the Fundamental Principles of Counting. Find the number of combinations if 3 seniors and a junior can be part of the committee.

[tex] FCP = \ _7C_3 \cdot \ _6C_1 [/tex]

[tex] FCP = 35 \cdot 6[/tex]

[tex] FCP = 210 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] There are 210 ways in which 3 seniors and a junior are the members of the committee.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

LETTER C:

» First, find the number of combinations of 7 seniors taken 2 at a time to be the members in a committee.

[tex] _7C_2 = \frac{7! }{2!(7-2)! } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_2 = \frac{7! }{2! \ 5! } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_2 = \frac{7 \cdot 6 \cdot \cancel{5!} }{2 \cdot \cancel{5!}} \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_2 = \frac{7 \cdot \cancel6 \ ^3 }{\cancel2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] _7C_2 = 7 \cdot 3[/tex]

[tex] _7C_2 = 21 [/tex]

» Then, find the number of combinations of 6 juniors taken 2 at a time to be the members in a committee.

[tex] _6C_2 = \frac{6! }{2!(6-2)! } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _6C_2 = \frac{6! }{2! \ 4! } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _6C_2 = \frac{6 \cdot 5 \cdot \cancel{4!} }{2 \cdot \cancel{4!} } \\ [/tex]

[tex] _6C_2 = \frac{\cancel6 \ ^3 \cdot 5 }{\cancel2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] _6C_2 = 3 \cdot 5[/tex]

[tex] _6C_2 = 15 [/tex]

» By applying the Fundamental Principles of Counting. Find the number of combinations if 2 seniors and 2 juniors can be part of the committee.

[tex] FCP = \ _7C_2 \cdot \ _6C_2 [/tex]

[tex] FCP = 21 \cdot 15 [/tex]

[tex] FCP = 315 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] There are 315 ways in which 2 seniors and 2 juniors are the members of the committee.
