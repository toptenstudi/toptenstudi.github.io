Understanding Acceleration
In physics, we will know acceleration or acceleration is an increase in speed in a certain unit of time.  The acceleration of an object is caused by forces acting on it, as explained in Newton's second law.
The SI unit for acceleration is meters per second squared (m/s²).  Acceleration is a vector quantity so it has both magnitude and direction.  As a vector, the net force is equal to the product of the object's mass (scalar size) and its acceleration.  In general, acceleration is considered as the motion of an object being accelerated or decelerated.  In other words, a rotating object (such as a rotating car) also has an acceleration.
Acceleration is also known as a vector quantity, which is a quantity that takes into account the direction of motion.  If the acceleration of an object is in the same direction as its speed, the greater the speed of the object, the faster the object is moving.  This type of acceleration is called positive acceleration.
On the other hand, if an object's acceleration is in the opposite direction to its velocity, the object's velocity will be less.  The movement of objects becomes slower.  This type of acceleration is called negative acceleration.  Negative acceleration is often called deceleration, while positive acceleration is often called acceleration.
