About Internal energy

The internal energy (E) is the total true kinetic energy and potential energy in the system. Therefore, the internal energy can be formulated by the equation E = Kinetic Energy + Potential energy

. However, because the amount of kinetic energy and potential energy in a system cannot be measured, therefore the amount of energy in a system cannot be determined, what can be determined is the amount of energy change in a system.

Changes in internal energy can be identified by measuring the heat (q) and work (w), which will occur when a system reacts. Therefore, the change in internal energy is formulated by the equation E = q - w.

If the system absorbs heat, then q is a positive value. If the system gives off heat, then q is a negative value.

If the system does work, then w in the formula is a positive value. If the system is subjected to work by the environment, then w is a negative value.
