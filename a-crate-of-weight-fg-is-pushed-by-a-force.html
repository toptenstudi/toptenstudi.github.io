15. A 2-kg crate is pushed with a net force of 20 N.


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The force acting on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F = ma}[/tex]

where:

F = force

m = mass

a = acceleration

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for a

F = ma

[tex]\dfrac{F}{m} = \dfrac{ma}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{F}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{\text{20 N}}{\text{2 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{a = \text{10 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

Therefore, the acceleration of the crate is 10 m/s².

The acceleration of an object is the rate at which its velocity changes over time. In this case, the crate is accelerating at 15.0 m/s2, which means that its velocity is changing at a rate of 15.0 m/s each second. We are also given the applied force of 35.0 N and the frictional force of 5.0 N. We can use this information to calculate the net force acting on the crate, which is the difference between the applied force and the frictional force. This gives us 35.0 N - 5.0 N = 30.0 N.

The net force acting on an object is equal to its mass times its acceleration. This means that if we know the net force acting on an object and its acceleration, we can calculate its mass. In this case, we know that the net force acting on the crate is 30.0 N and its acceleration is 15.0 m/s2. Therefore, we can calculate the mass of the crate using the equation F = ma, where F is the force, m is the mass, and a is the acceleration. This gives us 30.0 N = (m)(15.0 m/s2), which we can solve for m to find the mass of the crate. This gives us m = 30.0 N / 15.0 m/s2 = 2.0 kg.

Therefore, the weight of the crate is 2.0 kg. This is the force that gravity exerts on the crate, and it is equal to the mass of the crate times the acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s2).
