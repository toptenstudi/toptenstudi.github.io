13. differentiate general industries from handicrafts. write 3-5 sentences​


Answer:

General industries refer to large-scale manufacturing activities that involve the use of modern technologies and machinery for the production of goods in a standardized manner. These industries usually produce goods in large quantities and are designed to meet the demands of a wide range of consumers. Examples of general industries include automobile manufacturing, electronics, textile manufacturing, and food processing.

Handicrafts, on the other hand, refer to the production of goods by skilled artisans using traditional techniques and tools. Handicrafts are usually made in small quantities and are often unique, reflecting the creativity and individuality of the artisan. Examples of handicrafts include pottery, weaving, wood carving, and jewelry making.

18. This theory involved in response to shift from handicraft to industrial production it is called__________ theory


Answer:

industrialization theory

Explanation:

Industrialization is the process by which an economy is transformed from a primarily agricultural one to one based on the manufacturing of goods. Individual manual labor is often replaced by mechanized mass production, and craftsmen are replaced by assembly lines. Characteristics of industrialization include economic growth, the more efficient division of labor, and the use of technological innovation to solve problems as opposed to dependency on conditions outside of human control.

25. what factors most depleting the handicrafts industry in the philippines​


Answer:

There are several factors that have contributed to the depletion of the handicrafts industry in the Philippines, including:

Competition from cheaper, mass-produced products: The rise of cheap, mass-produced goods from China and other countries has made it difficult for local handicrafts makers to compete on price.

Lack of government support: The government has not provided enough support to the handicrafts industry in terms of funding, marketing, and training, which has hindered the industry's growth and development.

Changing consumer preferences: Modern consumers often prefer more contemporary and functional products, which can be difficult for traditional handicrafts makers to keep up with.

Limited access to markets: Many handicrafts makers in the Philippines have limited access to international markets due to high export costs and complex trade regulations.

Lack of innovation: Some handicrafts makers have been slow to adapt to changing consumer demands and have not invested in new technologies or design innovations.

Overall, addressing these challenges will be essential for the long-term survival and growth of the handicrafts industry in the Philippines. This could include increased government support, improved access to markets, and greater innovation and adaptation to changing consumer preferences.

Answer:

There have 5 contributed to the decline of the handicrafts industry in the Philippines, including:

1. Competition from mass-produced goods: With the rise of mass production and the availability of cheaper, imported goods, locally produced handicrafts have become less competitive in terms of price and availability.

2. Lack of modernization and innovation: Many of the traditional techniques and designs used in the production of handicrafts have not been updated to meet the demands of modern consumers. This has made them less appealing to younger generations who prefer more contemporary designs.

3. Limited access to markets: Many artisans and small-scale producers lack access to markets beyond their local communities, making it difficult for them to sell their products and reach a larger customer base.

4. Limited support and funding: There is a lack of government support and funding for the handicrafts industry in the Philippines, which has made it difficult for artisans and small-scale producers to access resources such as training, equipment, and capital.

5. Changing consumer preferences: As consumer preferences shift towards more modern and mass-produced goods, the demand for traditional handicrafts has declined, leading to a decrease in production and sales.

Overall, addressing these factors and providing support for the handicrafts industry in the Philippines is crucial in order to preserve this important cultural heritage and support the livelihoods of artisans and small-scale producers.


26. as a student how can you help your handicraft industry in your own way?​


In a growing technology-run and industrialised world, people are increasingly forgetting the traditional crafts and moving towards new innovation. Due to this, artisans are moving to alternative income generation methods, for instance -- daily wage labour and farming; and art is losing its grip.

Preserving and protecting the skills and knowledge of traditional crafts is a growing challenge. So, it is high time that art forms are revived and awareness spread about them in urban space. Let's move towards a world where handcrafted items are given due respect and the artisans are also get the recognition they deserve for their skills.

Here are 3 ways to protect the dying handicraft industry:

1. Understand the global market:To keep the traditional craft alive, it is important for artisans to understand how trade fairs work and participate in the same with an all round understanding of the profit and loss and pros and cons involved in the global market.

2. Increase interaction with the consumers:Artisans need to interact with the consumers and tell them all about their work and the meaning behind it. This inspires the customers and establishes a connection between art and buyer. This also helps the artisans know what the consumers want or need.

3. Recognise it as a source of supplementary income:Since the craft sector is the second largest employer in rural India after agriculture, it is a source of supplementary income for the seasonal agricultural workers, who have limited alternative employment opportunities in the village.
