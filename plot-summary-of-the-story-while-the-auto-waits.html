20. your reflection about the story while the auto waits 200 words​


Answer:

The short story "While the Auto Waits" by O. Henry is a poignant tale of love and sacrifice, highlighting the importance of selflessness and kindness in human relationships.

The story is set on a cold winter night, when a young couple, Mary and Tom, are out for a drive in their automobile. The car breaks down, leaving them stranded in the middle of nowhere. As they wait for help to arrive, Mary begins to feel the effects of the cold weather and her poor health. Tom realizes that he needs to act quickly if he wants to save Mary's life.

In a selfless act of love, Tom decides to sacrifice himself to keep Mary warm by lighting a fire and covering her with his coat. As he waits for help to arrive, he reflects on his life and his love for Mary, realizing that he is willing to give up everything for her.

The story is a powerful reminder of the importance of selflessness and sacrifice in human relationships. It is a testament to the enduring power of love, even in the face of difficult circumstances. The story also serves as a warning about the dangers of materialism and selfishness, encouraging readers to value love and relationships above all else.

Overall, "While the Auto Waits" is a touching story that leaves a lasting impression on the reader, reminding us to be kind, compassionate, and selfless in our interactions with others.
