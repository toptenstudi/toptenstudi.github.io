7. 1. Find the sum of money collected by the Games stall. 2. What percentage of the total money was collected by the Souvenir stall? 3. What fraction of the total money was collected by the food stall? 4. What fraction of the total money collected by the drink stall? 5. What percentage of the total money was collected by the Food and Drinks stall together? Paki tingnan po yung picture


1. Find the sum of money collected by the Games stall.

The game stall collected  [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] of the total amount of money collected.

So, total amount of money multiplied by the fraction of the game stall will give you the sum of money collected by the games stall.

  P5,880.00 x   [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] = P1,470.00

Answer:  P1,470.00

2. What percentage of the total money was collected by the Souvenir stall?

We first have to get the amount of the total money collected by the souvenir stall.

So, the total amount of money subtracted by the money collected by all the stores except the souvenir stall.

games stall = P1,470.00 (question 1) food stall = P2,352.00 (given) drinks stall = P882.00 (given)

  P5,880.00 - P1,470.00 - P2352.00 - P882.00 = P1,176.00

Now, we divide the amount collected by the souvenir stall by the total amount collected at the fair.

  P1,176.00 ÷ P5,880.00  = 0.2

To get the percentage, we simply multiply the decimal to 100%.

  0.2 x 100% = 20%

Answer:  20%

3. What fraction of the total money was collected by the food stall?

To get the fraction, we must first divide the amount collected by the food stall by the total amount collected at the fair.

  P2,352.00 ÷ P5,880 = 0.4

We then convert the decimal to get the fraction. Since the 4 is in the tenths place in the decimal, it will be converted to [tex]\frac{4}{10}[/tex]. [tex]\frac{4}{10}[/tex] could be the answer but it is NOT in the lowest term. 4 and 10 have a Greatest Common Factor of 2.

  [tex]\frac{4}{10}[/tex]  ≈  [tex]\frac{2}{5}[/tex]

Answer:  2/5

4. What fraction of the total money collected by the drink stall?

Using the same solution for question 3:

  P882.00 ÷ P5,880 = 0.15

       0.15 (read as 15 hundredths)

  0.15 = [tex]\frac{15}{100}[/tex]

       GCF = 5

  [tex]\frac{15}{100}[/tex] ≈ [tex]\frac{3}{20}[/tex]

Answer: 3/20

5. What percentage of the total money was collected by the Food and Drinks stall together?

Looking at the solutions for question 3 and 4. We know that the decimal for the money collected by the food stall is 0.4, and by the drinks stall is 0.15. We simply add the two together.

  0.4 + 0.15 = 0.55

To get the percentage, we simply multiply the decimal to 100%.

  0.55 x 100% = 55%

Answer: 55%

For example, in given number 1, the denominators are 3 and 4. So, list their multiples.
