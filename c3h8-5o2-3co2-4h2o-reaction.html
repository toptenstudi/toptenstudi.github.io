5. A sample of propane (C3H8) gas was burned with excess oxygen gas. If 4.20 L of CO2 were produced from the reaction of at STP, how many liters of propane were used in the reaction?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

C₃H₈ + 5O₂ → 3CO₂ + 4H₂O

Step 2: Calculate the volume of propane used in the reaction.

Based on the balanced chemical equation (assuming that the conditions are the same for C₃H₈ and CO₂), 1 L of C₃H₈ is stoichiometrically to 3 L of CO₂.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_{\text{C}_3\text{H}_8} & = \text{4.20 L} \: \text{CO}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 L} \: \text{C}_3\text{H}_8}{\text{3 L} \: \text{CO}_2} \\ & = \boxed{\text{1.40 L}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 1.40 L of propane were used in the reaction.

15. Which is the correct coefficient of oxygen in the equation? C3H8 + _O2 --> 3CO2 + 4H2O ​


Balance the following equation: C3H8 + O2 ----> CO2 + H2O. (There will be no need for state symbols.)

First of all, to balance these equations, list the number of each atom, for both sides of the equation. In this example, it would be:

Carbon: 3 Carbon: 1

Hydrogen: 8 Hydrogen: 2

Oxygen: 2 Oxygen: 3

The first step would be to balance out the carbons on the products side by multiplying CO2 by 3. The new equation will be

C3H8 + 02 ----> 3CO2 + H20

Now, the carbons are balanced, we will look at the hydrogens. We can multiply the number of waters on the product side by 4, to make 4 H20 molecules. This gives both sides of the equation 8 hydrogens. Next we can check our oxygens. There will be 10 oxygens on the products side and 2 on the reactants so to balance these out, we multiply the 02 on the reactants side by 5.

The final equation will be C3H8 + 502 ----> 3CO2 + 4H20.

30. Fe2o3 3co -----> 2fe 3co2 in this reaction, how many grams of fe2o3 are required to completely react with 84 grams of co


Answer:

Molar mass of CO: 28.01 g/mol

Molar mass of Fe2O3: 159.69 g/mol

Find how many moles are in 84 grams of CO

84 g ÷ 28.01 g/mol = 2.9989 ≈ 3 moles

1/3 × 3 moles = 1 mole

↑                  

Ratio of Fe2O3 to CO in reaction

1 mole × 159.69 g/mol Fe2O3 = 159.69 grams

Therefore, you need 159.69 grams of iron (III) oxide (Fe2O3) for it to completely react with 84 grams of carbon monoxide (CO).
