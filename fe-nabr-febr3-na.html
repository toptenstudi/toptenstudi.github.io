3. Fe+NaBr=Fe Bc+Na CHEMICAL EQUATION


Good Day

Given:

                       Fe + NaBr  ⇒ FeBr₃  +  Na

The type of reaction is single replacement in which Fe (iron) is more active that Na (sodium) thus, it replaces the place of sodium. Fe can only replace Na since they have the same charge. Fe cannot replace the place of Br (Bromine) since Bromine is negative charged and Fe is positive charged.

To Balance the equation:

 

                           Fe + 3NaBr  ⇒ FeBr₃  +  3Na

                                      ↓                          ↓

                               Reactant              Product

                                 Fe = 1                    Fe = 1

                                 Na = 3                   Na = 3        

                                 Br = 3                   Br = 3

We balance the chemical equation since atoms are not destroyed in the process and the mass is conserved. The mass of the reactant is equal to the mass of the product which also means, the number of atoms of elements in both the product and reactant are equal.

13. name of compound of NaBr​


The name of the compound NaBr is Sodium bromide.

Good Day

The chemical name of NaBr is Sodium Bromide

It is produced when we combine 2 moles of Sodium (Na) atom to 1 mole Bromine (Br₂) to produce a single product 1 mole of Sodium Bromide (NaBr).

The type of reaction is known as combination or synthesis reaction. Since sodium is a metal bonded to Bromine which is a nonmetal, the type of bond the formed is ionic bond and NaBr is an ionic compound.

Chemical Equation:

                      2 Na     +     Br₂    ⇒     2 NaBr

18. What is the coefficient of NaBr to balance the equation: Fe + ____ NaBr → FeBr₃ + 3Na​


Answer:

3

Explanation:

Count the number of atoms on both sides:

Left side: Fe = 1, Na = 1, Br = 1Right side: Fe = 1, Br = 3, Na = 3

Na and Br is not balanced. To balance Na and Br on both sides, we should multiply Na and Br on left side by 3. So the coefficient of NaBr should be 3.
