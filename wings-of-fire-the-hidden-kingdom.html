1. No, a fire itself does not cast a shadow. Shadows are formed when light is blocked by an opaque object, creating an area of darkness behind it. Since fire is a source of light, it does not cast a shadow.

2. No, momentum is a property of moving objects and is directly related to their mass and velocity. It cannot be hidden from human eyes, as it is a measurable physical quantity that can be observed and calculated. However, the effects of momentum may not always be immediately apparent without proper instrumentation or observation.

3. Currently, there is no complete and widely accepted theory that fully explains the relationship between gravity and quantum mechanics. The theories of general relativity, which describes gravity on a large scale, and quantum mechanics, which describes the behavior of particles on a small scale, are not fully compatible and reconciling them is a major challenge in modern physics. Various theoretical frameworks, such as string theory and loop quantum gravity, are being explored in an attempt to unify gravity and quantum mechanics.

4. No, momentum is a measurable physical quantity that can be observed and calculated based on the mass and velocity of an object. It cannot be hidden from human eyes, as it is a fundamental property of moving objects.

5. Yes, gold can be created from other elements through a process called nuclear transmutation. This can occur naturally through radioactive decay or artificially through nuclear reactions in particle accelerators or reactors. For example, gold can be produced by bombarding a target material with high-energy particles, causing nuclear reactions that result in the production of gold atoms. However, these processes are typically not economically feasible for large-scale production of gold and are mainly used for scientific research or specialized applications.

29. Who seperated kingdoms to kingdom archaebacteria?


Answer:

Who seperated kingdoms to kingdom archaebacteria?

In 1977, Woese and his colleagues suggested that the original bacteria kingdom should be divided into two new kingdoms, called Eubacteria and Archaebacteria. This resulted in a six-kingdom taxonomy that has been widely accepted for many years.

Explanation:

How did scientists find archaebacteria?

The Archaea were discovered when the molecular signature technique was applied to a class of methane-generating organisms frequently found in bogs or pond mud. These methanogens cannot live in oxygenated environments, and so can only be studied by using special techniques in the laboratory.

Who introduced kingdom Archaebacteria?

Microbiologist Carl Woese

crobiologist Carl WoeseIn 1977 American microbiologist Carl Woese, on the basis of analyses of ribosomal RNA, proposed that the prokaryotes, long considered to be a single group of organisms (essentially, the bacteria), actually consist of two separate lineages. Woese called these two lineages the eubacteria and the archaebacteria.
