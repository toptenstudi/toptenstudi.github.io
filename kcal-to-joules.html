24. what is the potential of the 30kg object at the height of 30 meters? a. 900 joules b. 450 joules c. 3550 joules d. 2970 joules​


Solution:

The potential energy of an object is

PE = mgh

where:

PE = potential energy

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity

g = 9.81 m/s² ≈ 10 m/s²

h = height

Listing the values

m = 30 kg

h = 3 m

Solving for PE

PE = mgh

PE = (30 kg)(10 m/s²)(3 m)

PE = 900 J

Answer:

A. 900 joules

25. If there is a 50 kcal of energy in the third trophic level, how much energy in kcalwas available at the first trophic level?A. 5 kcalC. 500 kcalB. 50 kcalD. 5,000 kcal​


Answer:

5,000 kcal

Explanation:

The amount of energy at each trophic level decreases as it moves through an ecosystem. As little as 10 percent of the energy at any trophic level is transferred to the next level; the rest is lost largely through metabolic processes as heat. If a grassland ecosystem has 50,000 kilocalories (kcal) of energy concentrated in vegetation, only about 5,000 kcal will be transferred to primary consumers, and very little (only 10 kcal) will make it to the tertiary level .

Question

Ana ordered a lunch meal in her school canteen that includes a burger patty, sautéed mongo, and a cup of rice. The meal provides 30 grams of protein, 120 grams of carbohydrate, and 25 grams of fat. 

Answer:

1. How many kcal of proteins will Ana derived from the meal? 

1 gram of protein = 4 kilocalories (kcal)Protein = 30 × 4 = 120 kcal 

2. How many kcal of carbohydrates will she be able to get from the meal?

1 gram of carbohydrates = 4 kcalCarbohydrates = 120 × 4 = 480 kcal

3. How many kcal of fats will anna be taking in for lunch?

1 gram of fats = 9 kcalFats = 25 × 9 = 225 kcal 

4. How many kilocalories does this meal provide? 

Total kcal = Protein (kcal) + Carbohydrates (kcal) + Fats (kcal)Total kcal = 120 + 480 + 225 = 825 kcal

5. Do you think that this meal is a good diet why? or why not?

 A healthy diet varies for every individual. The ideal calorie intake depends on the age, gender, and the level of their physical activities in daily basis. For Ana's case, I can't tell if her diet is appropriate since there's no age stated on the problem, and her activities on daily basis is not mentioned. But considering that she eats on the school canteen, I assume that she belongs to the school-age children (7-12 years old). If her age is within the range of 7-12, then her meal for lunch is a good diet. (Again, this is hypothetical).The estimated total kcal for school-age children is 1,200 – 1,600 kcal for females and 1,200 – 2,000 kcal for males. Remember that a healthy lunch for school-age group should supply at least 1/3 of the child's daily food needs.
