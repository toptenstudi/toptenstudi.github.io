11. 28. A 2kg object is moving at 6m/s. what is the kinetic energy of the object?A. 12 JoulesB. 36 JoulesC. 18 JoulesD. 6 Joules​


PROBLEM:

A 2kg object is moving at 6m/s. What is the kinetic energy of the object?

A. 12 Joules

B. 36 Joules

C. 18 Joules

D. 6 Joules

FORMULA:

[tex]KE = \frac{1}{2} mv^{2} [/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = velocity

SOLUTION:

Substitute the given velocity and mass, simplify the following.

[tex]KE = \frac{1}{2} mv^{2} \\ \\ KE = \frac{1}{2} (2kg)(6m/s {)}^{2} \\ \\ KE = \frac{1}{2} (2kg)(36m/s {)} \\ \\ KE = \frac{2}{2} kg(36m/s) \\ \\ KE = 1kg(36m/s) \\ \\ KE = 36 \: J[/tex]

ANSWER:

The kinetic energy is 36 Joules.

Letter B is the answer.

Here, we're relating a few things: (1) the mass of the substance, (2) its specific heat, and (3) its temperature change with the equation,

q=mCsΔT

0.21 cal/g ⋅ ° C ⋅ 4.184 J/cal ≈ (0.879 J )

q = 200.0g ⋅ 0.879 J/g ⋅ ° C ⋅  10 ° C

22. what is the potential of the 30kg object at the height of 30 meters? a. 900 joules b. 450 joules c. 3550 joules d. 2970 joules​


Solution:

The potential energy of an object is

PE = mgh

where:

PE = potential energy

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity

g = 9.81 m/s² ≈ 10 m/s²

h = height

Listing the values

m = 30 kg

h = 3 m

Solving for PE

PE = mgh

PE = (30 kg)(10 m/s²)(3 m)

PE = 900 J

Answer:

A. 900 joules
