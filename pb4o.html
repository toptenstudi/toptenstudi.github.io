16. Lead (II) oxide is obtained by roasting galena, lead (II) sulfide, in air. The unbalanced equation is: 2 PbS (s) + 3 0₂ (g) → 2 PbO (s) + 2 SO₂ (g) a. Determine the theoretical yield of PbO if 200.0 g of PbS is heated. b. What is the percent yield if 170.0 g of PbO is obtained?​


Answer:

a. The balanced equation for the reaction is:

2PbS (s) + 3O2 (g) → 2PbO (s) + 2SO2 (g)

The molar mass of PbS is:

207.2 g/mol

So, 200.0 g of PbS corresponds to:

200.0 g / 207.2 g/mol = 0.964 mol

According to the balanced equation, 2 mol of PbO are produced for every 2 mol of PbS. Therefore, the number of moles of PbO produced is also 0.964 mol.

The molar mass of PbO is:

223.2 g/mol

So, the theoretical yield of PbO is:

0.964 mol x 223.2 g/mol = 215.1 g PbO

Therefore, the theoretical yield of PbO is 215.1 g.

b. The percent yield is given by the following formula:

Percent yield = actual yield / theoretical yield x 100%

We are given that the actual yield of PbO is:

170.0 g

And we calculated in part a that the theoretical yield is:

215.1 g

So, the percent yield is:

Percent yield = 170.0 g / 215.1 g x 100% = 79.0%

Therefore, the percent yield is 79.0%.

19. determine the theoretical yield of PbO if 210.1g of PbS is heated. compute the percent yield if 150.5g of PbO is obtained. PbS (s) + O2 (g) - PbO (s) + SO2 (g)balance the given question​


Answer:

196.0 g

76.79%

Explanation:

Molecular weights

Pb → 207.2 g/mol

S → 32.07 g/mol

O → 16.00 g/mol

MW PbS = 207.2 + 32.07 = 239.3 g/mol

MW PbO = 207.2 + 16.00 = 223.2 g/mol

Balancing the chemical equation

2PbS (s) + 3O2 (g) → 2PbO (s) + 2SO2 (g)

Solving for the theoretical yield

theoretical yield = 210.1 g PbS × (1 mol PbS / 239.3 g Pbs) × (2 mol PbO / 2 mol PbS) × (223.2 g PbO / 1 mol PbO)

theoretical yield = 196.0 g

Solving for the percent yield

percent yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100

percent yield = (150.5 g / 196.0 g) × 100

percent yield = 76.79%
