2. the role of assessment in making instructional decision


The role of assessment in making instructional decisions is very important as part of the assessment process that can reflect the achievement of a goal. Through assessment, we can measure skills, insights, talents, and other qualifications.

Accepting or rejecting the examinee for entrance or qualifying to a program or school activity will depend on the evaluation findings. In making this choice, it is necessary to determine which kids require remediation or may be suggested for the school's enrichment program.

Several benefits of making instructional decision are:

examining every kid to identify those that want further help as soon as possibleServices for special education and general education are seamlessly combined.A commitment to research-based practices that are matched to the requirements of students

Instructional decision making integrates assessment and instruction within a multi-level prevention system to maximize student achievement and to reduce behavior problems.

Schools identify students in need of additional supports for learning outcomes, monitor students' progress, provide evidence-based interventions and adjust the intensity and nature of the instruction depending on a student's responsiveness.

26. A. Instruction: Cite personal experiences and explain emotions especially in making decisions. 1. Anger 2. Sadness 3. Overexcitement please help me​


Answer:

Anger: One personal experience I had where anger played a role in my decision-making process was when I was driving home from work and another driver cut me off in traffic. I was so angry at the other driver's reckless behavior that I felt like I needed to do something to show my frustration. I ended up honking my horn and gesturing angrily at the other driver, even though I knew it wouldn't do any good and might just escalate the situation. In hindsight, I realize that my decision to express my anger in this way was not a very wise one, as it could have led to more dangerous or aggressive behavior on the road.

Sadness: Another emotion that has influenced my decision-making is sadness. For example, when I was in college, I had a close friend who was struggling with depression. I was so sad and worried about her that it was difficult for me to focus on anything else. As a result, I found myself making poor decisions in other areas of my life, such as neglecting my studies or skipping classes. I eventually realized that I needed to seek help for my friend and for myself in order to move forward and make better decisions.

Overexcitement: A third emotion that has affected my decision-making is overexcitement. For example, when I was planning a vacation with some friends, I was so excited about the prospect of going on an adventure that I didn't think through all of the details or consider the potential risks or downsides. As a result, we ended up booking a trip that was much more expensive and logistically complicated than we had anticipated, and it ended up causing a lot of stress and frustration for everyone involved. In this case, my overexcitement led me to make a hasty and ill-considered decision.

28. Instructions. Reread the poem "Delotavo's Diaspora." Then, cite pieces of evidence from the poem to support the general statement given below. "Leaving the country to work abroad is a difficult decision for a Filipino family."​


Answer:

In "Delotavo's Diaspora," the poet describes the father's departure as "wrenching, as only the birth pangs of separation can be," indicating the emotional pain of leaving. The mother and children are left behind to "keep the home fires burning" and "live without him." The children are described as growing up with "an uncle, a lola, a neighbour or two," showing the difficulty of maintaining a family structure without one parent. The mother works hard to provide for the family, selling "balut and bibingka and puto" and working "long hours" as a domestic worker. This highlights the financial struggle of those left behind, further emphasizing the difficulty of the decision to work abroad. Overall, these pieces of evidence support the general statement that leaving the country to work abroad is a difficult decision for a Filipino family.
