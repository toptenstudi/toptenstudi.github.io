8. How does introducing procedures or processes or giving directions, for example in a laboratory or computing exercise, ensure that stages or sequences are made clear and are explained in verbal as well as written form help students with intellectual disability follow the procedure/process? ​


Answer:

Introducing procedures or processes in a clear and concise manner, both verbally and in written form, helps students with intellectual disabilities to follow the procedure more effectively. Verbal explanations provide immediate feedback and support, while written instructions allow students to reference the steps at their own pace. Breaking down complex procedures into smaller, sequential steps can also make the process easier to understand and follow. Having a visual representation of the steps, such as a flowchart or diagram, can also be helpful for students with intellectual disabilities to see the connections between the steps. Providing clear and consistent directions can reduce confusion and increase the likelihood of success for students with intellectual disabilities.


9. 2. Do you think it would be harder to live with a physical or an intellectual disability? If yes or No Why?​


PHYSICAL

Living with a physical or intellectual disability both come with their own set of challenges, and it can be difficult to compare the two in terms of which is harder to live with. Some individuals with physical disabilities may face difficulties with mobility, performing daily tasks, and may require physical assistance or accommodations to participate in activities. On the other hand, individuals with intellectual disabilities may struggle with communication, social skills, and may require additional support to learn and succeed in academic or vocational settings.

Ultimately, whether one disability is harder to live with than another depends on the individual and the extent of their disability. It’s important to recognize that both physical and intellectual disabilities can impact a person’s daily life, and it’s essential for society to provide support and accommodations to help individuals with disabilities reach their full potential.

Question

How can you identify an intellectual disability?

Answer

Some of the signs of intellectual disability

1. sit up, crawl, or walk later than other children.

2. learn to talk later, or have trouble speaking.

3. find it hard to remember things.

4. have trouble understanding social rules.

5. have trouble seeing the results of their actions.

6. have trouble solving problems.

Explanation:

What is Intellectual Disability?

Intellectual disability involves problems with general mental abilities that affect functioning in two areas:

- intellectual functioning (such as learning, problem solving, judgement)

- adaptive functioning (activities of daily life such as communication and independent living)

Additionally, the intellectual and adaptive deficity begin early in the developmental period.

Intellectual disability affects about 1% of the population, and of those about 85% have mild intellectual disability. Males are more likely than females to be diagnosed with intellectual disability.
