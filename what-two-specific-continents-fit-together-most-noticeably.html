14. who created two maps that showed how the continents were like two pieces of a jigsaw puzzle that fitted together quite perfectly??​


#BrainlyFast

Answer:

Alfred Wegener

Alfred Wegener proposed the "The Continental Drift Theory" which is the older version of plate tectonic theory. According to the continental drift theory, the seven continents today was once connected before forming a single land mass known as "Pangaea". This theory tells us that the crustal plates are moving or drifting making the seven continents located in their position today.

One of his evidence is the continental jigsaw puzzle wherein the edge of one continent is fitted together quite perfectly towards the other continent. Example: The South America and Africa.

16. What clues might scientists have used to determine the continents once fit together​


Answer:

In the early part of the 20th century, scientists began to put together evidence that the continents could move around on Earth's surface. The evidence for continental drift included the fit of the continents; the distribution of ancient fossils, rocks, and mountain ranges; and the locations of ancient climatic zones.
