25. Explain gilt and grower practices​


Answer:

Gilt and grower practices are related to the management of pig production. These terms are used to describe different stages of the pig's life cycle and the management practices associated with each stage.

Gilt practices refer to the management of female pigs that have not yet given birth to a litter. These pigs are typically between six and eight months old and have not yet reached sexual maturity. Gilt management practices include proper nutrition, housing, and health care to ensure that the animal reaches maturity in good health and is ready to breed when the time comes.

Grower practices, on the other hand, refer to the management of pigs that have already reached sexual maturity and are being raised for market. Grower pigs are typically between 40 and 100 pounds and are raised until they reach market weight, which is around 240-270 pounds. Grower pig management practices include proper nutrition, housing, and health care, as well as monitoring for any signs of disease or health issues that could affect the animal's growth and development.

In summary, gilt and grower practices are two distinct stages in the management of pig production. Gilt practices focus on preparing young female pigs for breeding, while grower practices focus on raising mature pigs for market. Proper management practices are essential for ensuring the health and well-being of the animals and maximizing productivity and profitability for the pig farmer.
