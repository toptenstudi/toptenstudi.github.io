1. what is the western concept of science as a social endeavor​


Answer:

Science is a social endeavor because it involves groups of scientists working together. These scientists also correct or verify each other, such as through peer review or replication attempts. Also, science is social in the sense that it takes place through social institutions, and often for the benefit of society.

Explanation:

In this section, we’ve seen that science is a community endeavor. Contrary to the stereotype of solitary lab work, scientists frequently interact with colleagues to collab-orate on projects, review each other’s work, share information, and brainstorm new ideas. In these interactions, scientists work to maintain a set of cultural norms and ex-pectations: scrutinize ideas, be honest, give credit where credit is due, and work within the ethical guidelines of the community.
