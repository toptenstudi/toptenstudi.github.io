12. How to find the area bounded by y=x, y=0 , and x=3?


Answer:

A=4.5 square units

Explanation:

First, you should plot all these points.

y=x means that both x and y have the same value. Examples are (1,1),(2,2),(-1,-1) and so on. So the line with look like a slanting line with the top going to the right and bottom extending to the left. Of course it passes through the origin (0,0)

y=0 is a point 0 on the y-axis and or simply the origin.

x=3 is a point 3 on the x-axis. This also means a vertical line that passes through the x-axis' 3 extending above and below.

Plotting all these, you should have a triangle now, and also an intersection of x=3 and y=x at the point (3,3)

Now for the area, the formula for finding the area of a right triangle is A=ab/2, where a and b are the measurements of the legs of triangle.

Since we already have a graph, it would be easy to determine the measurements of the legs. Both of them are 3 units.

A=ab/2=(3)(3)/2=9/2 or 4.5 square units.

23. different of open bound versus closed and bound​


Answer:

An open interval does not include its endpoints, and is enclosed in parentheses. A closed interval includes its endpoints, and is enclosed in square brackets. An interval is considered bounded if both endpoints are real numbers. An interval is unbounded if both endpoints are not real numbers¿
