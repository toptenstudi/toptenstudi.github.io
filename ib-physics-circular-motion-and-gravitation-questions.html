24. what is Gravitational force and motion?​


What is Gravitational force?

Gravitational force is a force that exists between any two objects that have mass. It is the force that pulls objects toward each other. The more massive an object is, the more gravitational force it exerts on another object. The closer two objects are to each other, the stronger their gravitational force is.

What is motion?

Motion refers to the change in position of an object over time. When an object is influenced by a gravitational force, it experiences motion due to that force. For example, when a ball is dropped from a height, it falls toward the ground due to the gravitational force between the ball and the earth. The motion of the ball can be described using the laws of motion and the equations of motion, which can help predict its behavior.

Hence, motion and gravitational force are related in that the force of gravity can cause objects to move or accelerate.

27. how does gravitational force affect the motion of objects ?


The gravitational force is a force that attracts any two objects with mass. We call the gravitational force attractive because it always tries to pull masses together, it never pushes them apart. This is why objects keep falling, or stay on the ground, because they are attracted by gravity.
