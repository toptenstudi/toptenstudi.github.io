Answer:

Natural selection is the process through which populations of living organisms adapt and change. A classic example of natural selection at work is the origin of giraffes’ long necks. The ancestors of modern giraffes were animals similar to deer or antelope, with necks of ordinary length. However, because the trees in their habitat were tall, those giraffes with slightly longer necks had an advantage over their shorter-necked fellows. The longer-necked giraffes reproduced more, so in the next generation longer necks were more common.
