22. A flagpole is at the top of a building. From a point 420 ft from the base of the building, an engineer finds that the angles of elevation on the top of the building and the top of the flagpole are 18° and 25°, respectively. Calculate the height of the flagpole.


Answer:

h≈59.38ft

Step-by-step explanation:

The height of the building (x) is:

tan(18°)=x/420

x=420*tan(18°)

x≈136.47 ft

The height of the building plus height of the flagpole is:

tan(25°)=y/420

y=420*tan(25°)

y≈195.8 ft

Hence, the height of the flagpole is:

h=y-x

h=195.85-136.47

h≈59.38ft

1.5 :1 = x:5

x being the height of the flagpole. You will need to multiply the extremes (1.5 and 5) and the means (1 and x) to get x. x would be, in this case 7.5. That would be the height of the flagpole.
