30. Historical Examples that show how intolerance of dissent has been a problem for certain sectors of society


Answer:

Acts of dissent in history both from the past and present that have been silenced by intolerance, causing problems to certain sectors in society.

Here are some examples dissent that the powerful tried to silence:

The Mendiola Massacre which took the life of 13 demonstrators pushing for land reform in the Philippines. The arrest and incarceration of Nelson Mandela for going against the White South African government. The rule of Chile’s Augusto Pinochet where thousands of people opposing him disappeared. The arrest and imprisonment of Julian Assange for exposing the war crimes of the American government.  The arrest of Chelsea Manning for being a whistleblower against the US government and their war crimes. The American government’s attack on the Native American protestors opposing the installation of the Dakota Access Pipeline in 2017. The prosecution of Edward Snowden for exposing America’s Surveillance State. Mohammad Ali’s career was put on hold because he refused to go murder people in Vietnam. His boxing license was canceled, he was forbidden to leave the country and faced jail time because of his conviction.

In each of those examples given, certain sectors were burdened with the abuses of the powerful, and it took those brave people’s sacrifices to stand up for most of us watching on the sidelines.
