6. Why should the crutch be held at 30 degrees?


Answer:

They should actually be positioned about 5 cm below the axilla with the elbow flexed 15 degrees, approximately.[1] The design includes an axilla bar, a handpiece and double uprights joined distally by a single leg. They are adjustable in height, both the overall height and handgrip height can be adjusted (adjustable approximately 48 to 60 inches (12 to 153 cm)[2]

Forearm crutches (or lofstrand, elbow or Canadian crutches). Their design includes a single upright, a forearm cuff and a handgrip. The height of the forearm crutches are indicated from handgrip to the floor (adjustable from 29 to 35 inches or 74 to 89 cm).[2]

Gutter Crutches (or adjustable arthritic crutches, forearm support crutches) These are additional types of crutches, which is composed of padded forearm support made up of metal, a strap and adjustable handpiece with a rubber ferrule. These crutches are used for patients who are on partial weight bearing like Rheumatoid disease.[1]

1. Techniques for transporting the victim:

a. Human Crutch Method: Support the injured person's weight by placing their arm over your shoulder and holding their opposite hip or thigh.

b. Drag Method: Grasp the person's clothes at the shoulders and drag them towards you, supporting their head and neck.

C. Cradle Method: Support the injured person's head and neck on your thighs and lift them by placing one arm under their back and the other arm under their legs.

2. Methods for carrying chairs:

-Carry the chair by the seat or backrest with both hands.

-Rest the chair on one shoulder with one hand holding the backrest and the other hand holding the seat.

3. Method for carrying a stretcher:

-Lift the stretcher by placing one hand under the headrest and the other hand under the foot end.

-Carry the stretcher at waist height with both hands.

4. Method for loading a stretcher using two blankets:

-Place one blanket on the stretcher.

-Place the injured person on the blanket.

-Use the second blanket to cover the injured person.

-Lift the stretcher by the corners of the blankets and carry it at waist height with both hands.
