Answer:

Answer:

A complex sentence is made up of an independent and dependent clause. An independent clause is a simple sentence that gives a complete thought and can stand alone. A dependent / subordinate clause is a clause that does not give a complete thought, it starts with a subordinating conjunction such as because, so, since, if, unless, after, etc. And cannot stand alone. The complex sentences that were presented show cause-effect relation. Because, so, since and if are commonly used subordinate result or what happens because of something.

1 minute = 60 seconds

2 × 60 = 120

Then divide 120 by 3

120 ÷ 3 = 40

Multipy 40 by 2
