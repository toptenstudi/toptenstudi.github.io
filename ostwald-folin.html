7. The first step in the ostwald process for producing nitric acid is if the reaction of 150 g of ammonia with 150 g of oxygen gas yields 87 g of nitric oxide what is the percent yield of the solution


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

4NH₃ + 5O₂ → 4NO + 6H₂O

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of NO formed by each reactant.

• Using NH₃

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 4 moles of NH₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 4 moles of NO.

The molar mass of NH₃ is 17.031 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of NO} & = \text{150 g} \: \text{NH}_3 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{NH}_3}{\text{17.031 g} \: \text{NH}_3} \times \frac{\text{4 mol NO}}{\text{4 mol} \: \text{NH}_3} \\ & = \text{8.8075 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• Using O₂

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 5 moles of O₂ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 4 moles of NO.

The molar mass of O₂ is 31.998 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of NO} & = \text{150 g} \: \text{O}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{O}_2}{\text{31.998 g} \: \text{O}_2} \times \frac{\text{4 mol NO}}{\text{5 mol} \: \text{O}_2} \\ & = \text{3.7502 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Determine the limiting reagent.

Since O₂ produced less amount of NO than NH₃,

O₂ is the limiting reagent.

Step 4: Calculate the theoretical yield.

The theoretical yield of a reaction is the maximum mass of a product that can be formed. It is dictated by the limiting reactant. In this case, we will start at the number of moles of NO formed from the limiting reactant (O₂) which is equal to 3.7502 mol.

The molar mass of NO is 30.006 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{theoretical yield} & = \text{3.7502 mol NO} \times \frac{\text{30.006 g NO}}{\text{1 mol NO}} \\ & =\text{112.53 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the percent yield.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{percent yield} & = \frac{\text{actual yield}}{\text{theoretical yield}} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{\text{87 g}}{\text{112.53 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{77.3\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the percent yield of the reaction is 77.3%.
