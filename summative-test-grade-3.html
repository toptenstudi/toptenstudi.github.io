Answer:

"For your hardworking teacher who diligently checks."

1. Plants have different parts such as roots, stems, leaves, flowers, and fruits. The roots anchor the plant to the ground and absorb water and nutrients from the soil. The stem supports the plant and transports water and nutrients from the roots to the leaves. The leaves are the main site of photosynthesis, where sunlight is converted into energy for the plant. Flowers are reproductive structures that produce seeds, while fruits protect and disperse the seeds.

2. Plants are important to humans in many ways. They provide us with food, oxygen, medicine, and materials for clothing, shelter, and other products. They also help regulate the climate, prevent soil erosion, and support biodiversity.

3. Living things are organisms that have the ability to grow, reproduce, and respond to stimuli, while non-living things do not have these characteristics. Living things are made up of cells, use energy, and have genetic material that is passed down from one generation to the next. Non-living things, on the other hand, are made up of atoms and molecules and do not have the ability to grow, reproduce, or respond to stimuli.

4. Observable characteristics that are passed from parent to offspring are called traits. In humans, animals, and plants, these traits can include physical features like eye color, hair texture, and leaf shape, as well as behaviors like hunting strategies or growth patterns. These traits are determined by genes, which are segments of DNA that code for specific proteins and enzymes.When offspring inherit genes from their parents, they may exhibit the same traits as their parents or a combination of traits from both parents. These observable characteristics are determined by the interaction between the genetic makeup and environmental factors. For example, a plant's height may be determined by its genes, but it may also be influenced by the amount of sunlight, water, and nutrients it receives. In animals, behaviors like hunting strategies may be influenced by both genetics and learning from parents or other members of their social group.

4. Observable characteristics passed from parent to offspring are called traits and can include physical features and behaviors, determined by genes and environmental factors.
