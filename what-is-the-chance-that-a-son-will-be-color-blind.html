18. 1.A color-blindness is a recessive,sex-linked disorder humans, A color-blind man has a child with a woman who is a carrier of the disorder. Illustrate using a punnett square the probability of having children who will have normal vision and children who will be color-blind. GUIDE QUESTIONS : 1.What is the genotype of the male ? 2.What is the genotype of the female ? 3.What is the chance that the child will be color-blind ? 4.What is the chance that a daughter will be color-blind? 5.What is the chance that a son will be color-blind ?


Answer:

1. [tex]X^bY[/tex]

2.[tex]X^BX^b[/tex]

3. 50%

4. 50%

5. 50%

Explanation:

As stated in the given, color blindness is a recessive and sex-linked (to X as women can have it too). We first get the genotype of the parents.

Male: As he has the sickness, he has the trait [tex]X^bY[/tex]

Female: As she is a carrier, she is heterozygous, [tex]X^BX^b[/tex]

From this, let u make a punnet square.

               [tex]X^b[/tex]               [tex]Y[/tex]

[tex]X^B[/tex]       [tex]X^BX^b[/tex]         [tex]X^BY[/tex]

[tex]X^b[/tex]       [tex]X^bX^b[/tex]           [tex]X^bY[/tex]

The chance that the child will be color blind is when all X's he or she has has the recessive linked to it or the small b. Out of the four, there are 2, [tex]X^bX^b[/tex] and [tex]X^bY[/tex]. This means a child has 2/4 or 1/2 or 50% chance of being color-blind.

One each per gender. This means a daughter has 1/2 or 50% chance of being color-blind and a son has 1/2 or 50% chance being color-blind as well.
