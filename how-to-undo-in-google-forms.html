2. How to get the weighted mean of google form survey.​


Answer:Assign weightsCollect dataCalculatedweighted scoresSum the weighted scoresCalculate the weighted mean

Explanation:

To calculate the weighted mean of a Google Form survey, follow these steps:

1. Assign weights: Assign weights to each response option in your Google Form survey. For example, if you have a multiple-choice question with three options (A, B, C) and you want to assign weights of 1, 2, and 3 respectively, then A=1, B=2, and C=3.

2. Collect data: Collect responses from your survey using Google Form.

3. Calculate weighted scores: Multiply each response by its corresponding weight to get a weighted score. For example, if you received 10 responses for Option A (weighted as 1), 5 responses for Option B (weighted as 2), and 3 responses for Option C (weighted as 3), the weighted scores would be: A=10, B=10, and C=9.

4. Sum the weighted scores: Add up all the weighted scores to get the total weighted score.

5. Sum the weights: Add up all the weights assigned to the response options.

6. Calculate the weighted mean: Divide the total weighted score by the sum of the weights to get the weighted mean.

The formula for the weighted mean is:

Weighted Mean = Total Weighted Score / Sum of Weights

29. How Google stored all of informations?​


Like most search engines, Google indexes documents by building a data structure known as inverted index. Such an index obtains a list of documents by a query word. The index is very large due to the number of documents stored in the servers. The index is partitioned by document IDs into many pieces called shards.
