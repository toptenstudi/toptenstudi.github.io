1. A Vector has an x-component of -25.0 units and a y-components of 40.0 units. Find the magnitude and direction of the vector​


Answer: We can use the Pythagorean theorem to find the magnitude of the vector:

magnitude = sqrt(x^2 + y^2)

where x and y are the x and y components of the vector, respectively. Substituting the values given, we get:

magnitude = sqrt((-25)^2 + (40)^2) = sqrt(625 + 1600) = sqrt(2225)

magnitude = 47.1699 (rounded to four decimal places)

To find the direction, we can use the inverse tangent function:

direction = tan^-1(y/x)

Substituting the values given, we get:

direction = tan^-1((40)/(−25))

direction = -58.9° or 301.1° (depending on the conventions used for measuring angles in the particular application)

Therefore, the magnitude of the vector is approximately 47.1699 units, and its direction is approximately 301.1° in standard position.

3. displacement vector of 30 km, 50° measured from the +x-axis has an x-component of _____.​


To find the x-component of a displacement vector, you can use the formula x component = displacement * cosine(angle). In this case, the x component would be:

x component = 30 km * cos(50°)

Plugging this into a calculator, we find that the x component is approximately 15.7 km.

Note that the angle is measured in degrees and must be converted to radians before being used in the cosine function. You can use the formula radians = degrees * (pi/180) to convert an angle from degrees to radians.

28. The magnitude of a vector F is 10 and the direction of a vector is 60° with the horizontal . Find the components of vector


To find the components of a vector, you can use the formulas:

Fx = F * cos(θ)

Fy = F * sin(θ)

Where F is the magnitude of the vector, θ is the angle the vector makes with the horizontal, Fx is the x-component of the vector, and Fy is the y-component of the vector.

Using these formulas, we can find the x-component and y-component of the vector with magnitude F=10 and direction θ=60°:

Fx = 10 * cos(60°) = 10 * 0.5 = 5

Fy = 10 * sin(60°) = 10 * 0.866 = 8.66

So the x-component of the vector is 5 and the y-component of the vector is 8.66.
