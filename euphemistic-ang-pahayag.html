Euphemism

A euphemism (YOO-fuh-miz-uhm) is a form of figurative language. These idiomatic, indirect expressions replace harsh, impolite, taboo, or unpleasant terms with more delicate phrases. They can be used humorously or to downplay or mask a situation, causing some to find the device deceitful or counterproductive.

Examples:

1. Uncle Keith passed away last year.

2. I have to spent a penny.

3. You have an interesting point of view.

4. I really need a bit of peace of quite.

5. My grandma is already a senior citizen.

6. Uncle Jason is between jobs.

7. He is a little thin on top.

8. This pre-loved sofa is for sale.

9. She has resigned her commission.

10. And I believe the mortician has gone down to receive the body.
