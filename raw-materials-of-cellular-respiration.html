16. briefly describe the first step of cellular respiration,emphasizing the location,raw materials needed and the end products


Glycolysis is the first stage of the whole process of cellular respiration and takes place in the cytosol of the cell, It is the breaking down of glucose (C6H12O6) to be converted to two molecules pyruvate.

First, the glucose molecule when exposed to the energy and heat, attachment of two phosphate groups from two molecules of ATP occurs and it is now called fructose-1,6-biphosphate.

Then, this molecule is split into two and is now called glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate or DHAP. The DHAP is then converted to pyruvate where along the way, two molecules of NADH and four molecules of ATP are made.

in English:

Glycolysis is the first stage of the whole process of cellular respiration and takes place in the cytosol of the cell, It is the breaking down of glucose (C6H1206) to be converted to two molecules pyruvate.

First, the glucose molecule when exposed to the energy and heat, attachment of two phosphate groups from two molecules of ATP occurs and it is now called fructose-1,6-biphosphate.

in Tagalog:Ang glycolysis ay ang unang yugto ng buong proseso ng paghinga ng cellular at nagaganap sa cytosol ng cell, Ito ang pagbagsak ng glucose (C6H1206) upang mai-convert sa dalawang mga molecule pyruvate.

Una, ang molekula ng glucose kapag nahantad sa enerhiya at init, ang pagkakabit ng dalawang pangkat ng pospeyt mula sa dalawang molekula ng ATP ay nangyayari at tinatawag itong fructose-1,6-biphosphate.

Pagkatapos, ang molekulang ito ay nahahati sa dalawa at ngayon ay tinatawag na glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate o DHAP. Pagkatapos ay ang DHAP ay nai-convert sa pyruvate kung saan kasama, ang dalawang mga molekula ng NADH at apat na mga molekula ng ATP ay ginawa.

29. what are the three stages of cellular respiration?where does each stage occur,and what are the products of each of these stages?what are the raw materials and products of cellular respiration?what stage of cellular respiration generates the most amount of ATP,what does it uses to do this?how do you think cellular respiration is related to photosynthesiswhy is it necessary of cellular respiration to occur 24 hours a day?​


3 Stages of Cellular Respiration:

Glycolysis

- occurs in the cytoplasm

- produces 2 ATP, 2 NADH, and 2 pyruvate molecules

Krebs Cycle  

- takes place in the mitochondria

- two molecules of carbon, three molecules of NADH, one    molecule of FADH2 and one molecule of ATP or GTP are produced.

Electron Transport Chain

- occurs in the inner mitochondrial membrane.

- products of the electron transport chain are water and ATP

Products and Raw materials of Cellular Respiration:

Raw materials:

Glucose Oxygen

Products:

Water Carbon dioxide

Stage that generates the most amount of ATP:

Electron transport Chain produces as much as 34 ATP molecules, compared to only 2 ATP for glycolysis and 2 ATP for Krebs cycle. In this stage, FADH and NADH pass their electron in the electron transport chain. As result, releasing large amount of chemical energy.

How Cellular Respiration related to photosynthesis:

Photosynthesis converts carbon dioxide and water into oxygen and glucose. Glucose is used as food by the plant and oxygen is a by-product. Cellular respiration converts oxygen and glucose into water and carbon dioxide. Water and carbon dioxide are by- products and ATP is energy that is transformed from the process.

Why is it necessary of cellular respiration to occur 24 hours a day?

Because their cells need a constant energy source to stay alive.
