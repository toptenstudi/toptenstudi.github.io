5. Is the square root of 40 rational or irrational?


Answer:

The square root of 40 is an irrational number.

Step-by-step explanation:

Rational and Irrational Numbers

What are rational numbers?

Rational numbers are numbers that can be written as ratios or fractions.

Every whole number is a rational number, because any whole number can be written as a fraction. But not all rational numbers are whole numbers.

Examples:

14 is rational because it can be written as ‌14⁄1‌.

9 is rational because it can be written as ‌‌9⁄1‌.

8.5 is rational because it can be written as the ratio ‌17⁄2‌.

0.333... (3 repeating) is also rational because it can be written as the ratio 1/3.

What are irrational numbers?

Irrational numbers are numbers cannot be written as ratios or fractions.

It can be written as decimal and has endless non-repeating digits to the right of the decimal point.

Examples:

π = 3.1415926535897932384626433832795...

The value of pi is irrational because we cannot write down a simple fraction that equals to pi.

√21 = 4.5825756949558

Going back to the question above, square root of 40, it is irrational because its square root is:

√40 = 6.3245553203367

We cannot write down a fraction for this.
