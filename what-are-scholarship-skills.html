13. What is Scholarship be granted to students with high academic performance.


A scholarship is given to a student because of a reason: the student has qualified for or won it by academic, artistic or athletic ability, or by agreeing to follow a particular career, or has some special ethnic or other characteristic. Scholarships are not given for financial need alone. In contrast, a grant is an entitlement: approval is automatic if the students meet qualifications, based on financial need.

14. What is the problem in obtaining information about scholarship assistance?


Obtaining Scholarship Assistance Issues

----What is the problem in obtaining information about scholarship assistance?----

There are several potential problems that individuals may encounter when trying to obtain information about scholarship assistance. Some common issues include:

Limited information: There may be a lack of information available about scholarships, particularly for certain groups of people or for specific fields of study.

Confusing or conflicting information: The information that is available may be difficult to understand, or there may be conflicting information from different sources.

Difficulty in finding relevant scholarships: There may be a large number of scholarships available, making it difficult for individuals to find ones that are relevant to their specific needs.

Difficulty in applying for scholarships: The application process for scholarships may be complicated or time-consuming, making it difficult for individuals to complete.

Limited funding: There may be limited funding available for scholarships, making it difficult for individuals to secure the financial assistance they need.

Deadlines and specific criteria may be too restrictive, making it hard for many students to apply, and the process may be too competitive.
