Type of Optical Image

The apparent reproduction of an object formed by a lens or mirror system from reflected, refracted, or diffracted light waves is known as an optical image. Real and virtual images are the two types of images.

Here are the answers to the following questions:

1. image form in the optical sensor of the camera - REAL

2. eyepiece of a telescope - REAL

3. side mirror of a vehicle - VIRTUAL

4. vanity mirror - VIRTUAL

5. objective lens of a microscope - REAL

6. magnifying lens - REAL

7. contact lenses - REAL

8. eyeglasses - REAL

9. security mirror in a convenient store - VIRTUAL

10. improvised periscope - REAL

Real Optical Image

The actual intersection of light rays creates a real image. It is available on a screen.

Virtual Optical Image

When light rays appear to originate from a point but do not actually meet, a virtual image is formed. It is visible to human eyes. As a result, rearview mirrors create virtual images.

18. what are the two types of optical image?explain each type of image formation.​


Optical image, the apparent reproduction of an object, formed by a lens or mirror system from reflected, refracted, or diffracted light waves. There are two kinds of images, real and virtual.

A real image is the collection of focus points actually made by converging rays, while a virtual image is the collection of focus points made by extensions of diverging rays. ... A real image occurs where rays converge, whereas a virtual image occurs where rays only appear to diverge.
