I think I am more of a allocentric tourist rather than psychocentric tourist

Before we go on, let me try to correct the original question that the correct term is "allocentric" instead of "allocentrie". Going back to the main topic, generally there are two types of tourists in terms of how they travel. The first one is allocentric tourist, and the second is psychocentric tourist.

Allocentric tourist

Allocentric tourists are tourists who tend to be more adventurous and like to experiment with how they are traveling or visiting a destination. Allocentric tourists would not mind exploring the town or tourist sites nearby, trying out newly found food there, as well as taking airplanes as the main transportation to travel.

Psychocentric tourist

Meanwhile, psychocentric tourists are keen to enjoy their travel or holiday while choosing the most calm and no-risk means of transportation (definitely not traveling by plane because of safety reasons), do not visit nearby tourist spots much, and tend to be not adventurous.

The differences between the two can be easily remembered by noting that the "calm" type of tourists start with "psycho", because they probably hate being surprised or anything spontaneous while being a tourist. Meanwhile, an allocentric tourist is the exact opposite of that.

As for myself, I associate with being an allocentric tourist more. Although I would rather spend my time not in the hotel, it does not mean that I do not have the time to rest while spending my holiday by traveling, though.

Being an allocentric tourist simply means that I enjoy having new experience while traveling. And these experiences are not limited only to moving from one tourist spot to the next all the time, but enjoying every single step that I take while traveling. For example, trying out new shopping centers in different part of the town, etc.

6. How does transportation links tourists to different tourist attractions?â€‹


Answer:

Transportation plays a crucial role in connecting tourists to different tourist attractions. It provides the physical means for tourists to travel from one place to another, allowing them to access and explore various attractions efficiently. Here are some ways transportation links tourists to different tourist attractions:

Accessibility: Transportation networks such as roads, railways, airports, and waterways provide access to tourist attractions that may be located in different geographical locations. They allow tourists to reach destinations that may be otherwise difficult or impossible to reach without adequate transportation options.

Convenience: Transportation provides convenience to tourists by offering various modes of transport such as buses, trains, ferries, and flights that cater to different preferences, budgets, and schedules. This makes it easier for tourists to plan their trips and visit multiple attractions in a region or country.

Efficiency: Efficient transportation links, such as well-connected highways, railways, or air routes, enable tourists to travel quickly and easily between different tourist attractions, saving time and effort. This enhances the overall travel experience and allows tourists to visit more attractions within a limited timeframe.

Flexibility: Transportation options allow tourists to choose the most suitable mode of transport based on their preferences, budget, and itinerary. For example, tourists can opt for public transportation for budget travel or rent a car for more flexibility and convenience. This flexibility enables tourists to visit various attractions at their own pace and convenience.

Economic impact: Transportation links tourists to different tourist attractions, creating economic opportunities for local communities. Improved transportation infrastructure can boost tourism-related businesses, such as hotels, restaurants, tour operators, and souvenir shops, providing livelihoods for local residents and supporting the local economy.

In summary, transportation serves as a critical link between tourists and different tourist attractions by providing accessibility, convenience, efficiency, flexibility, and creating economic impacts. It plays a significant role in facilitating travel and enhancing the overall tourist experience. So, reliable and well-connected transportation networks are essential for the development and sustainability of tourism in any destination.
