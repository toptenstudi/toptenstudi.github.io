2. SnF2 (Stannous fluoride)​


Answer:

Stannous Fluoride, or Tn(II) Fluoride, is a compound commonly used in toothpastes for the prevention of gingivitis, dental infections, cavities, and to relieve dental hypersensitivity. Although similar in function and activity to Sodium Fluoride (NaF), the conventionally added ingredient in toothpastes, stannous fluoride has been shown to be more effective at stopping and reversing dental lesions. It manages and prevents dental caries and gingivitis by promoting enamel mineralization, reducing gingival inflammation and bleeding through its potential broad-spectrum antibiotic effect and modulation of the microbial composition of the dental biofilm. It is an FDA-approved over-the-counter product.

To identify the elements in each chemical formula, you must look at your periodic table. Element is represented by a symbol (letter/s). For example, the chemical formula of water ([tex]H_{2} O[/tex]) has constituents elements of hydrogen (H) and oxygen (O).

Then if your task is to find the number of atoms present in each element, then you just have to look at its chemical formula and look for its subscript.

What is subscript?

Subscript is the number beside the element symbol. Let's use water as an example. Para hindi ka ma-confused, list down all the elements present in the compound.

Hydrogen (H) = 2Oxygen (O) = 1

Therefore, in the chemical formula [tex]H_{2} O[/tex], water is made up of 2 hydrogen atoms and 1 oxygen atom. If there is no number beside the element symbol, then it is understood that there's only one atom present in the element.

So let's try to answer your question. I want you to learn, so I will just give the answers for numbers 1 and 2. The rest of the numbers will remain unanswered ^_^

Answer:

1. theoretical yield = 16.69 g

2. actual yield = 13.85 g

Explanation:

1. If 9.60g of C7H8 is reacted with excess KMnO4, what is the theoretical yield, in grams of KC7H5O2?

Compute the theoretical yield of KC7H5O2.

theoretical yield = 9.60 g C7H8 × (1 mol C7H8 / 92.140 g C7H8) × (1 mol KC7H5O2 / 1 mol C7H8) × (160.21 g KC7H5O2 / 1 mol KC7H5O2)

theoretical yield = 16.69 g

2. If the percentage yield is 83%, what mass of KC7H5O2 can be expected?

Calculate the actual yield of KC7H5O2.

percent yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100

actual yield = (percent yield / 100)(theoretical yield)

actual yield = (83 / 100)(16.69 g)

actual yield = 13.85 g

18. How do alkanes and alkenes react with potassium permanganate? Explain any differences and reason for such property?


Alkanes and Alkenes with Potassium Permanganate

Alkanes and Potassium Permanganate (KMnO4)

Alkanes do not react with potassium permanganate due to the following reasons:

Alkanes are slightly stable (compared to alkenes) and do not readily react with KMnO4.Their stability is due to presence of only sigma bonds (single bonds) and absence of pi bonds (double bonds).They are saturated hydrocarbons: every C is bonded to many hydrogen atoms as possible.

To be more familiar with alkanes, visit this link: https://brainly.ph/question/241110

Alkenes and Potassium Permanganate (KMnO4)

Alkenes readily react with potassium permanganate because of these factors:

Alkenes are unsaturated hydrocarbons which contain double bonds (pi bond) in their structure.With cold dilute neutral or alkaline KMnO4, alkenes are oxidized and produce alcohol.
