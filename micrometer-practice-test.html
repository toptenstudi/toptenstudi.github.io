1. differentiate test light from micrometer.


A test light is a diagnostic tool used to check for electrical current in a circuit. It consists of a probe with a light at the end that is connected to a power source, usually a battery. When the probe is placed in contact with a live wire or circuit, the light will illuminate to indicate the presence of current.

A micrometer, on the other hand, is a precision measuring instrument used to measure small distances and thicknesses. It consists of a calibrated screw with a rotating thimble and a fixed anvil. The thimble is rotated to apply pressure to the object being measured, and the distance is read on a scale marked on the thimble. Micrometers are commonly used in mechanical and manufacturing industries to measure the dimensions of small parts and components.

In summary, a test light is used to check for electrical current, while a micrometer is used to measure small distances and thicknesses.

24. how is ocular micrometer calibrated using stage micrometer​


Answer:

The inscribed lines on a stage micrometer are exactly 0.01 mm (or 10 m) apart. In order to calibrate the ocular micrometer for a particular objective lens, the ocular and stage micrometers are superimposed, and the number of ocular graduations per stage micrometer graduation is determined


25. what is micrometer caliper? ​


Micrometer, in full micrometer caliper, instrument for making precise linear measurements of dimensions such as diameters, thicknesses, and lengths of solid bodies; it consists of a C-shaped frame with a movable jaw operated by an integral screw. The fineness of the measurement that can be made depends on the lead of the screw—i.e., the amount the spindle moves toward or away from the anvil in one revolution and the means provided for indicating fractional parts of a revolution. The accuracy of the measurements depends on the accuracy of the screw-nut combination.
