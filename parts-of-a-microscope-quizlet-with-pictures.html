2.what are the iluminating parts of microscope?

In a modern microscope it consists of a light source, such as an electric lamp or a light-emitting diode, and a lens system forming the condenser. The condenser is placed below the stage and concentrates the light, providing bright, uniform illumination in the region of the object under observation.

22. magnifying parts of Microscope Illuminating parts of MicroscopeMechanical parts ofMocroscope​


Answer:

1. They have an objective lens (which sits close to the object) and an eyepiece lens (which sits closer to your eye). Both of these contribute to the magnification of the object. The eyepiece lens usually magnifies 10x, and a typical objective lens magnifies 40x.

2. Illuminating parts are a mirror and a condenser lens. The light beams from different sources are reflected back by the mirror into the condenser lens, which focuses the light onto the specimen. There is an iris diaphragm which regulates the illumination of the field of view.

3. base, c-shaped arm and stage.

Explanation:

The microscope is an instrument that allows us to see microscopic objects or those that are not visible to the naked eye. Several discoveries have been made because of the microscope. It has parts that help it carry out this function.

1. Eyepiece contains a lens where the viewer looks through to see the specimen.

2. Objectives contains also lenses that will help in magnifying objects. They are either low power or high power objectives depending on their magnifying capacity.

3. Body tube connects the eyepiece to the objective lenses.

4. Arm supports the microscope and connects the body tube to the base of the microscope.

5. Coarse Adjustment Knob moves the objective lenses closer to the specimen to bring it to general focus.

6. Fine Adjustment Knob fine tunes that focus and increase the details of the specimen by giving a sharper image.

7. Nosepiece is a rotating knob that houses the objective lenses. The viewer can rotate from one objective to another.

8. Stage and stage clips support the slide containing the specimen. The stage is the area where we place the specimen and the stage clips hold the slide in place.

9. Light source or mirror provides illumination or light for us to seep the specimen.

10. Diaphragm allows us to adjust the amount of light that reaches the specimen.

11. Base supports the whole specimen. It where also the light source or is attached
