4. Give meaning,examples and solution 1. rational exponent 2.exponent and base 3.product rule 4.quotient rule 5.power of a power 6.power of a product rule 7.power of a quotient rule


A rational exponent is a fraction used as an exponent to indicate the root of a number. For example, the expression "x^(2/3)" indicates that x is being raised to the 2/3 power, which is equal to the cube root of x.

In an exponential expression, the base is the number being raised to a power, and the exponent is the power to which the base is being raised. For example, in the expression "2^3," the base is 2 and the exponent is 3.

The product rule for exponents states that when multiplying two exponential expressions with the same base, you can add the exponents to find the result. For example, if you want to simplify the expression "x^3 * x^4," you can use the product rule to get "x^(3+4) = x^7."

The quotient rule for exponents states that when dividing two exponential expressions with the same base, you can subtract the exponents to find the result. For example, if you want to simplify the expression "x^5 / x^3," you can use the quotient rule to get "x^(5-3) = x^2."

The power of a power rule states that when you have an expression with an exponent raised to another exponent, you can multiply the two exponents to find the result. For example, if you want to simplify the expression "(x^2)^3," you can use the power of a power rule to get "x^(2*3) = x^6."

The power of a product rule states that when you have an expression with a product raised to an exponent, you can raise each factor in the product to the exponent to find the result. For example, if you want to simplify the expression "(x*y)^2," you can use the power of a product rule to get "x^2 * y^2."

The power of a quotient rule states that when you have an expression with a quotient raised to an exponent, you can raise the numerator and denominator of the quotient to the exponent to find the result. For example, if you want to simplify the expression "(x/y)^3," you can use the power of a quotient rule to get "x^3 / y^3."

17. Make word problems and show the solution for: Product of powers rule Quotient of powers rule Power of a power rule Power of a product rule Power of a quotient rule Zero power rule Negative exponent rule


Answer:

Product of powers rule:

Word problem: Jane is a chemistry student and has to calculate the surface area of different types of molecules. The formula requires multiplying two numbers with exponents. The first number is 10 raised to the power of 3, and the second number is 10 raised to the power of 2.

- What is the product of these two numbers?

Solution: Using the product of powers rule, we can add the exponents when multiplying two numbers with the same base. So, 10 raised to the power of 3 multiplied by 10 raised to the power of 2 equals 10 raised to the power of (3+2) which is equal to 10 raised to the power of 5. Therefore, the product of 10 raised to the power of 3 and 10 raised to the power of 2 is equal to 10 raised to the power of 5.

Quotient of powers rule:

Word problem: John is working on a science project and needs to divide two numbers with exponents. The first number is 8 raised to the power of 5, and the second number is 8 raised to the power of 2.

- What is the quotient of these two numbers?

Solution: Using the quotient of powers rule, we can subtract the exponents when dividing two numbers with the same base. So, 8 raised to the power of 5 divided by 8 raised to the power of 2 equals 8 raised to the power of (5-2) which is equal to 8 raised to the power of 3. Therefore, the quotient of 8 raised to the power of 5 and 8 raised to the power of 2 is equal to 8 raised to the power of 3.

Power of a power rule:

Word problem: Emily is studying for her math test and needs to evaluate a power raised to another power. The base is 4 and it is raised to the power of 3, and this result is raised to the power of 2.

- What is the value of this expression?

Solution: Using the power of a power rule, we can multiply the exponents when raising a power to another power. So, (4 raised to the power of 3) raised to the power of 2 equals 4 raised to the power of (3x2) which is equal to 4 raised to the power of 6. Therefore, the value of (4 raised to the power of 3) raised to the power of 2 is equal to 4 raised to the power of 6.

Power of a product rule:

Word problem: Robert is a construction worker and needs to calculate the volume of a rectangular prism. The base has a length of 4 meters and a width of 3 meters, and the height is raised to the power of 2.

- What is the volume of the rectangular prism?

Solution: Using the power of a product rule, we can distribute the exponent to each factor when raising a product to a power. So, the volume of the rectangular prism is equal to the product of the base area (4 x 3) raised to the power of 2. This is equal to (12) raised to the power of 2, which is equal to 144. Therefore, the volume of the rectangular prism is 144 cubic meters.

Power of a quotient rule:

A company has 3 divisions, each with different number of employees. Division A has 300 employees, division B has 150 employees, and division C has 75 employees. What is the total number of employees per division, raised to the power of -2?

Solution: We can calculate the total number of employees per division by adding the number of employees for each division: 300 + 150 + 75 = 525. Then, we can apply the power of a quotient rule: (525/3)^(-2) = (175)^(-2) = 1/175^2 = 0.0000256.

Zero power rule:

A construction worker is tasked to lay out 10 bricks, but he mistakenly put zero bricks on the first row. How many ways can he arrange the bricks on the remaining 4 rows if the first row is still empty?

Solution: Since there are no bricks on the first row, we can consider it as having zero bricks. Therefore, the number of ways to arrange the remaining 10 bricks on the 4 rows is simply 10^4 = 10,000 ways, using the zero power rule.

Negative exponent rule:

A student is trying to solve a physics problem that involves converting units. He needs to convert 5 meters to centimeters but he only remembers the conversion factor from centimeters to meters. The conversion factor is 1 cm = 0.01 m. What is the value of (1/0.01)^3?

Solution: To convert meters to centimeters, we need to multiply by the conversion factor 100 cm/m. Since we only remember the conversion factor from centimeters to meters, we can take the reciprocal to get the conversion factor from meters to centimeters: 1 m/100 cm = 0.01 m/cm. Therefore, (1/0.01)^3 = (100)^3 = 1,000,000.

21. Search and find the meaning of the following1.Exponent2.Laws of exponent3.Product rule4.Quomtient rule5.Power rule6.Negative exponent rule7.Zero exponent rule​


Answer:

An exponent is a mathematical notation indicating that a quantity is to be multiplied by itself a certain number of times. For example, in the expression "2^3", the "3" is the exponent and it represents "2" multiplied by itself three times (2 * 2 * 2 = 8).

The laws of exponents are mathematical rules that govern the behavior of exponents when they are combined in different ways. The laws include:

Product rule: When two numbers with the same base are multiplied, you can add their exponents to find the exponent of their product. For example, (a^m) * (a^n) = a^(m + n)

Quotient rule: When two numbers with the same base are divided, you can subtract the exponent of the denominator from the exponent of the numerator to find the exponent of the quotient. For example, (a^m) / (a^n) = a^(m - n)

Power rule: When a number with an exponent is raised to another exponent, you can multiply the exponents to find the exponent of the result. For example, (a^m)^n = a^(m * n)

The product rule states that when two numbers with the same base are multiplied, you can add their exponents to find the exponent of their product.

The quotient rule states that when two numbers with the same base are divided, you can subtract the exponent of the denominator from the exponent of the numerator to find the exponent of the quotient.

The power rule states that when a number with an exponent is raised to another exponent, you can multiply the exponents to find the exponent of the result.

The negative exponent rule states that a number with a negative exponent is equal to 1 divided by that number with the corresponding positive exponent. For example, (a^-m) = 1 / (a^m)

The zero exponent rule states that any number raised to the power of zero is equal to 1. For example, a^0 = 1, where "a" can be any number.
