9. Round all of these to the nearest thousandth 10. 597 20. 2553 554. 2620 11. 2475 20. 3842.


MATH - ROUNDING OFF NUMBERSAnswer:

Round all of these to the nearest thousandth

10. 597 → 10. 597

20. 2553 → 20. 255

554. 2620 → 554. 262

11. 2475 → 11. 248

20. 3842 → 20. 384

Step-by-step explanation:

The third digit to the right of the decimal point is the thousandths place. Always remember, thousands and thousanths (with "th") is different.

Step 1 : Identify what is being asked

In this task, we are tasked to round all of these to the nearest thousandth.

Step 2: Identify what are the given facts

10. 597

20. 2553

554. 2620

11. 2475

20. 3842

Step 3: Identify the thousandths place. It is located on the third digit to the right of the decimal point. The underlined digit/number is the thousandths place.

10. 597

20. 2553

554. 2620

11. 2475

20. 3842

Step 4: Identify the digit to the right of the thousandths place. If the digit to the right of the thusandths place (ten thousanths place) is null, or 0-4, it will be rounded down to their original thousandths place since it is nearer than the next upper digit. 5-9 is nearer than the next digit, so we will round up (+1)

10. 597 → 10. 597

There is no thousandths, so we will retain this form.

20. 2553 → 20. 255

The ten thousandths place is 3, therefore we shall round down and just remove the 3 (ten thousandths place). Retain the other numbers.

554. 2620 → 554. 262

The ten thousandths place is 0, therefore we shall round down and just remove the 3 (ten thousandths place). Actually removing the zero will not affect the rounded value since it is just equal. Retain the other numbers.

11. 2475 → 11. 248

The ten thousandths place is 3, therefore we shall round up, adding 1 to the thousandths place and just remove the 5 (ten thousandths place). Retain the other numbers.

20. 3842 → 20. 384

The ten thousandths place is 2, therefore we shall round down and just remove the 2 (ten thousandths place). Retain the other numbers.
