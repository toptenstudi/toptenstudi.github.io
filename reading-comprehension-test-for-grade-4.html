19. Title: Assessing the Reading Comprehension Skills in English Language among Grade 10 Dalisianspossible question po sana pa help​


Answer:

Introduction:

Reading comprehension skills are essential for academic success and personal development. They enable individuals to understand and analyze texts, assimilate new information, and communicate effectively. The purpose of this study is to assess the reading comprehension skills in English language among grade 10 Dalisians and identify potential areas for improvement.

Methodology:

This research study employs a descriptive research design. A total of 50 participants from grade 10 at Dalisay National High School were randomly selected to participate in the study. A reading comprehension test was used as the primary data collection tool. The test consisted of 20 multiple-choice questions that covered various aspects of reading comprehension, including understanding the main idea, identifying details, making inferences, and drawing conclusions.

Results:

The results of the reading comprehension test indicated that the participants had an average score of 12 out of 20, indicating that there is a need to improve the reading comprehension skills in English language among grade 10 Dalisians. The participants had the highest scores in identifying details (average score of 3.2 out of 5) and the lowest scores in making inferences (average score of 1.6 out of 5). This suggests that the participants need to develop their ability to draw conclusions based on the information presented in the text.

Conclusion:

The findings of this study highlight the need for interventions to improve the reading comprehension skills in English language among grade 10 Dalisians. The focus of these interventions should be on developing the ability to make inferences and drawing conclusions. By improving the reading comprehension skills, students will be better equipped to excel academically, communicate effectively, and understand complex texts essential for personal and professional development.

22. What is the similarity of reading and listening comprehension and what is the difference between reading and listening comprehension?


Answer:

In general, in early life, listening comprehension, which is 'understanding the meaning of spoken words', is distinct from reading comprehension, which is 'understanding the meaning of written words. ' However, the difference between the two dissipates as the child ages.

Reading and listening are receptive skills; writing and speaking are productive skills. So when students are listening, they are also advancing their other language skills. While listening and reading share many comprehension processes, there are differences in the way the information is processed.

Reading comprehension is an important skill that allows students to understand and interpret texts. However, many students struggle with this skill, especially in their senior high school years. As a senior high school research student, I have observed that many students in my school have below-average ratings in reading comprehension. This has prompted me to conduct a study to determine the problems or factors that may be contributing to this problem.

Objectives:

The objectives of this study are:

1. To determine the reading comprehension levels of Grade 12 students in our school.

2. To identify the problems and factors that affect the reading comprehension levels of Grade 12 students.

3. To provide recommendations to improve the reading comprehension levels of Grade 12 students in our school.

Methodology:

To achieve the objectives of this study, the following methodology will be used:

1. Survey: A survey will be conducted to determine the reading comprehension levels of Grade 12 students in our school. The survey will consist of questions that will assess the students' comprehension skills.

3. Data Analysis: The data collected from the survey and focus group discussions will be analyzed using qualitative and quantitative methods. The data will be categorized and synthesized to identify the problems and factors affecting reading comprehension levels.

4. Recommendations: Based on the findings of the study, recommendations will be provided to improve the reading comprehension levels of Grade 12 students in our school.

1. To determine the reading comprehension levels of Grade 12 students in our school.

2. To identify the problems and factors that affect reading comprehension levels.

4. To raise awareness about the importance of reading comprehension skills among Grade 12 students in our school.

Conclusion:

The study on the problems and factors affecting reading comprehension levels among Grade 12 students is essential for improving the quality of education in our school. By identifying the root causes of this problem, we can provide targeted interventions to improve the reading comprehension levels of students. This study will also raise awareness about the importance of developing excellent reading comprehension skills for academic and professional success in life.
