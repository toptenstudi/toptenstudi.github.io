Answer:

1. Intake stroke: the intake stroke draws air and fuel into the combustion chamber. The piston descends in the cylinder bore to evacuate the combustion chamber. When the inlet valve opens, atmospheric pressure forces the air-fuel charge into the evacuated chamber.

2. Exhaust stroke: during the exhaust stroke, the inlet valve remains closed whilst the exhaust valve opens. The moving piston pushes the burned fumes through the now open exhaust port and another intake stroke starts again. During one cycle, the piston makes two round trips and the crankshaft revolves twice.

3. During the intake stroke, the fuel and air mixture is drawn into the cylinder with the exhaust valve closed. Then the air and fuel mixture is compressed in a compression stroke. At the top of the stroke, the spark plug ignites the mixture.

4. Compression

During the compression phase, the piston moves back to TDC. In the process, it pushes the air-fuel mixture into a smaller and smaller space. This compression increases the pressure of the mixture, as well as its temperature

5. As the piston travels back down the cylinder, the exhaust valve goes fully shut and the intake valve goes fully open and starts to shut. Compression stroke. The piston is at BDC and starts to travel up the cylinder. The exhaust valve is still shut and the intake valve goes fully shut.

6. Power Stroke

Because the intake and exhaust valves are closed, the combustion of the fuel takes place in a totally enclosed (and nearly constant volume) vessel. The combustion increases the temperature of the exhaust gases, any residual air in the combustion chamber, and the combustion chamber itself.

7. In a spark ignition engine, the fuel is mixed with air and then inducted into the cylinder during the intake process. After the piston compresses the fuel-air mixture, the spark ignites it, causing combustion. The expansion of the combustion gases pushes the piston during the power stroke.

8. The explosive fuel-air mixture may be ignited either by an electric spark or by the resulting compression temperature. In reciprocating engines the explosion causes the rotation of some engine parts by driving the piston in the cylinder. The motion is transmitted to the crankshaft by means of the connecting rod.

9. Exhaust stroke: during the exhaust stroke, the inlet valve remains closed whilst the exhaust valve opens. The moving piston pushes the burned fumes through the now open exhaust port and another intake stroke starts again. During one cycle, the piston makes two round trips and the crankshaft revolves twice.

10. Exhaust stroke: during the exhaust stroke, the inlet valve remains closed whilst the exhaust valve opens. Gas Compression

Gas compression is a method usually done in order to increase the pressure of the gas. Change in the gas's state, temperature, and volume are the results of this process. The change in physical state of gas depends whether the process is under adiabatic, isothermal, or polytropic conditions.

Gas compression using piston is also done to illustrate, explain, and describe the pressure-volume work.

When compressing a gas using a cylinder and a piston, the piston is being pushed up and down. As the piston goes down, the gas inside the cylinder is being compressed; raising its temperature and decreasing its volume.

There will be a time that the piston will rise. As the piston rise, the compressed gas will expand. Expansion is the opposite of compression. Expansion of the gas will lower its temperature and increase its volume.

The change of pressure and volume through this process is the one being used to compute for the pressure-volume work.

The compression stroke is being done in 4-stroke engine. A four-stroke engine is an example of internal combustion engine that uses four piston strokes. These strokes includes the intake, compression, power, and exhaust. In order to complete one operating cycle, all of these four must be satisfied. The piston make two complete passes in the cylinder to complete one operating cycle.
