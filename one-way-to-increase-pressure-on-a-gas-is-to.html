Three Ways to Increase the Pressure of a Gas Increase gas quality. In the equation this is expressed by "n." Adding more gaseous molecules increases the amount of collisions between the container's molecules and walls. This heightens the strain. Raising the gas temperature. In the equation this is expressed by "T." Rising temperatures add energy to the molecules of air, increasing their movement and, again, increasing collisions. Dismantle the gas capacity. In the equation that is the "V." Gasses can be compressed by their very nature, and if the same gas can be packed into a smaller jar, a greater pressure would be exerted. The gas molecules are forced to move closer together, through collisions (force) and heat.
