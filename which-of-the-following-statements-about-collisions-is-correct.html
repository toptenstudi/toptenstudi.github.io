5. A loaded truck collides with a car causing huge damage to the car. which of the following is true about the collision?


Newton's Third Law of Motion

The answer is the force on the truck is the same in magnitude as the force on the car.

The force exerted when the truck and car collide has a direct relationship to the impulse experienced by the objects during the collision.

Formula:

J = Ft

Where,

• J is the impulse that the item object

• T is the time

The impulse that an object experience increases as the force that is applied to it increases. The force acting on the heavy truck and the car is equal in size but opposing in direction, according to Newton's third rule of motion. As a result, we can say that the force acting on the truck is of a similar size as the force acting on the vehicle.
