12. Laws that creat law enforcement in the Philippinesâ€‹


Answer:

The Philippine National Police (PNP) is the primary law enforcement agency in the Philippines. It was established under Republic Act No. 6975 and is empowered by Section 24 to use force in making arrests and investigation and prevention of crime.

Under the Revised Penal Code and the Philippine National Police Operational Procedures, the PNP must generally issue a verbal warning before it can use force against the offender. Furthermore, it is only allowed to use such force as would be necessary and reasonable to overcome resistance put up by the offender; subdue the clear and imminent danger posed by him; or to justify the force/act under the principles of self-defence or defence of others.

29. What is enforcement of the law and intent of the law?â€‹


Enforcement of the law is the process of enforcing laws, regulations, rules, and other legal mandates. It involves the use of physical force, such as arrests, imprisonment, and fines, as well as non-physical means, such as public education and persuasion. The intent of the law is the meaning or purpose behind the law, which is why it was created in the first place. This can be derived from a combination of the text of the law itself, court interpretations, and any underlying policy rationales that formed the basis for its enactment.

Answer:

Enforcement of the law refers to the actions taken by law enforcement agencies to ensure compliance with the law, while intent of the law refers to the purpose or objective behind the creation of a particular law.
