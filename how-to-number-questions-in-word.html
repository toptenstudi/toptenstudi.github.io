Divide: Divide your number by one of the square roots you've chosen from the previous step.

Average: Take the average of step 2 and the root.

Repeat: Keep repeating steps 2 and 3 using the results you got from step 3 until you get a number that's accurate enough for you to answer the question.l

Step 1: Estimate 10 lies between perfect squares of 3^2 and 4^2.

Step 2: Divide

Divide 10 by 3. We get 3.33

Step 3: Average

Average 3.33 and 3, which gives us 3.1667

Step 4: Repeat

To get a more accurate number, keep repeating step 2 and 3.

How can you subtract a similar fraction, dissimilar fraction and mixed number? 3. How can you multiply a fraction and mixed numbers? 4. How can you divide a fraction and mixed numbers?​


✏️FRACTIONS==============================

» Answer the following questions in your words as comprehensive as possible.

#1: How can you add a similar fraction and dissimilar fraction?

To add similar fractions, just add the numerators and leave the denominator to its original number. For dissimilar fractions however, you need to find the LCD of these fractions to make it similar.

#2: How can you subtract a similar fraction, dissimilar fraction and mixed number?

Like adding, you can operate subtraction if the fractions are similar. If it is mixed, convert it into an improper fraction.

#3: How can you multiply a fraction and mixed numbers?

Multiplying fractions is easy. Multiply the fractions numerator to numerator and denominator to denominator. Convert into an improper fraction if there is a mixed number.

#4: How can you divide a fraction and mixed numbers?

To easily divide fractions, convert the operation into multiplication and then the reciprocal of the divisor. Convert into an improper fraction if there is a mixed number.
