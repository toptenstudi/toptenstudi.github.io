9. Pa explain po at example 1. THE SOURCE - in a teaching learning situation the teacher is the SOURCE of communication by originating or perceiving an idea or purpose which she wants to communicate in order to produce the particular response in the learner2. THE MESSAGE - The goal intent or purpose to be communicated by the teacher is express through a message, in reality the message is a physical form of words it is the translation of the ideas ​


Explanation:

The communication process consists of several elements that work together to convey a message from a sender to a receiver. The two elements you mentioned are the source and the message. I will explain each of them and give an example.

1. The source: The source is the person who originates or perceives an idea or purpose that they want to communicate. In a teaching and learning situation, the teacher is the source of communication since they are the ones who have the knowledge and skills that they want to pass on to their students. The teacher uses various methods like verbal instructions, visual aids, and demonstrations, to convey their message to the students.

For example, a teacher teaching a science class will be the source of information for the students. They will explain scientific concepts and theories to the students, and provide examples to help them understand the topic better.

2. The message: The message is the information that the source wants to communicate. It is the goal, intent, or purpose that the source wants to achieve through communication. In a teaching and learning situation, the message is usually in the form of words, but it can also be conveyed through visual aids, demonstrations, or other means.

For example, the message that the science teacher wants to communicate to the students may be about the process of photosynthesis in plants. They will explain the process in detail, using words and diagrams to help the students understand the concept better. The message will be conveyed through the teacher's words and actions, and the students will receive the information and process it to gain knowledge and understanding.

19. what is BIAS IN EMPHASIS in purposive communication and example. Thank you so much. ​


Answer:

In purposive communication, bias in emphasis refers to the intentional use of emphasis to influence the audience's perception or understanding of a message. It involves selectively highlighting certain parts of a message while downplaying or omitting others, with the aim of shaping the audience's response.

For example, imagine a politician giving a speech about a proposed tax policy. If the politician wants to emphasize the benefits of the policy to the middle class, they might repeatedly highlight how the policy will reduce taxes for the middle class while downplaying the impact on higher earners. By doing so, they are using bias in emphasis to influence the audience's perception of the policy, with the aim of gaining support from the middle class.

Another example of bias in emphasis could be seen in a company's marketing campaign, where certain features or benefits of a product are highlighted while other less favorable aspects are omitted or played down. By emphasizing the positive aspects of the product and downplaying the negative ones, the company is attempting to influence the audience's perception of the product and increase sales
