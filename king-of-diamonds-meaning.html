20. What is the probability of A king, ace, jack of clubs, or queen of diamonds appears in drawing a single card from a well-shuffled ordinary deck of cards.​


Answer:

5/26

Step-by-step explanation:

A standard deck of cards has 52 cards. There are 4 suits in it, namely clubs (♧), diamonds (♢), hearts (❤), and spades (♤). Each suite has 13 cards, namely, ace, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, jack, queen, and king.

There are 4 kings out of 52 cards. There are also 4 aces out of 52 cards. There is only 1 jack of clubs out of 52 cards. There is also 1 queen of diamonds out of 52 cards. Now, let us add the probabilities and get their sum.

[tex]P_{king} + P_{ace} + P_{jack of clubs} + P_{queen of diamonds}[/tex]

= 4/52 + 4/52 + 1/52 + 1/52

= 10/52

= 5/26

The probability of a king, ace, jack of clubs, or queen of diamonds appearing in drawing a single card from a well-shuffled ordinary deck of cards is 5/26.​

30. PROBABILITY (Grade 10) A card is drawn at random from a deck of 52 playing cards. Find the probability that the card drawn is: a.) an ace or a king b.) a king or a diamond


Answer:

A. An ace or a king= 15.38% or 2/13 or 2:13

B. A king or a diamond= 30.77% or 4/13 or 4:13

Step-by-step explanation:

Formula:

[tex]\frac{number of outcomes favorable to the event}{total number of possible outcomes}[/tex]

A. An ace or a king

There are 4 aces and 4 kings on a deck of 52 playing cards.

Aces and Kings= 8

Total number of playing cards= 52

[tex]\frac{8}{52}[/tex]= 15.38% or 2/13 or 2:13

B. A king or a diamond

There are 4 kings and 13 diamonds, but since there is 1 king on a diamond we will just add the 3 kings and 13 diamonds.

Kings and Diamonds= 16

Total number of playing cards= 52

[tex]\frac{16}{52}[/tex]= 30.77% or 4/13 or 4:13
