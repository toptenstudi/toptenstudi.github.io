History

Before the invention of the sewing machine, people made clothes manually, only using needles as a tool to make them. The needles used to make clothes first came from materials that, for today's standards, are considered unusual, such as stone, copper, bone, or ivory. The needles are used to join animal skins together to make clothes. While the thread used is made from animal muscle fibers.

In 1755, a German national living in England, Charles Weisenthal, patented his invention of a needle specifically designed for use in a machine. In 1790, Thomas Saint, from England, patented his first sewing machine. The tool made by Thomas Saint is a tool that can make holes in animal skins and then insert a needle and thread into them. However, the tool cannot operate properly, so it cannot be accepted by the community.

In 1830, Barthelemy Thimonier succeeded in creating a sewing machine that was practical and easy to use by the general public. This machine of his creation uses only one thread and a needle. However, his sewing machine was not well received by the community, especially the local tailors who made clothes manually. In fact, he almost died when his garment factory was burned down by a number of tailors who were afraid that their business would be competed with by the discovery of his sewing machine.

The most successful invention of the sewing machine occurred in the United States by Elias Howe. His sewing machine uses two threads from opposite directions and uses a slotted needle to thread the thread. The needles used in Howe's machine pierce the fabric and create a kind of loop of thread on the underside of the fabric. This allows the yarn to hold together previously separated fabrics.

There was a patent war between Elias Howe and Isaac Singer when Singer succeeded in making a tool with the same mechanism as Howe's creation. Both of them claim that their creation is the most original. Singer succeeded in making a sewing machine with a needle that can be moved by pedaling a foot pedal. Since then, sewing machine sales have skyrocketed in several cities in America.
