to tack or baste is to make quick, temporary stitching intended to be removed. Tacking is used in a variety of ways: To easily hold a seam or trim in place until it can be permanently sewn, usually with a long running stitch made by hand or machine called a tacking stitch or basting stitch.

12. it is quiet important in successful in sewing. A. running stitch b. basting c. outline stitch d. backs stitch. ​


Answer:               PA BRAINLIEST

All of the stitches listed (running stitch, basting, outline stitch, and backstitch) are important for successful sewing.

Explanation:

The choice of which stitch to use depends on the specific sewing project and desired outcome. The running stitch is a basic stitch used for sewing seams or gathering fabric, while basting is used to temporarily hold fabric pieces together. The outline stitch is used for decorative purposes or to secure edges, and the backstitch is used for creating strong seams or for adding decorative detail.
