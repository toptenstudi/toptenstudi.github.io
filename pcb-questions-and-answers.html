6. PCBs are dumped into water. Algae have 100 times more PCBs than water Salmon, 5000 times more PCBs than algae. Salmon feeds on small fish. Small fish have 1000 times more PCBs than algae. They feed on algae. Figure 11. PCB (Polychlorinated biphenyl) dumped in lakes 14. The figure above shows the buildup of pollutants in organisms at higher trophic levels in a food chain. What do you call this phenomenon? A. biological magnification B. fish kill C. eutrophication D. red tide​


Answer:

The phenomenon shown in the figure is called biological magnification or biomagnification. This refers to the process by which certain substances, such as toxins or pollutants, become increasingly concentrated in the tissues of organisms at higher levels in the food chain. In this case, the PCBs are being dumped into the water and are taken up by algae, which are then consumed by small fish. The PCBs become increasingly concentrated in the tissues of the small fish, and when the small fish are consumed by larger fish like salmon, the concentration of PCBs in the salmon becomes even higher. This is an example of how pollutants can accumulate and become more concentrated as they move up the food chain.
