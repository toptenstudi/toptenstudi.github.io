Cause of the Stormy Day

Unstable air forms when warm, moist air is near the ground and cold, dry air is up above. To create a thunderstorm, the unstable air needs to have a nudge upward. This lift usually comes from differences in air density. Warmer, less dense air rises upward, creating lift.

Effect of the Stormy Day

The immediate effects include breakage of tree and shrub limbs and other plants. Branches that were damaged but not broken off completely may continue to dry out and die back over time.

19. Cause and effect stormy day answer in englist 1-5 ​


Answer:

1. Cause: A storm front moves in with low pressure and high winds.

Effect: Strong winds and heavy rains make it difficult to travel and lead to power outages and potential property damage.

2. Cause: A warm front collides with a cold front.

Effect: A storm system develops, leading to thunderstorms and possibly tornadoes.

3. Cause: Moist air from the ocean rises and cools.

Effect: Clouds form and release precipitation in the form of rain or snow.

4. Cause: High temperatures and humidity.

Effect: Storms form due to the increase in instability in the atmosphere leading to thunderstorms, lightning, and strong winds.

5. Cause: Jet stream moves over a region

Effect: Storms develop and can bring severe weather such as heavy rain, strong winds, and even tornadoes.

All these causes are the meteorological phenomena that can lead to the formation of a stormy day. It's important to note that stormy day is a general term and different types of storms can have different causes and effects.
