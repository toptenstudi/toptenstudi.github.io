2. Animals have the following except


Animals have the following (part of cell) except have cold cells (a characteristic feature).

EXPLANATION ABOUT PART OF ANIMALS CELL:

Animal cells are one part of a group of eukaryotic cells that have DNA in the cell nucleus and surrounded by a nuclear membrane called the nucleus. Well detikers, the structure of animal cells is not the same as other eukaryotic cell kingdoms, including plant cells.

What are the differences between animal cells and plant cells?

The difference between animal cells and plant cells is seen in the part of the cell wall that animal cells do not have. Plant cells have a cell wall whose main structural component is cellulose. This component makes the cell wall rigid, strong and not actively moving.

Characteristics of Animal Cells

The main role of animal cells is as a basic functional unit that helps the development of the body in animals. Animal cells are also known as the smallest organelles that are covered by a thin membrane. The inside has a colloidal solution with chemical compounds. Animal cells have their own advantages, namely the ability to duplicate independent cells by the process of division.

A. Cell wall

Explanation:

Because the cell wall surrounds the plasma membrane of plant cells and provides tensile strength and protection against mechanical and osmotic stress. It also allows cells to develop turgor pressure, which is the pressure of the cell contents against the cell wall.
