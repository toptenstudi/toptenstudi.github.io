7. Angela tests a mineral sample by scratching it to white and black porcelain plate to determine its true color. What property of minerals did she test?  ​


Answer:

Streak

Explanation:

Streak (or powder color) is a test used to identify minerals by checking the color of a mineral in its powdered form. This process is usually done by rubbing the sample mineral on a hard, white surface called streak plate to yield a line, or streak, of fine powder. Hard surfaces usually used in the streak test is a tile of unglazed porcelain.

The external color of a mineral could have been affected by weathering and oxidation. By checking the mineral's color in it's powdered form, the sample's true color is revealed which is a great aid in the identification of the mineral.

An example is the mineral calcite. Depending on its impurities, calcite may have different colors but all varieties of calcite will only produce one color in the streak test: White.

What is Rock?

It is naturally occurring mixtures of minerals, mineraloids, glass or organic matter. Rocks are made up of one or more minerals. Almost all rocks made of minerals, but different rocks contain different mixtures of minerals. Granite, for example, consists of quartz, feldspar, and mica. Scientists can distinguish more than 4,000 different minerals but many are very rare. About 200 minerals make up the bulk of most rocks.
