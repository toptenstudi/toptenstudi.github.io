I will introduce you to my boss this week. active passive9. It would have been fixed at the weekend. active passive10. The national anthem is being sung by Jason this time. active pasive​


Explanation:

ACTIVE VOICE

- In active voice, the subject performs the action or the verb on the object. Thus, it follows this format,

subject+verb+object

It tells us what someone or something does.

PASSIVE VOICE

- In passive voice, it is the subject that is being acted upon. Hence, it follows this format.

object+verb+subject

It tells us what is/was done on a person or thing.

Answers:

1. Active (The subject (I) performs the action (ate) on the object (cake).)

2. Active (The subject (librarian) performs the action (read the book) on the object (students).)

3. Passive (The subject (money) was being acted upon (stolen).)

4. Active (The subject (They) performs the action (are paid).)

5. Passive (The subject (movie) was being acted upon (is being made).)

6. Active (The subject (I) performs the action (washed) on the object (my car).)

7. Passive (The subject (his hair) was being acted upon (was cut.)

8. Active (The subject (I) performs the action (introduce) on the object (you).)

9. Passive (The subject (It) was being acted upon (would have been fixed)

10. Passive (The subject (national anthem) was being acted upon (is being sung)
