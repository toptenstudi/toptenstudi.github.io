6. King, Queen and Prince, partners, have capital balances of P400,000, P200,000 and P100,000, respectively, and share profits and losses in a 2:1:1 ratio. Liabilities amount to P500,000, including the loan of P50,000 extended by Prince. The cash balance is P300,000 and it increased to P1,050,000 as a result of the sale of the non-cash assets. How much were the non-cash assets of the partnership?Please pahelp po :')​


Answer:

We can start by computing the total capital of the partnership, which is:

Total capital = King's capital + Queen's capital + Prince's capital

Total capital = P400,000 + P200,000 + P100,000

Total capital = P700,000

Next, we can compute the total liabilities, which is:

Total liabilities = Liabilities - Prince's loan

Total liabilities = P500,000 - P50,000

Total liabilities = P450,000

We can now compute the total equity of the partnership, which is:

Total equity = Total capital - Total liabilities

Total equity = P700,000 - P450,000

Total equity = P250,000

Since the cash balance increased to P1,050,000 as a result of the sale of non-cash assets, we can compute the value of the non-cash assets as:

Non-cash assets = Cash balance - Total equity

Non-cash assets = P1,050,000 - P250,000

Non-cash assets = P800,000

Therefore, the non-cash assets of the partnership were worth P800,000.
