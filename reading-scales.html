SOLUTION:

[tex]C=\frac{^{\circ}F-32}{1.8}\\C=\frac{90-32}{1.8}\\C=\frac{58}{1.8}\\\boxed{\boldsymbol{C=32.2^{\circ}C}}[/tex]

Problem:

A girl stands with both feet on a weighing scale the scale reads 500 N

Note:

In this problem I am going to compute for the mass of the Girl.

Given Data:

a ( acceleration gravity) = 9.8 m/s²

F ( Force ) = 500 N or 500 kg·m/s²

m ( mass ) = ? unknown

Solution:

To solve this problem, we can use the formula Force = acceleration × mass ( F=ma ). To find mass, we can use the formula m = F/a

m = F/a

   = 500 kg·m/s² / 9.8 m/s²

   = 51.02 kg

Newton's 2nd law explains that the acceleration produced by the resultant force on an object is proportional and in line with the resultant force and inversely proportional to the mass of the object

∑F = m. a

F = force, N

m = mass = kg

a = acceleration, m / s²

In elevators there are two possible movements:

1. upward

then the forces that work

N - W = ma

N - mg = ma

N = ma + mg

N = m (a + g) ... equation 1

in this condition shows N> W

2. down ward

then the forces that work

w - N = ma

mg - N = ma

N = mg - ma

N = m (g - a) ... equation 2

in this condition shows N <W
