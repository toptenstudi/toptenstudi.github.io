16. science activity 2 triple venn diagram compare and contrast the properties of solid liquid and gas ​


The first circle

could represent the properties of solids, such as a defined shape, a fixed volume, and limited movement of particles.

The second circle

could represent the properties of liquids, such as an indefinite shape, a fixed volume, and more movement of particles than solids.

The third circle

could represent the properties of gases, such as an indefinite shape, an indefinite volume, and the most movement of particles among the three states of matter.

The size of each overlapping section indicates the degree to which a particular property is present. This diagram helps understand and remember the unique properties of each state of matter and their relationships.
