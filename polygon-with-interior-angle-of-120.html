19. the measureof each angle in aregular hexagon is 120 which of the following equation is used in solving the interior angles of a regular polygon?​


Answer:

Formula for sum of interior angles is:

Sum of Interior Angles = (n-2)×180°

where n = # of sides of the polygon

To solve for each interior angle, we simply divide the sum of interior angles by n,

Each Interior Angle = Sum of Interior Angles / n

So for Hexagon, it has six sides, so n = 6

Let's solve for the Sum of Interior Angles,

Sum of Interior Angles = (n-2)×180°

Sum of Interior Angles = (6-2)×180°

Sum of Interior Angles = (4)×180°

Sum of Interior Angles = 720°

To solve for Each Interior Angle,

Each Interior Angle = Sum of Interior Angles / n

Each Interior Angle = 720° / 6

Each Interior Angle = 120°

In this activity, they needed to determine the measure of an interior angle of a regular polygon by dividing that polygon into congruent isosceles triangles. Observe, in the figure below, the polygon used in this activity and the interior angles of one of the isosceles triangles that divided this polygon.What is the measure of the interior angle of this polygon that will be determined by the students in this activity?a. 45°.b. 67.5°.c. 120°.d. 135°.​


The measure of the interior angle of this polygon that will be determined by the students in this activity is 135º.

Notice in the figure that there are eight isosceles triangles. Consequently, there are eight angles with measure β. The sum of these angles is equal to 360 degrees, as they are central angles.

That said, we have that the value of β is:

β + β + β + β + β + β + β + β = 360º

8.β = 360º

β = 45º.

The interior angle of this polygon is twice the measure α. To determine this value, it is important to remember that:

When adding all the interior angles of a triangle we find 180º as a result.

In this way, we get:

α + α + β = 180º

2.α + 45º = 180º

2.α = 180º - 45º

2.α = 135º.

Hence, The correct answer is d. 135°.
