Answer:

Six (6) components of A work-based training plan:

a. Goals and objectives

b. Curriculum or training content

c. Assessment or evaluation methods

d. Learning activities or strategies

e. Resources and materials needed

f. Timeline or schedule for completion

Two (2) common modes of delivery:

a. Classroom-based training - where learners are taught in a physical classroom or training room.

b. Online or e-learning - where learners participate in training activities and access training resources using the internet.

Enumerate the uses of ICT in education:

a. Online learning and distance education

b. Research and reference

c. Interactive and multimedia resources

d. Communication and collaboration tools

e. Administrative and management tools

f. Assessment and evaluation tools

g. Learning management systems (LMS)

Six (6) reasons why it is important to know the proper procedures and practices in maintaining training facilities:

a. Safety - proper maintenance ensures that the facility is safe for learners and trainers.

b. Cost-effectiveness - regular maintenance can prevent costly repairs and replacements.

c. Comfort - a well-maintained facility promotes a comfortable learning environment.

d. Professionalism - a well-maintained facility reflects positively on the organization.

e. Compliance - proper maintenance ensures that the facility complies with regulations and standards.

f. Durability - regular maintenance can extend the life of the facility and equipment.

Three (3) important forms and documents relevant to maintenance that you, as a trainer should keep in file:

a. Maintenance logs - documents maintenance activities, including repairs and replacements.

b. Inspection reports - documents the results of regular inspections of the facility and equipment.

c. Work orders - documents requests for maintenance work or repairs.

Why do we need to maintain the training facilities?

Regular maintenance of training facilities ensures that they are safe, comfortable, and effective learning environments for trainers and learners. It also helps to prevent costly repairs and replacements, promotes a professional image, and ensures compliance with regulations and standards.

Nine (9) areas of competency-based training (CBT) facility:

a. Reception or entrance area

b. Offices or administration area

c. Classroom or training room

d. Library or resource center

e. Computer lab or technology center

f. Workshop or laboratory

g. Kitchen or dining area

h. Restrooms or ablution area

i. Outdoor area or space

What should be considered when planning the training facility?

When planning the training facility, it is important to consider factors such as the size and layout of the space, the needs of the learners, the types of training activities that will be conducted, the availability of resources and materials, the safety and comfort of learners and trainers, and compliance with regulations and standards.

Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning (Mayer):

The Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning is a theory that explains how people learn from multimedia materials, such as videos, animations, and graphics. The theory suggests that learning is most effective when information is presented in a way that minimizes cognitive overload and maximizes the use of working memory. This can be achieved by presenting information in a way that is consistent with the learner's existing knowledge, using both visual and auditory channels, and using multimedia materials that are relevant, concise, and organized.

Dale's Cone of Experience:

Dale's Cone of Experience is a visual model that illustrates different types of learning experiences, arranged in a hierarchy based on their level of abstraction. At the base of the cone are concrete experiences, such as direct sensory experiences, and at the top are abstract concepts and ideas. The cone suggests that learners are more likely to retain information and skills when they are exposed to concrete experiences, followed by increasingly abstract and conceptual
