1. Identify what the following statement describes: A fantasy plot that is accepted by the characters. Select one: a. Magic Realism b. Fantasy c. Magic Realism and Realism d. Surrealism e. Magic Realism and Fantasy f. Realism


Magic Realism and Fantasy:

Both magic realism and fantasy posses a fantasy plot that is accepted by the characters.

Fantasy often includes creatures and other species, and often draws from mythology. The setting is usually medieval, and totally not using technology. For instance, the Lord of the Rings by J.R.R Tolkien features several creatures other than human that range from elves to hobbits. Frodo’s world is a medieval world with horses as transportation. Fantasy might be considered as a parent genre. It is a broad, umbrella term that contains several sub - genres, all of which have one thing in common: magical or fantastical things happen.

Magic Realism is a genre that is usually underrepresented and is often mixed up with urban fantasy. These two genres share similar aspects, but looking closely will bring prominent differences. Magic Realism is based in reality with extraordinary events viewed as normal as putting on shoes. Magic is a part of everyday life and is not considered as something fascinating or strange. The true part of the world should play the grandest role in the story, above all magical elements. The setting can be an ordinary location. The characters are the normal everyday people, the ones that you would pass on the street and would not consider. The fantastical elements are part of their daily life. Magic is something they have to handle, in the same manner that they handle their jobs.
