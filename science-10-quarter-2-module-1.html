Pulmonary circulation is the movement of blood from the heart to the lungs. It delivers deoxygenated blood to the lungs, where it is oxygenated and carbon dioxide is released. After then, the oxygenated blood returns to the heart. The heart and the rest of the body are connected through systemic circulation. It transports oxygenated blood to cells while also returning deoxygenated blood to the heart.

To start systemic circulation, the heart pushes oxygenated blood out of the left ventricle and into the aorta. After supplying oxygen and nutrients to cells throughout the body, blood returns deoxygenated blood to the right atrium of the heart. The oxygenated blood travels from the left atrium to the left ventricle below, where it is recirculated throughout the body.

The circulatory and respiratory systems work together to provide oxygen and eliminate carbon dioxide from the body. External respiration is made easier by pulmonary circulation, which allows deoxygenated blood to move into the lungs. It takes in oxygen from small air sacs called alveoli and exhales carbon dioxide.
