First, let us understand the constant position of Debit and Credit in accounting whether you make a journal entry, balance sheet or financial statement. Debit is normally found on  the left side and Credit is normally found on the right side. Each account like asset, liability, Expenses, Revenues and owner's equity and drawing has its own normal balance like for example asset will increase in debit and decrease in credit, liability decrease in debit and increase in credit, expenses increase in debit and decrease in credit, Revenues decrease in debit and increase in credit, owners equity decrease in debit and increase in credit. Now if you closely scrutinized the examples i have given, you can see when to increase and when to decrease. Therefore the answer of your question is LETTER D. Decrease in liability and equity  because liability and owners equity will definitely decrease if you record them on  Debit side. they are all basic flow of accounts all you have to do is to memorized its normal balance so that it is easy for you to determine when to increase an account and when to decrease.
