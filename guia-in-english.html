Baguio City-based artist Kawayan de Guia produces drawings, paintings, sculpture and installations that explore sociopolitical issues in contemporary Filipino society. The artist fuses seemingly opposing ideas, such as consumerism and spiritualism, into collages and assembled installations made from found objects that embody local character. Although broken and discarded, these miscellaneous items are imbued with personal significanceâ€”something de Guia perceives as part of a cultural collective memory, which he aims to galvanize and reactivate through his work. De Guia has completed several residencies, including at the Art Omi International Center in Ghent, New York. He received the Philip Morris Asian Art Award in 2003 and the Ateneo Art Award for Visual Art, Manila, in 2011.
