6. Identify the following compounds as Ionic (I) or Covalent (C) , Then name them. NaCl


Answer:

The compound NaCl is ionic. It is also known as table salt or sodium chloride. Sodium (Na) is a metal and chlorine (Cl) is a nonmetal, so they form an ionic bond by transferring electrons from sodium to chlorine. Ionic compounds are typically solid at room temperature, have high melting and boiling points, and are good conductors of electricity when melted or dissolved in water. They are also usually composed of a metal and a nonmetal.

12. what are the rules for naming simple covalent compounds?​


Rules in Naming Covalent CompoundsName first the nonmetal between the two which is further to the left side of the periodic table. Capitalize the first letter of its elemental name. For example, a covalent compound has atoms of carbon and oxygen. Name first the carbon because it is placed to the left side of the nonmetal group than oxygen. See below:

Carbon | Nitrogen | Oxygen | Fluorine

Then, name the second non-metal element by its name by changing its ending into -ide form. (Check the subtitle below entitled "-IDE FORM OF NON-METALS".) The first letter of the name of the second nonmetal must be in lower case.Add prefixes such as mono-, di-, tri- and etc. to indicate the number of atoms of that element in the molecule.

PrefixesMono- is 1Di- is 2Tri- is 3Tetra- is 4Penta- is 5Hexa- is 6Hepta- is 7Octa- is 8Nona- is 9Deca- is 10

Examples of covalent compounds:

CO2 (subscript 2) --> Carbon + 2 Oxygen atoms

Carbon dioxide

PCl3 (subscript 3) --> Phosphorus + 3 Chlorine atoms

Phosphorus trichloride

-IDE FORM OF NONMETALSC --> CarbideN --> NitrideO --> OxideF --> FluorideP --> PhosphideS --> SulfideCl --> ChlorideSe --> SelenideBr --> BromideI --> Iodide

Happy learning!

14. 5. Give the procedures on how to name binary covalent compounds.​


answer:

Naming Binary Covalent Compounds

Name the non-metal furthest to the left on the periodic table by its elemental name.

Name the other non-metal by its elemental name and an -ide ending.

Use the prefixes mono-, di-, tri-. to indicate the number of that element in the molecule.

If mono is the first prefix, it is understood and not written.
