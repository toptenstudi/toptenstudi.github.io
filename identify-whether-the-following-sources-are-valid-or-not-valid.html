6. why is it important to determine whether your source of information is reliable and valid? ​


Answer:

Because sources are the foundations not only of journalism but humanity itself. When Isaac Newton said the famous quote "If I have seen further it is by standing on the shoulders of Giants.", The underlining is that all his work was made possible because he learned from time-proofed knowledge of other luminaries before him. The "deadline zero" condition of digital made journalism far more error-prone than before because people take things said or showed for granted without being sure that if it's true or not.

Credibility and reliability are slightly different in terms, but for journalism, the value is the same, meaning "if I hear a word from this person/organization, can I take it as true?". It does not mean that the statement made itself reflects truth (think about two political rivals disputing an issue), but it needs to be forcefully information that cannot be denied by the source itself. If we are talking about something less disputable, like economy statistics for a period or the ruling of a court regarding a case, then the information needs to be unequivocal and irrefutable.

Bias is the trickiest because absolutely "everything that walks or crawled at one time or another" (to use a great quote from Clint Eastwood's "Unforgiven") is biased. It's impossible not to be. Good journalists, however, are trained to challenge their own biases when pursuing a story as well as considering different points of view to ensure the article will not miss relevant aspects. However, the bias is there. Take The Economist, by far the best reporting and analysis around. Its world-class journalists need to spouse the radically centrist, liberal, pro-openness principles of the publication itself, but their final output is formidable. The editorials even assume that they have specific views when tackling a subject. However, one thing is sure: they check what they publish and give the basics (especially economics) right. Bias, to an extent, is not a problem, if the person telling the story is aware of it and provides context to ensure the audience can realize it.
