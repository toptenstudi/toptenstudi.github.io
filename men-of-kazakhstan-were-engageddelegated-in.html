25. In a leadership seminar, the ratio of male delegates to the female delegates was 7:8. If there were 40 more female delegates than male delegates, how many delegates were there at the seminar?​


✒️RATIOS

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

In a leadership seminar, the ratio of male delegates to the female delegates was 7:8. If there were 40 more female delegates than male delegates, how many delegates were there at the seminar?

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \:\: \rm{600 \: delegates} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» Let x be the number of males and (x + 40) be the number of females in the ratio of 7:8. Find x.

[tex] 7 \::\: 8 = x \::\: (x + 40) [/tex]

» Multiply the means and the extremes.

[tex] 8x = 7(x + 40) [/tex]

[tex] 8x = 7x + 280 [/tex]

[tex] 8x - 7x = 280 [/tex]

[tex] x = 280 [/tex]

» Therefore, there are 280 males. Find the number of females if it is 40 more than the number of males.

[tex] F = x + 40 [/tex]

[tex] F = 280 + 40 [/tex]

[tex] F = 320 [/tex]

» Thus, there are 320 females in the seminar. Find the total number of delegates.

[tex] 280 \:M + 320 \: F = 600 \: delegates [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] There are 600 delegates in the seminar.
