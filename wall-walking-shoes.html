12. What should I look for in walking shoes?


Answer:

Finding the best walking shoes for you can be a tedious process if you don’t know what you’re looking for. Experts say to try three different pairs before buying. One tip is performing the toe test –the tip of your thumb fits between the end of the shoe and the end of your longest toe.

Explanation:

When you are looking at walking shoes, the most important aspects to consider are comfort and overall fit.  

Different Feet Type

Consider the type of your feet to choose the best walking shoes for your

Neutral-arched feet. Your feet aren't overly arched nor overly flat. Look for shoes with firm midsoles, straight to semi-curved lasts and moderate rear-foot stability. Low-arched or flat feet. Low arches or flat feet may contribute to muscle stress and joint problems in some individuals. If you have significantly flat feet, you may benefit from a walking shoe with a straight last and motion control to help stabilize your feet. High-arched feet. High arches may contribute to excessive strain on joints and muscles especially if you perform a lot of impact or jumping activities.  

Parts of a Shoe

Here are the parts of the shoes you have to check prior to buying:

Achilles tendon protector. Reduces stress on the Achilles tendon by locking the shoe around the heel. Heel collar. Cushions the ankle and ensures proper fit. Upper. Holds the shoe on your foot and is usually made of leather, mesh or synthetic material. Mesh allows better ventilation and is lighter weight. Insole. Cushions and supports your foot and arch. Removable insoles can be laundered or taken out to dry between walking sessions. Gel, foam or air midsole. All of these materials cushion and reduce impact when your foot strikes the ground. Outsole. Makes contact with the ground. Grooves and treads can help maintain traction. Toe box. Provides space for the toes. A roomy and round toe box helps prevent calluses.
