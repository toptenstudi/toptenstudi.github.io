7. 2. What guidelines govern the naming and writing of formulas of ionic compounds?​


Answer:

Rule 1. The cation is written first in the name; the anion is written second in the name.

Rule 2. When the formula unit contains two or more of the same polyatomic ion, that ion is written in parentheses with the subscript written outside the parentheses.

Answer:

Subtract the smaller electronegativity from the larger one to find the difference. For example, if we're looking at the molecule HF, we would subtract the electronegativity of hydrogen (2.1) from fluorine (4.0). 4.0 - 2.1 = 1.9. If the difference is below about 0.5, the bond is nonpolar covalent.

23. 3. A compound is 44.82% Potassium, 18.39% Sulfur, and 36.79% Oxygen Write theempirical formula and name the compound.​


Given:

[tex]\% \: \text{K} = 44.82\%[/tex]

[tex]\% \: \text{S} = 18.39\%[/tex]

[tex]\% \: \text{O} = 36.79\%[/tex]

Required:

empirical formula

Solution:

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\text{mass K = 44.82 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass S = 18.39 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass O = 36.79 g}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element

[tex]n \: \text{K = 44.82 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{39.10 g}} = \text{1.146 mol}[/tex]

[tex]n \: \text{S = 18.39 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{32.07 g}} = \text{0.5734 mol}[/tex]

[tex]n \: \text{O = 36.79 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{16.00 g}} = \text{2.299 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula

[tex]empirical \: formula = \text{K}_{x}\text{S}_{y}\text{O}_{z}[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles

[tex]x = \frac{\text{1.146 mol}}{\text{0.5734 mol}} = 2[/tex]

[tex]y = \frac{\text{0.5734 mol}}{\text{0.5734 mol}} = 1[/tex]

[tex]z = \frac{\text{2.299 mol}}{\text{0.5734 mol}} = 4[/tex]

Step 5: Write the empirical formula

[tex]empirical \: formula = \text{K}_{2}\text{S}\text{O}_{4}[/tex]

The name of the compound is potassium sulfate.
