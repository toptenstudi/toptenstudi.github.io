16. Ethnocentrism is a worldview that can be observed in several countries. To see this, research on how the concept of ethnocentrism is being observed or done in the United States of America, Japan, China and United Kingdom. Provide one example in each country then answer the following questions. 1. What did you discover after your research? 2. Can you see some observations similar to our own culture? Alternative Q: Which countries have similar ethnocentric views with us? Which countries have different ethnocentric views with us? 3. Why do you think they have these ethnocentric practices? What benefit can they get from doing these? 4. As a Filipino, what practices or values you are proud of that are worth sharing with others? 5. How will you promote these values and practices?​


Answer:

USA: The concept of American exceptionalism is an example of ethnocentrism in the United States. It refers to the belief that the United States is inherently superior to other countries in terms of political, economic, and social systems.

Japan: The idea of "nihonjinron" (Japanese uniqueness) is a form of ethnocentrism in Japan. It is a belief in the inherent superiority of Japanese culture, values, and customs.

China: Han nationalism is a form of ethnocentrism in China, which is based on the idea that the Han Chinese are the dominant and superior ethnic group in China, and other ethnic minorities should assimilate to Han culture.
