9. 1. Place a book on top of a table as shown in Figure 2. Figure 1.2: Book on top of a table Q6. Is the book at rest or in motion? Q7.Are there forces acting on the book? If yes, draw the forces acting on the book.


In these case the book and the table are at rest in relation to a frame of reference on Earth. This implies that the forces acting on both the book and the table are balanced. The table is attracted toward the Earth by the fravitational force (weight) of the book and the table itself. At the same time it is pushed upward by the normal force originated by the floor due to the repulsive electrical force between the electrons of the legs incontact with the floor and the electrons on the floor. The two forces acting on the table are equal and opposite and thie sum is zero. So the table stays at rest. In the case of the book, it is acted upon by the gravitational force attracting it downward (its weight) and by the normal force of the table pushing it upward (again due to electrical repulsion between the electrons of both surfaces). The normal force has the same magnitud but opposite direction to the weight of the book. When these two forces are added they equal zero. So the book stays at rest on the table.                                                                                                      I HOPE IT HELPS YOU
