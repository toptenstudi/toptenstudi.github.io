5. A wheel has diameter 84 cm. Find how many complete revolutions must it make to cover 792 metres.


We have,
Diameter of wheel = 84 cm
=> Radius of wheel = [tex] \frac{84}{2 } = 42 \: cm[/tex]

The distance covered in 1 revolution [tex] = 2\pi \: r = 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 44 = 264 \: cm[/tex]
To cover 264 cm, it's number of revolutions = 1
To cover 1 cm, it's number of revolutions [tex] = \frac{1}{264} [/tex]
To cover 79200 cm, it's number of revolutions
[tex] = \frac{79200}{264} = 300[/tex]

Hence, to cover 792 metres, it makes 300 revolutions.

21. A 120 cm piece was cut from a rope measuring 30 m. what is the length in metres of the remaining pieces​


Answer:

If a 120 cm piece is cut from a 30 m rope, then the length of the remaining rope can be found by subtracting 120 cm from 30 m.

First, we need to convert 120 cm to meters:

120 cm = 120/100 m = 1.2 m

Now, we can subtract 1.2 m from 30 m to get the length of the remaining pieces:

30 m - 1.2 m = 28.8 m

Therefore, the length of the remaining pieces is 28.8 meters.
