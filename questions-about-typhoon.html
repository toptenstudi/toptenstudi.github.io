Answer:

1. What do you observe in the movement path of the typhoon? How about the wind speed change?

- From the movement path of the typhoon, it can be observed that it follows a general direction or path, usually towards the west or northwest. However, there are times when the path changes or shifts, depending on various factors such as the presence of other weather systems or the topography of the landmass it encounters.

-In terms of wind speed change, the typhoon typically intensifies as it moves over warm ocean waters and weakens as it encounters landmass or cooler waters.

2. Based on the data provided, what can you conclude about the wind speed of the typhoon as it encounters obstacles such as landmass?

-Based on the data provided, the wind speed of the typhoon typically decreases as it encounters landmass. This is because the friction between the wind and the land surface slows down the wind speed. However, the impact of the wind on the land can still cause damage to infrastructure and property.

3. In the presence of a storm or typhoon, provide counter-measures that you perform in order to secure the safety of you and your family and to minimize damage to property.

-Stay indoors and avoid going outside during the typhoon.

-Secure all loose objects and outdoor furniture to prevent them from being carried by strong winds.

Turn off all electrical and gas appliances to avoid electrical shocks and fires.

-Stock up on food, water, and other emergency supplies such as flashlights, batteries, and first-aid kits.

Make sure that all windows and doors are tightly closed and locked to prevent water from entering the house.

-Stay updated with the latest weather bulletins and evacuation orders from local authorities.
