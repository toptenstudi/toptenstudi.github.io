5. Give at least 3 list of childhood today vs childhood in 1980s ​

Answer:

1. Technology: Children today have grown up with technology, such as smartphones, tablets, and social media, as a central part of their lives. In contrast, children in the 1980s did not have access to the same level of technology, and their entertainment and socialization largely took place offline.

2. Outdoor play: Children in the 1980s often spent more time playing outside and engaging in unstructured play, such as riding bikes, playing ball games, and exploring their neighborhoods. In contrast, children today often spend more time indoors and have more structured play activities, such as organized sports and scheduled playdates.

3. Parental involvement: In recent years, there has been a trend towards more involved parenting, with parents taking an active role in their children's lives and education. This can include activities such as helicopter parenting, where parents closely monitor their children's activities and behavior. In the 1980s, there was less emphasis on parental involvement, and children were often given more freedom to make their own choices and explore their interests independently.
