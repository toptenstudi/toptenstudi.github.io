3. Forms of spindle fibers during cell division (no using goo gle)​


Forms of spindle fibers during cell division

Spindle fibers connect to produce a protein structure that splits a cell's genetic material. During both kinds of nuclear division, mitosis and meiosis, the spindle is required to evenly divide the chromosomes in a mother cell into two daughter cells. The mitotic spindle is the name given to the spindle fibers during mitosis. Meanwhile, the spindle fibers are referred to as the meiotic spindle during meiosis. Two wheel-shaped protein structures called centrioles place themselves at opposing ends of the cell during the start of nuclear division, generating cell poles. Microtubules, long protein fibers, stretch from the centrioles in all directions, producing a spindle, which is what it's called. By linking to protein complexes termed kinetochores, some microtubules join the poles to the chromosomes.
