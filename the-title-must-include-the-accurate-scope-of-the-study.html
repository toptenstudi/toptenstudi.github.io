Milo Marathon: A Sports Event as a  Corporate Social Responsibility

SCOPE AND DELIMITATIONS

This study will focus on the application of Corporate  Social Responsibility on Milo Marathon. Background:

What is a Research?

The scientific method is used to conduct a comprehensive, exhaustive investigation of a specific problem or subject. Learn more about common researchers and notable experts who engage in this process by studying the concept and purpose of research.

The scientific method is used to conduct a comprehensive and detailed investigation of a given topic, concern, or issue. It's the grown-up version of primary school science fair projects, in which you try to learn something by conducting an experiment. This is best performed by turning the problem into a question, with the goal of the study is to find an answer.

What are Scope and Delimitations?

A research paper or thesis' scope and delimitations are two elements. The scope of a study describes the parameters within which the investigation will operate and explains how far the research area will be investigated in the work. Let's imagine a researcher wishes to investigate the impact of mobile phones on elementary school children's behavior patterns. However, the researcher will not be able to cover every facet of the subject.

As a result, the scope will have to be limited to a subset of the target demographic. In this scenario, the scope of the study could be limited to a group of 50 children in grades 3-5 at a single school. Their school conduct habits may have been monitored for up to six months. These would serve as the study's boundaries.

As a result, delimitations are features that limit the scope and specify the study's boundaries, such as sample size, geographical location or setting, population traits, and so on. In addition, the researcher may opt to collect data using some study instruments and procedures but not others.

These boundaries may be set for practical reasons, such as a lack of time or funds to conduct a more thorough study. The study's delimitation section should explain why certain choices were taken while others were not, as well as how this may have influenced the research's outcome.

Answer:

When writing the scopes and delimitation of a study, the researcher must show how the study is narrowed into more specific variables, sites, and participants. It is also important to show the limitations and extent of the research problem. Here are some guide questions to help you.

1. What is the general purpose of the study?

2. What is the duration of the study?

3. What are the population and samples that you will use in the study?
