Joint Variation

If y varies jointly as x and z, and y = 33 when x = 9 and z = 12, find y when x = 6 and z = 12.

[tex]\:[/tex]

Equation of variation: [tex]\tt \Large \blue{\bold{y = kxz}}[/tex]

Constant of variation: [tex]\tt \Large \green{\bold{k = \frac{y}{xz}}}[/tex]

[tex]\:[/tex]

Solve for k.

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow \green{\bold{k = \frac{y}{xz}}}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow k = \frac{33}{9 \times 12}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow k = \frac{33}{108}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow k = \frac{33 \div 3}{108 \div 3}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow \green{\bold{k = \frac{11}{36}}}[/tex]

[tex]\:[/tex]

Find y when x = 6 and z = 12.

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow \blue{\bold{y = kxz}}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow y = \frac{11}{36} \times 6 \times 12[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow y = \frac{11}{\cancel{36}} \times \cancel{6} \times 12[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow y = \frac{11}{6} \times 1 \times 12[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow y = \frac{11}{6} \times 12[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow y = \frac{11}{\cancel{6}} \times \cancel{12}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow y = \frac{11}{1} \times 2[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow y = 11 \times 2[/tex]

[tex]\tt \Large \rightarrow \purple{\bold{y = 22}}[/tex]

[tex]\:[/tex]

Final Answer:

If x = 6 and z = 12, then [tex]\tt \Large \purple{\bold{y = 22}}[/tex].

15. 1.)z varies jointly as x and y and z=60 when x=5 and y=6.______ A.find z when x=7 and y=6______ B.find x when z=72 and y=4_____ C.find y when z =80 and x=4____ Plz answer my question XD


Answer:

1. The value of z is 84.

2. The value of x is 9.

3. The value of y is 10.

Step-by-step explanation:

These problems can be solved by applying the concepts about variation.

Joint Variation is the condition where one variable varies directly on two or more variables. Let's say for example, [tex]z[/tex] varies jointly as [tex]x[/tex] and [tex]y[/tex] such that there exists a [tex]k[/tex] that satisfies the equation [tex]z=kxy[/tex].

Different Types of Variation

1. Direct Variation

2. Indirect Variation

Direct variation happens when two variables go in the same direction. This means that if one variable is increasing, the other one is also increasing.

Indirect variation happens when two variables don't go in the same direction. This means that one of the variables is increasing, and the other one is decreasing.

Since [tex]z[/tex] varies jointly as [tex]x[/tex] and [tex]y[/tex], then the situation satisfies the equation [tex]z=kxy[/tex]. Substitute the values of [tex]z=60[/tex], [tex]x=5[/tex], and [tex]y=6[/tex], then solve for [tex]k.\\[/tex]

                                                  [tex]\begin{aligned}z&=kxy\\60&=k(5)(6)\\60&=30k\\\frac{60}{30}&=\frac{30k}{30}\\2&=k\end{aligned}[/tex]

A. To solve for [tex]z[/tex], substitute the values of [tex]x=7[/tex], [tex]y=6[/tex], and [tex]k=2[/tex] into the equation [tex]z=kxy[/tex], then simplify.

                                                [tex]\begin{aligned}z&=kxy\\z&=2(7)(6)\\z&=84\end{aligned}[/tex]

B. To solve for [tex]x\\[/tex], substitute the values of [tex]z=72[/tex], [tex]y=4[/tex], and [tex]k=2[/tex] into the equation [tex]z=kxy[/tex], then simplify.

                                                 [tex]\begin{aligned}z&=kxy\\72&=(2)(x)(4)\\72&=8x\\\frac{72}{8}&=\frac{8x}{8}\\9&=x\end{aligned}[/tex]

C. To solve for [tex]y[/tex], substitute the values of [tex]z=80[/tex], [tex]x=4[/tex], and [tex]k=2[/tex], into the equation [tex]z=kxy[/tex], then simplify.

                                                [tex]\begin{aligned}z&=kxy\\80&=2(4)y\\80&=8y\\\frac{80}{8}&=\frac{8y}{8}\\10&=y\end{aligned}[/tex]
