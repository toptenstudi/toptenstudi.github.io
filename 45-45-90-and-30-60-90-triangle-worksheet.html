17. WORKSHEET IN MATHENThird QuarterWEEK 5A. Calculate the distance that you would travel if you drove for:1. 4 hours at 60 km / h2. 14 hours at 130 km/h3. 1/2 hour at 45 km / h4. 90 minutes at 180 km /h5. 1.5 hours at 65 km/B. How long does it take to travel?1. 200 kilometers at 40 km/h?2. 180 kilometers at 90 km/h?3. 320 kilometers at 65 km/h?4. 280 kilometers at 30 km/ h?5. 250 kilometers at 85 km/h?​


--------------------------------------------------------------------

A. Calculate the distance that you would travel if you drove for:

1. 4 hours at 60 km / h

[tex]\large\boxed{\text{ = 240km}} \\4 \times 60 = 240[/tex]

2. 14 hours at 130 km/h

[tex]\large\boxed{\text{ = 1820km}} \\ 14 \times 130 = 1820[/tex]

3. 1/2 hour at 45 km / h

[tex]\large\boxed{\text{ = 22.5km}} \\ \frac{1}{2} \times 45 = 22.5[/tex]

4. 90 minutes at 180 km/h

[tex]\large\boxed{\text{ = 16200km}} \\ 90 \times 180 = 16200[/tex]

5. 1.5 hours at 65 km/

[tex]\large\boxed{\text{ = 97.5km}} \\ 1.5 \times 65 = 97.5[/tex]

============================

B. How long does it take to travel?

1. 200 kilometers at 40 km/h?

[tex]\large\boxed{\text{ = 5km}} \\ 200 \div 40 = 5[/tex]

2. 180 kilometers at 90 km/h?

[tex]\large\boxed{\text{ = 2km}} \\ 180 \div 90 = 2[/tex]

3. 320 kilometers at 65 km/h?

[tex]\large\boxed{\text{ = 4.9km}} \\ 320 \div 65 = 4.9[/tex]

4. 280 kilometers at 30 km/ h?

[tex]\large\boxed{\text{ = 9.3km}} \\280 \div 30 = 9.3 [/tex]

5. 250 kilometers at 85 km/h?

[tex]\large\boxed{\text{ = 2.9km}} \\250 \div 85 = 2.9[/tex]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

Explanation for letter A.

The formula used there is speed × time because it is looking for distance.

Explanation for letter B.

The formula used there is distance ÷ time because it is looking for speed

--------------------------------------------------------------------

Speed = Distance ÷ Time

Time = Distance ÷ Speed

Distance = Speed × Time

--------------------------------------------------------------------
