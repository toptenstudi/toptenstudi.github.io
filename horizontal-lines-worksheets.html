It is recognized by its green outline, 5. This displays the name of the worksheet, such as Sheet1 or Sheet2.​


Answer:

1.This changes the magnification of a worksheet.

When you want to quickly change the magnification of a worksheet, use the Zoom slider. You'll find the Zoom slider in the bottom right corner of the Excel window. To use the Zoom slider, drag the slider to the right or to the left. To zoom in, drag the slider to the right.

2.They horizontally in a worksheet and are identified by a number in the row header.

Rows run horizontally in Ecel and are identified by a number in the row header. Columns run vertically in Excel and are identified by a letter in the column header. The cell reference identifies the location of a cell, and consists of the column letter and row number that intersect all the cells location.

3.They are the rectangular boxes located in the central area of a worksheet.

Cells are the rectangular boxes located in the central area of a worksheet. Cells contain labels, data, and formulas. To make worksheet data stand out, cells can be formatted to change the text or to add a fill color.

4.It is recognized by its green outline,

The active cell is the current cell. There is a green border around the active cell. ... The location of a cell in a worksheet as identified by its column letter and row number, such as A1.

5.This displays the name of the worksheet, such as Sheet1 or Sheet2.

Detailed Solution. The correct answer is the Title bar. The title bar displays both the name of the application and the name of the spreadsheet in MS-Excel.
