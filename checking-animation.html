Understanding of Animal, Animal Group, Characteristics of animal group​

ANIMALS AND GROUP OF ANIMALS AND CHARACTERISTIC, UNDERSTANDING

Animalia is the biological kingdom that encompasses all living things. The kingdom is then divided into about 30 phyla, or groups (plural form of phylum). Animals make up about 75% of all species on the planet. After then, animals are divided into two groups: vertebrates and invertebrates.

Vertebrates are animals with a backbone.

Vertebrates are classified as part of the Phylum Chordata.

Amphibians, birds, fish, mammals, and reptiles are the five different types of vertebrates.

Invertebrates are animals without a backbone.

The phylum Arthropoda contains the majority of invertebrates.

Amphibians are all vertebrates that require water or moist surroundings to exist.

They are cold-blooded creatures.

Their thin skin absorbs water and allows them to breathe.

They have at least one defense gland on their skin.

The majority of them follow the egg-larva-adult life cycle.

Birds are warm-blooded vertebrates with the ability to fly.

Although not all birds can fly, they all have wings.

Birds use their beaks to grab and consume food.

A bird's digestive mechanism permits it to feed whenever it wants and digest it afterward.

To reproduce, birds deposit eggs.

They are endothermic, which means they regulate their own body temperature.

They are bipedal, meaning they walk on two legs.

Their bones are hollow, and they have no teeth.

Fish are vertebrates as well, and they are the oldest vertebrates known.

They are ectothermic, or cold-blooded, which means their body temperature is regulated by their surroundings.

Fins are found on fish.

The majority of fish, but not all, have scales on their bodies and breathe through their gills.

Fish are creatures that dwell in water.

Mammalia refers to a class of vertebrates that includes mammals.

Young mammals acquire their nutrition from their mothers' milk.

Hair is seen on the majority of mammals.

Unlike all other vertebrates, their mouth is directly attached to their skull.

Almost all mammals are capable of giving birth to living children.

They are endothermic, which means they have warm blood.

The first vertebrates to live entirely on land are assumed to be reptiles. Today, however, not all reptiles dwell solely on land.

They are ectothermic, or cold-blooded.

To reproduce, they lay eggs.

They have four legs or are descended from four-legged creatures.

They take in air through their lungs.

Scales or scutes cover their entire bodies.

Invertebrates make up around 95% of all creatures. Backbones are absent in invertebrates. Invertebrates come in a variety of shapes and sizes, but they all share a few features.

Multicellular organisms are made up of many cells that collaborate.

Tissues, or cells that operate together in a more complicated fashion, are found in most, but not all, animals.

The majority, but not all, are able to move.

Invertebrates are classified into over 35 phyla.

They are usually soft.

5. Which structures are found in both animal and plant cells? Check all that apply.


Answer:

Structurally, plant and animal cells are very similar because they are both eukaryotic cells. They both contain membrane-bound organelles such as the nucleus, mitochondria, endoplasmic reticulum, golgi apparatus, lysosomes, and peroxisomes. Both also contain similar membranes, cytosol, and cytoskeletal elements
