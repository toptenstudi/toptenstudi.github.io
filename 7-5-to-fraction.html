Explanation:

Proper fractions: Proper fractions are those fractions with a higher amount of denominator which less than the amount of numerator.

Improper fractions: These types of fractions are the opposite of proper fractions. Here, the numerator is bigger than the denominator in the fraction.

Similar: This kind of fraction requires 2 or more given because it can be identified by looking at the denominator of each given fraction then see if the denominators are all the same.

Dissimilar terms: This kind of fraction requires 2 or more given because it can be identified by looking at the denominator of each given fraction then see if the denominators are all different from each other.

Mixed number: Unlike the other fractions, this fraction has another part other than the numerator and denominator. This has a whole number which makes them different.
