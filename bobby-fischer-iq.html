3. What is fischer projection in biochemistry?


The Fischer projection, devised by Emil Fischer in 1891, is a two-dimensional representation of a three-dimensional organic molecule by projection. Fischer projections were originally proposed for the depiction of carbohydrates and used by chemists, particularly in organic chemistry and biochemistry.

19. what is the meaning of der kleine fischer painting? ​


Answer:

"Der kleine Fischer" is a painting by German artist Franz von Stuck, created in 1883. The painting depicts a young fisherman, dressed in traditional clothing, sitting on the bank of a river with his fishing net. The background is a beautiful landscape with mountains, trees, and a flowing river.

The painting is often interpreted as a romanticized representation of rural life, and the simplicity and innocence of childhood. The boy's fishing net may symbolize his hopes and dreams for the future, and his connection to nature. The idyllic landscape in the background may represent the beauty and tranquility of the natural world.

Overall, "Der kleine Fischer" is a work of art that captures the beauty and simplicity of rural life, and the importance of staying connected to nature.

29. explain"lock and key" model of emil Fischer​


Answer:

The specific action of an enzyme with a single substrate can be explained using a Lock and Key analogy first postulated in 1894 by Emil Fischer. In this analogy, the lock is the enzyme and the key is the substrate. Only the correctly sized key (substrate) fits into the key hole (active site) of the lock (enzyme).
