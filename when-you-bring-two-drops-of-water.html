In physics and chemistry, a particle is an infinitesimal, dimensional object that can have a number of physical or chemical properties, such as volume or mass. Particles vary widely in size or quantity, from subatomic particles such as electrons, to microscopic particles such as atoms and molecules, to macroscopic particles such as powders and other granular materials. Particles can also be used to make scientific models of larger objects depending on their density, such as people moving in a crowd or moving celestial bodies.

A molecule is an electrically ordinary group of two or more atoms held together by chemical bonds. Molecules are distinguished from ions by the absence of an electric charge. However, in quantum physics, organic chemistry, and biochemistry, the term molecule is often used somewhat loosely, also being used for polyatomic ions.

In the kinetic theory of gases, the term molecule is often used for any gas particle regardless of its composition. According to this definition, noble gas atoms are considered as molecules as monatomic molecules.
