4. determine the vertex, axis of symmetry, range and maximum or minimum point(needed right now)​


The vertex of a parabola is the point at which the parabola changes direction and either opens upwards (if the parabola is in the shape of a "U") or downwards (if the parabola is in the shape of an "n"). The axis of symmetry of a parabola is the line that divides the parabola into two symmetrical halves. The range of a parabola is the set of all possible y-values that the parabola can take on. The maximum or minimum point of a parabola is the point at which the parabola reaches its highest or lowest point, respectively.

(x - 0)² = 4a[y - (-4)]

x² = 4a(y + 4)

(1)² = 4a[(-1)+4]

•Equation of the axis of symmetry: x = 2

•Domain: All real numbers

•Range: y ≥ k

Given: f(x) = (x-2)²+3

We dissect the function:

In y = a(x-h)²+k

in f(x) = (x-2)²+3

We get:

a = 1 h = 2k = 3

Vertex: It is at the point (h,k)

Opening of the point:

When the value of a is positive , the opening of the point is upwards.When the value of a is negative , the opening of the point is downwards.

Since. a = 1 , or it is positive. Then the opening of the point is upwards.

Vertex:

Vertex = (h,k)Vertex = (2,3)

Equation of axis of symmetry:

The equation is x = h

So , the equation is x = 2

Domain: The domain of quadratic function is all real numbers.

Range:

When the parabola opens upward , the range is y ≥ kWhen the parabola opens downward , the range is y ≤ k

Since , the given opens upwards.
