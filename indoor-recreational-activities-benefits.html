21. Why indoor activities is recreational activities


Indoor activities are also considered to be recreational activitiesWhat are recreational activities?

A recreational activities are activities that are done or conducted to boost energy and to have fun and enjoyment. It usually has a lesson or a learning. Recreational activities are often done in reunions, team buildings, camping, and retreats.

Indoor vs. Outdoor

Recreational activities can be conducted outdoors wherein the participants are usually being one with nature. They make use of natural resources such as the sunlight, the wind, the rain, the water, the trees, and many more. Some examples of outdoor recreational activities are: swimming, fishing, boating, snorkeling, jungle hunt, outdoor volleyball, basketball, soccer, tennis, and so much more.

Before, recreational activities are usually held outdoors. But since innovation is inevitable, activities now can also be held indoors. Some indoor recreational activities are: chess, playing board games, cooking, playing card games, indoor hide and seek, and many more.

Why indoors?

Why not? Recreational activities' purpose is to have fun and enjoy while learning. All these can be done at the comfort of your home, hassle-free and safer.

26. Indoor and outdoor recreational activities definition


Answer:

Indoor recreational activities are the things that you do for fun and enjoyment in the comfort of your home, the gym, or any other covered area. Some examples are: going to the gym, playing board games, doing indoor yoga, going to art museums etc.

Outdoor recreational activities are the things that you do for fun and enjoyment in an open area. Some examples are jogging, fishing, camping, skydiving etc.

Often people do recreational activities because they want to relieve stress and anxiety.

Other Ways of Classifying Recreational Activities

In addition to classifying recreational activities as outdoor and indoor, the following are some ways that it can be classified:

Social activities  like parties, banquets, picnicsPhysical activities like sports, diving,etc.Arts and crafts activities Dramatic activities like doing plays, skits, etc. (plays, puppetry, skits, etc.) Musical activities Cultural activities - going to museum, attending an art exhibit, discussion groups, poetry reading etc. (art appreciation, music appreciation, panels, discussion groups, etc.) Service activities- participating in medical missions, blood drive, fundraising etc.
