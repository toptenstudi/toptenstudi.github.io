8. Geometry problem. with answer and solution (create)​


Answer:

Here's a geometry problem with its solution:

Problem: Find the area of a trapezoid with bases measuring 8 cm and 12 cm, and height measuring 6 cm.

Solution:

The formula for the area of a trapezoid is:

Area = (sum of bases) x height / 2

In this problem, the sum of the bases is 8 cm + 12 cm = 20 cm, and the height is 6 cm.

Substituting these values into the formula, we get:

Area = (8 cm + 12 cm) x 6 cm / 2

Area = 20 cm x 6 cm / 2

Area = 60 cm²

Therefore, the area of the trapezoid is 60 cm².

28. Geometry question and step by step answer


Question:

A triangle has a perimeter of 50. If 2 of its sides are equal and the third side is 5 more than the equal sides, what is the length of the third side?

Answer:

The length of third side is 20

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Assign variables:

Let x = length of the equal side

Step 2: Write out the formula for perimeter of triangle.

P = sum of the three sides

Step 3: Plug in the values from the question and from the sketch.

50 = x + x + x + 5

Combine like terms

50 = 3x + 5

Isolate variable x

3x = 50 – 5

3x = 45

x =15

Be careful! The question requires the length of the third side.

The length of third side = 15 + 5 =20
