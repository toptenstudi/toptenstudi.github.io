13. Explain the functions, the nature, and the process of communication ​


The communication functions are as follows:

Function of Communication :

Submit information

Communication allows humans to convey information. For example, knowledge conveyed through books, news conveyed through television, to personal information conveyed through social media.

Educate

Humans grow into good personalities because of the education delivered through communication. As a baby, the mother will communicate with her child so that the child understands language. Education through communication continues to schools, colleges, to people's lives.

Entertain

Communication can be a tool to entertain someone. For example, conveying sympathy when someone is sad, entertaining motivational books, fun television shows, as well as music with encouraging lyrics, all are forms of communication.

Affect

Communication can influence a person's actions and thoughts so that the proverb does not know then do not love. the event of knowing is done by communication. Another example is the socialization of environmental awareness, which is a form of communication that influences others to care about the environment.

Nature of Communication in Interpersonal Communication :

The message conveyed by the sender to the recipient can be packaged verbally with words or nonverbally without words. Communication whose messages are packaged verbally is called verbal communication, while communication whose messages are packaged nonverbally is called nonverbal communication. So, verbal communication is conveying meaning by using words. While nonverbal communication does not use words. In daily communication 35% is in the form of verbal communication and 65% is in the form of nonverbal communication.

The communication process is how the communicator conveys the message of the communicant, so that it can create an equation of meaning between the communicant and the communicator. This communication process aims to create effective communication (according to the purpose of communication in general). The process of communication, much through development. The communication process can occur if there is interaction between humans and there is delivery of messages to realize communication motives.

The stages of the communication process are as follows:

Interpretation.Penance.Delivery.Journey.Reception.Refunding.Interpretation.
