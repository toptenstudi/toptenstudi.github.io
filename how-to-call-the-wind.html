You know?The elements of weather are this temperature, precipitation, wind speed, humidity, and the last is cloud cover.

What is Temperature?

Temperature as discussed in the past lesson. is measured with a thermometer and it refers to how hot or cold the atmosphere is. The unit of measure use is degree Celsius (°C) or degree Fahrenheit (°F).

What is Precipitation?

Precipitation is any form of liquid or solid water particles that fall from the atmosphere and reach the surface of the Earth. These particles include drízzle, rain, hail, and on rare occasions. snow and sleet. Precipitation is caused when a mass of warm, moist air hits a mass of cold air. Condensation causes the moisture to form droplets that become rain or crystals that become snow or ice. When these droplets or crystals become too heavy to be suspended in the atmosphere, they fall to Earth as precipitation.

What is Wind Speed?

In meteorology, wind speed, or air flow velocity, is a major volume of the atmosphere caused by the movement of air from high to low pressure, usually due to changes in the form of temperature whether it is high or low.

What is Humidity?

Humidity is the amount of water vapor in the air. If there is a lot of water vapor in the air, the humidity will be high. The higher the humidity, the wetter it feels outside. Too much or too little humidity can be dangerous. For example, high humidity combined with hot temperature is a combination that can be a health risk, especially for the very young and the very old.

What is Cloud Cover?

Cloud cover refers to the fraction of the sky obscured by clouds when observed from a particular location. Okta is usual unit of measurement of the cloud cover. Clouds can affect the amount of sunlight reaching the Earth's surface. Days are cooler than clear ones because clouds prevent more of the sun's radiation from reaching the Earth's surface. The opposite is true at night then, clouds act as a blanket, keeping the Earth warm.
