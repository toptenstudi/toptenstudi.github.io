10. as romeo is in despair (and after he tries to kill himself) what does the nurse give romeo and what is its significance


In William Shakespeare's play "Romeo and Juliet," after Romeo is banished from Verona and is in despair, the Nurse gives him a ring from Juliet. The ring is significant because it serves as a symbol of Juliet's love and devotion to Romeo, and it also represents their secret marriage.

The Nurse's decision to give Romeo the ring shows her loyalty to the young couple and her desire to help them be together despite the obstacles they are facing. The ring also symbolizes hope for Romeo, as it reminds him of his love for Juliet and the possibility of their reunion in the future.

Furthermore, the ring serves as a plot device later in the play when Juliet uses it to fake her own death in order to avoid marrying Paris, which ultimately leads to the tragic ending of the play.
