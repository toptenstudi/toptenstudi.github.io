Answer:

1.What is Mathematics?

Mathematics is the study of numbers, shapes, and patterns. It involves the use of logical reasoning and critical thinking to solve problems and understand the world around us.

2. Explain and enumerate the characteristics of Mathematics.
Abstraction: the use of mathematical concepts and symbols to represent real-world situations

Logic: the use of reasoning and proof to establish mathematical truths

Universality: the applicability of mathematical concepts and methods across a wide range of fields and disciplines

Objectivity: the ability to arrive at mathematical truths through the use of systematic procedures and methods

Certainty: the ability to establish mathematical truths with a high degree of certainty.

24. 1+2+x=x+1+2 it is mathematical expression or mathematical sentence? ​


Answer:

X=1 is a mathematical expression and is used as the subject of the opening clause of this compound sentence.

On the other hand, if we read X=1 verbally we get “Exe equals one.” That looks a lot like a sentence.

We seem to have a linguistic conundrum with this one.

My philosophy is that ambiguity can be your friend and this is one of those situations. Therefore, I declare this to be a speaker’s choice situation.
