13. 1. Discuss why it is important to consider the entire life cycle, rather than just the first stage.2. Discuss how materials engineering can play a role in “green design.”3. Suggest other consumer actions for minimal environmental impact than just recycling .​


Materials Engineering: Environmental Issues

1. Discuss why it is important to consider the entire life cycle, rather than just the first stage.

(The life cycle of a material, also known as the cradle-to-grave life circuit of a material is can be seen in the picture.)

We know that our planet is virtually a closed system, thus, everything we consume here on Earth will stay in here and affects our planet somehow. Also, the resources we are glamorously using is finite that is why we need to maximize the ways of using these materials in its entire lifetime before it gets broken or wears out.

As we can see, the life of a material does not only end when we are done using the product that is why as an Engineering practitioner or a Business man that manufactures a product, it is essential to consider the entire life cycle of a material/product, rather than just the first stage. When a product wears out or broken, it will be either recycled or will return to nature. If a product is recycled, it can be useful again. However, if the product just go directly back in nature, it can add up to the Environmental issues that Earth is facing, especially if the material is non-biodagradable such as glass, rubber, plastics, etc.

Considering the entire life cycle of a material/product, rather than just the first stage is like hitting three birds in one stone since you are helping in decreasing the environmental issues that Earth is facing while making a cost-efficient products and seeing the full extent of an Engineer's capability in designing a sustainable products all at the same time.

2. Discuss how materials engineering can play a role in “green design.”

Materials Engineering - an academic field dealing with materials in an atomic level in order to design an efficient product that is suitable for its application

Green Design - refers to an approach of manufacturing a product in consideration of the cradle-to-grave life circuit of a material

Materials Engineering is the key player in what we call green design. Because of materials engineering, Engineers will be able to know the properties of any material and how it reacts to certain conditions, which is crucial in overall environmental assessment of the product starting from its design, materials and components, manufacturing techniques to be used, and its disposal/recycling methods.

3. Suggest other consumer actions for minimal environmental impact than just recycling.

Buy what you only need. Take care of things by using them properly, storing them in the right place and maintainly cleaning it. If it is still useable or functioning, donate it to other people rather thab throwing it into trash. Study the proper disposal of your wastes. (i. e. scraps like newspapers or bottles can be sold in junkshops for recycling purposes, used cooking oils bought by small businesses, etc.)
