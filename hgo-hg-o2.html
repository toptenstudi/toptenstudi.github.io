12. . In the reaction 2Ag2O → 4Ag + O2, 4.58 L of O2 were formed at 745 mm Hg and 308 K. How many grams of Ag2O decomposed?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V_{\text{O}_2} = \text{4.58 L} \\ & MM_{\text{Ag}_2\text{O}} = \text{231.74 g/mol} \\ & T = \text{308 K} \\ & P = \text{745 mmHg = 0.98026 atm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of O₂ formed by using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{O}_2} & = \frac{PV_{\text{O}_2}}{\text{RT}} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.98026 atm})(\text{4.58 L})}{\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{308 K})} \\ & = \text{0.17764 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass of Ag₂O decomposed by using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 2 moles of Ag₂O is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of O₂.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{Ag}_2\text{O} & = \text{0.17764 mol} \: \text{O}_2 \times \frac{\text{2 mol} \: \text{Ag}_2\text{O}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{O}_2} \times \frac{\text{231.74 g} \: \text{Ag}_2\text{O}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Ag}_2\text{O}} \\ & = \text{82.3 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 82.3 g of Ag₂O is decomposed.

1. To calculate AS for the reaction, we first need to determine the entropy change for each substance involved in the reaction:

S(Hg) = 75.6 J/mol.K (from the table)

S(O2) = 205.0 J/mol.K (from the table)

S(HgO) = 76.32 J/mol.K (calculated using the formula below)

ΔS(Hg) = S(HgO) - S(Hg) = 76.32 - 75.6 = +0.72 J/mol.K

ΔS(O2) = S(HgO) - 1/2 S(O2) = 76.32 - 102.5 = -26.18 J/mol.K

AS = ΣΔS(products) - ΣΔS(reactants)

AS = [S(HgO)] - [S(Hg) + 1/2 S(O2)]

AS = 76.32 - 75.6 - 51.25

AS = -30.53 J/K

ΔG⁰ = ΔH⁰ - TΔS⁰

ΔG⁰ = 90.7 kJ/mol - (298 K)(-30.53 J/K)

ΔG⁰ = 177 kJ/mol - 81.03 kJ/mol

ΔG⁰ = +95.97 kJ/mol

ΔG⁰(500°C) = ΔH⁰ - TΔS⁰

ΔG⁰(500°C) = 177 kJ/mol - (773 K)(285 J/K)/(1000 J/kJ)

ΔG⁰(500°C) = 177 kJ/mol - 219.01 kJ/mol

ΔG⁰(500°C) = -41.01 kJ/mol

[tex]P_{1} = \text{720.0 mm Hg}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{450.0 mL}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{760.0 mm Hg}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{450.0 mL} × \frac{\text{720.0 mm Hg}}{\text{760.0 mm Hg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{426.3 mL}}[/tex]

10. 4.75 mol of O2 is enclosed in a 1.5-L container. How many additional moles of O2 should be added to the container to increase the volume to 3.95 L at the same temperature and pressure?Given:

[tex]V_{1} = \text{1.5 L}[/tex]

[tex]n_{1} = \text{4.75 mol}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{3.95 L}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]n_{additional}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the final number of moles of O₂.

[tex]\frac{V_{1}}{n_{1}} = \frac{V_{2}}{n_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{2} = n_{1} × \frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{2} = \text{4.75 mol} × \frac{\text{3.95 L}}{\text{1.5 L}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{2} = \text{12.51 mol}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the additional number of moles of O₂.

[tex]n_{additional} = n_{2} - n_{1}[/tex]

[tex]n_{additional} = \text{12.51 mol} - \text{4.75 mol}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{n_{additional} = \text{7.76 mol}}[/tex]

30. Suppose that the air in the atmosphere has 2.79 mol N2, 0.656 mol O2, and 0.0225 mol Ar. What is the partial pressure of each gas when the barometric pressure is 754.1 mm Hg?


The partial pressure of each gas :

• N₂ = 606.584 mmHg

• O₂ = 142.623 mmHg

• Ar = 4.892 mmHg

Further explanation  

Dalton's law of partial pressures states that the total pressure of a mixture of gases is equal to the sum of the partial pressures of the component gases  

Can be formulated:  

[tex]\tt Ptotal=P_1+P_2+P_3...P_n[/tex]

The partial pressure is the pressure of each gas in a mixture  

 [tex]\tt P_i=\dfrac{x_i}{x_{tot}}\\\\x=mole~fraction[/tex]

 

Total moles :

[tex]\tt 2.79+0.656+0.0225=3.4685[/tex]

The partial pressure of each gas :

[tex]\tt P_{N_2}=\dfrac{2.79}{3.4685}\times 754.1=606.584\\\\P_{O_2}=\dfrac{0.656}{3.4685}\times 754.1=142.623\\\\P_{Ar}=\dfrac{0.0225}{3.4685}\times 754.1=4.892[/tex]
