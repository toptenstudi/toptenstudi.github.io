3. Complete the worksheet below with what have you learned regarding operations on functions. Write your own definition and steps on performing each functions operation. You may give your own example to better illustrate your point. Addition Subtraction Multiplication Division Composition​


OPERATION ON FUNCTION

1. Addition

Assume we have two functions, f(x) and g(x) . The sum of these two functions is defined as,

(f + g)(x) = f(x) + g(x)

Example:

Consider f(x) = 3x + 1 and g(x) = x + 3

(f + g)(x) = f(x) + g(x)f(x) + g(x) = 3x + 1 + x + 3f(x) + g(x) = 4x + 4

2. Subtraction

Assume we have two functions, f(x) and g(x) . The differentiation between these two functions can be expressed as follows:

(f - g)(x) = f(x) - g(x)

Example:

Consider f(x) = 3x + 1 and g(x) = x + 3

(f - g)(x) = f(x) - g(x)f(x) - g(x) = 3x + 1 - (x + 3)f(x) - g(x) = 3x + 1 - x - 3f(x) - g(x) = 2x - 2

3. Multiplication

Assume we have two functions, f(x) and g(x) (x). The product of these two functions is defined as,

(f . g)(x) = f(x) . g(x)

Example:

Consider f(x) = 3x + 1 and g(x) = x + 3

(f . g)(x) = f(x) . g(x)f(x) . g(x) = (3x + 1)(x + 3)  f(x) . g(x) = 3x·x + 3x·3 + 1·x + 1·3f(x) . g(x) = 3x² + 9x + x + 3f(x) . g(x) = 3x² + 10x + 3

4. Division

Assume we have two functions, f(x) and g(x) (x). The division of these two functions is defined as,

(f / g)(x) = f(x) / g(x), where g(x) ≠ 0

where x belongs to both f and g's domains We must specify g(x) ≠ 0 because we cannot divide by zero.

Example:

Consider f(x) = 3x + 1 and g(x) = x + 3

(f / g)(x) = f(x) / g(x)f(x) / g(x) = (3x + 1)/(x + 3)

5. divisibility rules of 4,8,11,12 and worksheets


Answer:

Divisibility by 4 :

a number is said to be divisible by 4 if the number formed by the last two digits is a multiple of divisible by 4.divide the last 2 digits by 4.

Examples :

516 → 16 ÷ 4 = 4 624 → 24 ÷ 4 = 6

Checking :

516 ÷ 4 = 129 624 ÷ 4 = 156

Divisibility by 8 :

a number is said to be divisible by 8 if its last three digits are zeros or if the number formed by the last three-digits is divisible by 8.

Examples :

95 000 ÷ 8 = 11 8755 288 → 288 ÷ 8 = 36

Checking :

5 288 ÷ 8 = 661

Divisibility by 11 :

a number is said to be divisible by 11 when the difference between the sum of the odd positioned digits and the sum of the even positioned digits is zero (0) or eleven (11).

Example :

5 775The sum of odd positioned digit :

5 + 7 = 12

The sum of even positioned digit:

5 + 7 = 12

Their difference :

12 - 12 = 0

Checking :

5 775 ÷ 11 = 525

Divisibility by 12 :

a number is said to be divisible by 12 if the number is divisible by both 3 and 4.

Divisibility by 3 :

a number is said to be divisible by 3 if the sum of its digits is a multiple or divisible by 3.

Example :

672 → 6 + 7 + 2 = 15 is divisible by 3672 → 72 ÷ 4 = 18

Which means 672 is divisible by 12

Checking :

672 ÷ 12 = 56
