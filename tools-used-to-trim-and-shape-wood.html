1. Cutting machine - Tool: A machine used to cut different types of wood into specific shapes and sizes.

2. Measurement - Term: A term used to refer to the process of taking and recording dimensions and sizes of objects and materials used in carpentry.

3. Finishing - Term: A term used to refer to the final touches applied to carpentry work to make it look complete and appealing. Finishing can involve the application of chemicals or substances such as varnish or paint.

4. Grease - Term: A term used to refer to an oily substance that is applied to machines to make them run smoothly and reduce friction.

5. Portable power tool for sanding furniture pieces - Tool: A portable machine that is used to sand and smooth furniture surfaces. This tool is commonly used in carpentry and furniture making.

6. Mallet - Tool: A tool used to strike chisels, nails, and other tools. It is made of wood or rubber and is used to apply force while ensuring that the tool being struck is not damaged.

7. Ear muffs - Tool: A tool used to protect the ears against high-frequency noise generated by machines and tools used in carpentry.

8. Nail set - Tool: A tool used to drive the head of a nail below the surface of the wood to create a smooth finish.

9. Jigsaw - Tool: A tool used to cut irregular shapes in large or small pieces of wood. It is commonly used in carpentry to make intricate cuts.

10. Marking gauge - Tool: A tool used to transfer measurements from a ruler or tape measure to the workpiece. It is commonly used to mark lines for cutting, drilling, or other operations.

11. Compass - Tool: A tool used to scribe arcs and circles in metal or wood. It has two adjustable legs that allow the user to set the radius of the arc or circle.

12. Spirit level - Tool: A tool used to test the vertical and horizontal surfaces of a workpiece to ensure that they are level. It is commonly used in carpentry and construction work.

13. Plane - Tool: A tool used to trim and shape wood by removing thin shavings from the surface of the wood. It is commonly used to create a smooth surface or to remove imperfections from a workpiece.

14. Clamp - Tool: A tool used to hold materials or tools in place while performing carpentry work. Clamps come in different sizes and types depending on the job they are used for.

15. Dovetail saw - Tool: A small back saw with a straight chisel-type handle used to cut very fine joints, such as dovetail joints. It is commonly used in furniture making and other types of fine carpentry work.
