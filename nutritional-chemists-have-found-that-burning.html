Food and starch are biomolecules or molecules than can be digested by the human body to supply the energy needed for bodily functions. Basically, all types of food make a person fat if one gains more calories than he/she burns. Calorie is the unit used to measure the energy value of food. It is defined as the energy needed to raise one kilogram of water by one degree Celsius. For this question, since it takes twice more energy to burn or utilize fat than it takes for starch, then fat would cause a person to gain more weight.
