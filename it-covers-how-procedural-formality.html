COMMUNICATIVE STRATEGIES

An approach to connecting with your target audience is called a communications strategy. It covers who you're talking to, why you're talking to them, how you'll talk to them, when you'll talk to them, what format the content should be in, and how you should share it.

Across

4. Restriction

It refers to any limitation you may have as a speaker. It is a tactic that limits or restricts the other party's response in a communication situation. The Speaker creates a list of categories, and the Listener is required to react solely within those.

5. Topic Shifting

It involves moving from one topic to another. This tactic includes nodding, grinning, and repeating the addressee's reply.

7. Termination

It refers to the conversation participant’s close initiating expressions that end a topic in a conversation. the speaker and listener communicate each other verbal and nonverbal messages to cease the interaction. It can occasionally be brief and swift. prolonged by additional details, inquiries, or discussion of the previously covered subject.

Down

1. Nomination

A speaker carries out to collaboratively and productively establish a topic. A speaker makes a nomination in order to establish a topic in a cooperative and useful manner. In essence, when using this technique, you strive to start a conversation with the persons you are speaking to.

2. Topic Control

It cover how procedural formality affects the development of topic in conversations. It discusses the impact of procedural severity and casualness on the flow of conversational topics. This only implies that after a topic is introduced, everyone involved should work to develop it while avoiding interruptions and topic changes.

3. Turn Taking

Sometimes people are given opportunities to talk because others take much time during the conversation. when one person listens while the other speaks in a conversation. The roles of the listener and the speaker switch back and forth during a conversation (a circle of discussion).

6. Repair

It refers to how speakers address the problems in speaking, listening, and comprehending that they may encounter in a conversation. refers to the evaluation of a speaker's handling of a communication conflict and the circumstance.
