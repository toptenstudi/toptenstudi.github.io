24. 1. There ore many more________________ than there are primary consumers.2. Organisms that eat other organisms are called__________________3. Organisms that make ther own food are called__________ or __________4. Grass is _____________________5. Zebras (grass-eaters) are___________6. Lions (zebra-eaters) are_________ ​


1. There are many more producer than there are primary consumers.

2. Organisms that eat other organisms are called Heterotroph

3. Organisms that make ther own food are called producer or autotrophs

4. Grass is producer

5. Zebras (grass-eaters) are primary consumer

6. Lions (zebra-eaters) are secondary consumer

EXPLANATION

1. There is more producers, because without producers consumer populations would go down. So this is proving that we have more producers in an ecosystem than consumers.

2. A heterotroph is an organism that eats other plants or animals for energy and nutrients. The term stems from the Greek words hetero for “other” and trophe for “nourishment.” Organisms are characterized into two broad categories based upon how they obtain their energy and nutrients: autotrophs and heterotrophs.

3. An autotroph is an organism that can produce its own food using light, water, carbon dioxide, or other chemicals. Because autotrophs produce their own food, they are sometimes called producers.

4. Grass is a producer, a self-sustaining organism that obtains its energy from the sun. In the process, it introduces new organic substances into the food chain and plays a key role for consumers. Grass forms the basis of the food chain because of this ability.

5. The zebra (primary consumer) is food to all three as well, as well as the gazelle who is also a primary consumer.

6. The third in the chain are Secondary Consumers. These usually eat up the primary consumers and other animal matter. They are commonly called carnivores, and examples include lions, snakes, and cats.
