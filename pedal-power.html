3. A cyclist can pedal at a speed of 3m/s for 6 min.how many meters will the cyclist pedal in that time.​


Answer:

d = 1080 m

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

s = 3m/s

t = 6 min

Required:

Distance (d)

Equation:

d = s *t

Solution

Substitute all the given values to d = s *t

d = 3 m/s * 6 min

Converting the time to seconds

t = 6 min * 60 s

t = 360 s

Therefore

d = 3m/s * 360 s

d = 1080 m

10. 4. How are we going to stop a moving vehicle? A. Release the brake pedal to increase friction. B. Look at the brake pedal to increase friction. C. Press the brake pedal to increase friction. D. Do nothing to increase the friction. 90​


Answer:

To stop a moving vehicle, the correct option is C. Press the brake pedal to increase friction.

When we press the brake pedal, it applies force to the brake pads, which then press against the brake discs or drums. This creates friction between the two surfaces, which converts the kinetic energy of the moving vehicle into heat and slows it down.

18. back pedal in basketball​


Answer:

This is a handy transition drill for those players/teams that struggle with their back-to-basket quickness.

Players sprint to the first set of markers @ the half way line. Once they reach the markers they touch and pivot 180* in one movement and back pedal on their toes as quickly as possible until they are directly under the opposite basket.

This is repeated three times up all three lanes.

Alternate drill:

Remove middle lane, add a ball handler/passer to the middle lane. Ball handler makes three dribbles up the court from the baseline before passing to the back pedalling player.
