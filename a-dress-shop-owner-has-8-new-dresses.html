14. a dress-shop owner has 8 new dresses that she wants to display in the window. If the the display window has 5 mannequins, in how many ways can she the up?


Answer:

56

Step-by-step explanation:

This problem is similar to taking 5 dresses from 8 possible ones with no regards to order. We do not take into account the order because what matters is if a dress gets displayed or not. We use combination. A combination is a selection of items from a collection such that the order of the selection does not matter. The formula for taking r things from n possible ones with no particular order is expressed by.

[tex]\frac{n!}{n!(n-r)!}[/tex]

The ! symbols is called "factorial". In simple terms, x! = (x)(x-1)(x-2)...(3)(2)(1). For example, 4! is 4*3*2 = 24. Since it is a treated product, we can cancel out fractions with both x! on the numerator and denominator.

substituting n = 8 and r = 5 to the formula gives us

[tex]=\frac{8!}{5!(8-5)!}\\=\frac{8*7*6*5!}{5!3!}\\=\frac{8*7*6}{3*2}\\=8*7\\=56[/tex]

There are 56 possible ways to select 5 dresses from 8 possible ones.
