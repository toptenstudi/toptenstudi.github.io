1. a light ray travelling parallel oto a concave mirror's axis strikes the mirror surface. The reflected ray​


This question comes under the Physics section.

A light ray which is travelling parallel to the concave mirror's axis strikes the mirrors surface. The reflected ray will pass through the focus of the concave mirror.

According to the ray diagram rule, if an incident ray is parallel to the principal axis of a concave mirror, then the reflected ray will pass through the focus of the mirror.

6. Ray of light travelling parallel of the principal axi of a concave mirror will come together.


Answer:

Any incident ray traveling parallel to the principal axis on the way to the mirror will pass through the focal point upon reflection. Any incident ray passing through the focal point on the way to the mirror will travel parallel to the principal axis upon reflection.

Reflection of Light on a Concave Mirror

Apart from flat mirrors, reflection events can occur in concave mirrors and the law of reflection also applies. A concave mirror is a mirror whose shape is curved like the inside of a ball that is split.

Concave mirror has parts that look like in the picture above. P is the center point of the mirror curvature. O is the point where the principal axis intersects with the concave mirror. F is the focal point of the mirror which is halfway between point P and point O.

If R is the radius of curvature of the mirror, namely the distance from point P to point O and f is the focal length of the mirror, namely the distance from the focal point of the mirror (F) to point O, then the relationship applies:

f=R/2

In the reflection of light by a concave mirror, the distance between the object and the mirror affects the image produced. The image formed by a concave mirror is the intersection of the reflected rays or the intersection of the extensions of the reflected rays. Concave mirrors collect light (converge). Thus, if there are parallel beams of light hitting the surface of a concave mirror, then the reflected light rays will cross the same point.
