3. What is the molarity (m) of a solution prepared by dissolving 15.0 grams of na3po4 in enough water to make 250.0 ml of solution?


Here are the steps to compute for the molarity of the solution:  

The formula for computing the molarity of a solution is this: Molarity = moles of the solute / liter of the solution.  

1.You need to compute for the moles of the solute (Na3PO4). To compute for the moles of solute (Na3PO4, here is the formula:  

Moles of solute (Na3PO4) = Given weight of Na3PO4 in the problem x (1 mole Na3PO4 / formula weight of Na3PO4)Moles of solute (Na3PO4) = 15g Na3PO4 x (1 mole Na3PO4 / 163.94g/mole) (Note: The formula weight of Na3PO4 is 163.94g/mole.)Moles of solute (Na3PO4) = 0.0915 mole

2.Next, compute for the molarity of the solution. To compute for the molarity of the solution, use the formula for molarity:  

Molarity = moles of the solute / liter of the solutionMolarity = 0.0915 mole of Na3PO4 / 0.250 liter of solution (Note: You need to convert the given 250 ml to liter. This is equivalent to 0.250 liter of solution.)Molarity = 0.366 M Na3PO4

Therefore, the molarity of the solution in the given problem is 0.366 M Na3PO4.

How do I find molar mass?

The molar mass is the mass of a given chemical element or chemical compound (g) divided by the amount of substance (mol). The molar mass of a compound can be calculated by adding the standard atomic masses (in g/mol) of the constituent atoms.

However, molar mass can also be calculated by multiplying the atomic mass in amu by the molar mass constant (1 g/mol). To calculate the molar mass of a compound with multiple atoms, sum all the atomic mass of the constituent atoms.

16. II. Task: Determine the molar mass Water (H₂O) Elements No. of Atoms Atomic Mass Total Mass Molar Mass (MM)=​


Explanation:

The molar mass of water (H2O) can be determined by adding up the atomic masses of each element present in the compound.

Water is composed of two hydrogen atoms (H) and one oxygen atom (O).

The atomic mass of hydrogen is 1.008 amu (atomic mass units).

The atomic mass of oxygen is 16.00 amu.

Therefore, the total mass of water is:

(2 x 1.008) + 16.00 = 18.016 amu

And the molar mass (MM) is equal to the total mass in amu, so the molar mass of water is 18.016 g/mol.

23. claculate the molar mass of water. the molar mass of oxygen=16.00 x 1=16/mole the molar mass of hydrogen=1.01 x 2=2.0g/mole


MOLAR MASS

Calculate the molar mass of water.

The chemical formula of water is H₂O

Molar Mass of Oxygen = 16 g/mol

16 • 1 = 16 g

Molar Mass of Hydrogen = 1.01 g/mol

1.01 • 2 = 2.0 g

Molar Mass of H₂O = molar mass O + molar mass H₂

Molar Mass of H₂O = 16 g + 2.0 g

Molar Mass of H₂O = 18 g/mol
