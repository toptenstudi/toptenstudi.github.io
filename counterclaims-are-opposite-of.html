28. write a counterclaim for each claims below. Claims: Divorce should be legalized in the Philippines. Counterclaims: Claims: Death penalty should be legalized Counterclaims: Claims: Students learn well at home, not in school. Counterclaims: Claims: Vaccine for all against Covid 19 Counterclaims: Claims: War against Drugs is a success. Counterclaims:


Claims: Divorce should be legalized in the Philippines.

Counterclaims: Divorce should not be legalized in the Philippines as it goes against the country's strong cultural and religious values, which promote the sanctity of marriage and the importance of family. Legalizing divorce may also increase the number of broken families and harm children who are caught in the middle of their parents' separation.

Claims: Death penalty should be legalized.

Counterclaims: Death penalty should not be legalized as it violates the right to life and could result in the execution of innocent people. The justice system is not perfect and mistakes can be made, and there is no way to undo an execution once it has been carried out. In addition, the death penalty has not been shown to have a deterrent effect on crime.

Claims: Students learn well at home, not in school.

Counterclaims: Students may not learn as well at home as they do in school due to lack of structure, resources, and support from teachers and peers. Schools also provide socialization and hands-on learning opportunities that are not available in the home environment. Furthermore, not all families have the means or knowledge to provide their children with an adequate education at home.

Claims: Vaccine for all against Covid 19.

Counterclaims: Not everyone may choose to receive the Covid-19 vaccine due to concerns about its safety, efficacy, or personal beliefs. In addition, some people may not have access to the vaccine due to logistical, financial, or political reasons. Widespread vaccination is necessary for achieving herd immunity, but it is not a guarantee that the pandemic will end immediately.

Claims: War against Drugs is a success.

Counterclaims: The War on Drugs has not been a success as it has not reduced drug use or drug-related crime, but has instead led to increased violence, human rights violations, and mass incarceration. It has also focused on punishment rather than rehabilitation and has neglected to address the root causes of drug use, such as poverty, lack of education, and mental health issues.

Answer:

stand: No

reason 1: the digital world is unlimited and instant, which can be very dangerous for young children who pick up and learn anything and are very vulnerable, they will also struggle to learn patience.

evidence: when you go on tiktk, youtube, or any other app, you won't have to wait long, if at all, for the next game, video, or meme. it's all just immediately there waiting for you to reach out and grab it. this can cause children to fail to learn patience, an already very difficult skill to learn for both children and adults alike. The internet also dosen't have a limiter for things like google, google play and other websites. this means children can get access to disturbing materials likely to affect their growth and development

counterclaim: if children are taught to balance their time on gadgets, what websites to avoid, and to not develop a dependance on them. gadgets can be a very useful tool.

reason 2: gadgets can cause children to develop health conditions. if children are given gadgets at a young age when a lot of them are still developing, it can cause them to develop health issues. as it's known, the blue light from gadgets causes damage to the eyes and unfortunately the is no evidence that "anti blue light glasses" actually provide protection against the blue light from gadgets.

evidence: when children are given gadgets at a young age, they will be dazzled by all the wonderful apps, videos and other things they now have access to. this leads to them staying in place and being quiet as long as the gadget is present. parents may love this but it is not healthy for the child. Children's eyes are still developing until the age of 20 and start deteriorating at the age of 40. the blue light will speed up the process and cause eye strain.

counterclaim: limiting the amount of time on a gadget, making sure they still move around and play, giving a healthy diet can help prevent to any health problems that would be developed from staying in place focused solely on their gadgets.

reason 3: giving gadgets to children at a young age puts them at a higher risk of mental health problems. when you get addicted to gadgets and the digital world they provide, you could be taking to 5 different people at the same time, yet feel alone and disconnected. this is because social comparison, feelings of missing out, and cyberbullying all come from the things we see on the internet. some children may see friends on their computer messaging someone hurtful words and not realize it's cyberbullying, why? because the friend said it's ok, it's only a joke. Social media is a wonderful when used correctly, but a horrible weapon when it is not. It often times gives children access to information they are not ready yet to handle.

evidence: A study published in 2020 showed that various websites and social media platforms prompted higher feelings of loneliness, depression, and anxiety.

counterclaim: Just like all the previously stated reasons, these only become an issue if children are given unlimited, unsupervised scrolling, messaging, and browsing. the key to solving all these issues is proper control and teaching children proper control and safety precautions when using the internet unsupervised.

30. III. Counter ArgumentA. Summarize the counterclaimsB. Provide supporting information for counterclaimsC. Refute the counterclaimsD.Give evidence for argument​


Answer:

counterclaim is the argument (or one of the arguments) opposing your thesis statement. A reason tells why the claim is made and is supported by the evidence. Evidence is the facts or research to support your claim.A refutation, on the other hand, takes this a step further by actually presenting evidence to disprove the opposing arguments
