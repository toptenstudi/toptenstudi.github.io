11. 5. An area on Earth where two or more lithosphericplates collide.​


An area on Earth where two or more lithospheric plates collide is called?

The area on planet Earth where two or more lithospheric plates collide is called as the convergent plate boundary.

Convergent plate boundary is the kind of plate boundary, where two or more plates move toward each other continuously, they collide or clash and often produce subduction zones, where one plate slides down under the second plate.

Answer:

C.Tectonic Plates

Explanation:

Tectonic plates are large pieces of the lithosphere that move around on top of the asthenosphere. Boundaries between tectonic plates are classified as convergent, divergent, or transform. Convection is the main driving force of plate tectonics. Tectonic plates move a few centimeters per year.


17. ____4. It refers to the movement of lithospheric plates._____5. the theory of moving lithospheric plates.​


Answer:

4. Plate motion/ Tectonic shift (?)

5. Plate tectonics (?)

Explanation:

The plates make up Earth's outer shell, called the lithosphere. ... The movement of the plates creates three types of tectonic boundaries: convergent, where plates move into one another; divergent, where plates move apart; and transform, where plates move sideways in relation to each other.

The heat from radioactive processes within the planet's interior causes the plates to move, sometimes toward and sometimes away from each other. This movement is called plate motion, or tectonic shift.

The theory of plate tectonics states that the Earth's solid outer crust, the lithosphere, is separated into plates that move over the asthenosphere, the molten upper portion of the mantle. Oceanic and continental plates come together, spread apart, and interact at boundaries all over the planet.
