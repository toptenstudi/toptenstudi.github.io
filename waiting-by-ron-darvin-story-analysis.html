"Waiting" by Ron Darvin is a short story that tells the tale of a young boy named Simon, who is waiting for his father to pick him up from school. Throughout the story, Simon becomes increasingly worried as his father does not arrive, and he is left waiting alone outside the school.

The climax of the story occurs when Simon's father finally arrives. Simon has been waiting for several hours, and he has been feeling increasingly anxious and scared. As his father approaches, Simon's emotions reach their peak, and he feels a mixture of relief and anger towards his father for making him wait for so long.

It is also a moment of emotional release for Simon, as he is finally able to express his feelings towards his father.

In conclusion, the climax of "Waiting" occurs when Simon's father finally arrives to pick him up, providing a resolution to the tension that has been building throughout the story.

15. reflection about story of Ron Clark​


Answer:

The story of Ron Clark is a powerful and inspiring one. As an educator, he believed in the transformative power of education and set out to make a difference in the lives of his students. Despite the challenges he faced, including working in an underfunded and underperforming school in Harlem, he was able to create a learning environment that was engaging, challenging, and supportive.

One of the things that struck me about Ron Clark's story is his relentless optimism and belief in the potential of his students. He refused to accept the idea that certain students were "unteachable" or destined to fail. Instead, he found creative ways to connect with his students, such as using music and dance to make learning fun and engaging.

Another key aspect of Ron Clark's approach was his focus on building relationships with his students. He took the time to get to know each student on a personal level, understanding their unique strengths, challenges, and interests. This allowed him to tailor his teaching approach to each student's needs and create a sense of community in the classroom.

Overall, Ron Clark's story is a reminder of the importance of dedicated and passionate educators who believe in their students and are willing to go above and beyond to help them succeed. His approach to teaching shows that with creativity, empathy, and perseverance, it is possible to create a positive and impactful learning environment that can transform the lives of students.
