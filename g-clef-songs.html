13. what are the similarities and differences f-clef and g-clef​


Answer:

The treble clef places middle C beneath the clef on the first ledger line below the clef. The bass clef places middle C above the clef on the first ledger line above the clef.

The treble clef symbol is a very stylized letter G, so it is sometimes known as the “G clef.” The line on which you find G in that clef passes through the middle of the broadest part of the letter G. The bass clef symbol is a very stylized letter F with 2 dots on it, and is sometimes called the “F clef.” The line on which you find F in that clef passes right between those two dots.
