5. 5. Which of the following volcano is the example of cinder volcano?A. Mt. Bulusan B. Mt. MayonC. Mt TaalD. Mt. Isarog​


Cinder Cones

The correct answer is Taal Volcano, even if it is classified as a complex volcano. This is because some of its craters, like Binintiang Malaki, is considered as a cinder cone. The correct answer is letter C.

What are cinder cones?

A cinder cone is a type of volcano that forms after a violent eruption. The lava extruded from the vent solidifies quickly, and deposited immediately near the vent, forming a cone-shaped structure. This is different from other volcanoes, like stratovolcanoes, shield volcanoes, and calderas.

Other example of cinder cones in the Philippines

Aside from Taal Volcano’s Binintiang Malaki, there are other cinder cones found all over the Philippines. Smith Volcano, in Babuyan Group of Islands, can also be considered as a cinder cone. Another great example is Bud Dajo in Sulu. All of these are active as of 2018, based on the latest information posted by PHIVOLCS on their website.

Classification of Bulusan, Mayon, and Isarog

The remaining volcanoes on the choices are considered as stratovolcanoes. These volcanoes formed due to the deposition of alternating layers of ash and lava. Stratovolcanoes are very common in the Philippines, and most of our active volcanoes are classified under this type.
