1. Irregular verbs change to verb tenses test for grade 3


Grade 3 students will be tested on their knowledge of irregular verbs and how they change to different verb tenses (https://brainly.ph/question/13095542). This is an important skill for students to learn, as it will help them communicate correctly in all types of writing. The test will consist of a series of questions that Grade 3 students must answer correctly in order to demonstrate their understanding of the material. Irregular verbs can be tricky, but with some practice, Grade 3 students should be able to ace this test!

I (hear) a new song on the radio. [ heard ]I (read) three books last week. [ read ]They (speak) French to the waitress. [ spoke ]He (understand) during the class, but now he doesn't understand.[ understood ]I (forget) to buy some milk. [ forgot ]She (have) a baby in June. [ had ]You (lose) your keys last week. [ lost ]They (swim) 500m. [ swam ]I (give) my mother a CD for Christmas. [ gave ]At the age of 23, she (become) a doctor. [ became ]I (know) the answer yesterday. [ knew ]He (tell) me that he lived in Toronto. [ told ]We (lend) John £200. [ lent ]She (drink) too much coffee yesterday. [ drank ]The children (sleep) in the car. [ slept ]He (keep) his promise. [ kept ]I (choose) steak for dinner. [ chose ]The film (begin) late. [ began ]We (fly) to Sydney. [ flew ]They (drive) to Beijing. [ drove ]He (teach) English at the University. [ taught ]I (send) you an email earlier. [ sent ]We (leave) the house at 7am. [ left ]He (feel) terrible after eating the prawns. [ felt ]She (bring) some chocolates to the party. [ brought ]

14. ENGLISH-(1 GRADE 6 PAD) 1.)MEANING OF VERB TENSES 2.)3 MAIN TENSES AND GIVE 2 EXAMPLES PER TENSE 3.)10 EXAMPLES OF IRREGULAR AND REGULAR VERBS 4.)MEANING OF CONDITIONAL SENTENCE 5.)4 TYPES OF CONDITIONAL SENTENCES​


Generally speaking, verb tenses identify the time period when an action occurs. The verb walks communicates not only how many people completed the action (it’s singular), but also when it occurred. In this case, the tense is present.

II.

Present

1. it Usually Rains everday here.

2. Does he Write an email?

Past

1. An old lady Walked with her cat.

2. 2 boys Played with a ball.

Future

1. I will read the newspaper when i go to the bus station.

2. They are going to dance

III.

Regular verbs Ver.2

1. Accept Accepted

2. Die Died

3. End Ended

4. Enjoy Enjoyed

5. Invite Invited

6. Review Reviewed

7. Pass Passed

8. Slow Slowed

9. Turn Turned

10. Mark Marked

Irregular verbs

1. Arise Arose

2. Awake Awoke

3. Be Was/Were

4. Bear Bore

5. Beat Beaten

6. Become Became

7. Foresee Foresaw/Foreseen

8. Foretell Foretold

9. Freeze Froze/Frozen

10. Give Gave/Given

IV.

Conditional sentences are statements discussing known factors or hypothetical situations and their consequences. Complete conditional sentences contain a conditional clause (often referred to as the if-clause) and the consequence. Consider the following sentences:

1. If a certain condition is true, then a particular result happens.

2. I would travel around the world if I won the lottery.

3. When water reaches 100 degrees, it boils.

V.

Here are a few examples:

General truth – If I eat breakfast, I feel good all day.

Future event – If I have a test tomorrow, I will study tonight.

Hypothetical situation – If I had a million dollars, I would buy a boat!

Hypothetical outcome – If I had prepared for the interview, I would have gotten the job.

Strategies in the control of an outbreak are screening, containment (or suppression), and mitigation. Screening is done with a device such as a thermometer to detect the elevated body temperature associated with fevers caused by the coronavirus.[185] Containment is undertaken in the early stages of the outbreak and aims to trace and isolate those infected as well as introduce other measures to stop the disease from spreading. When it is no longer possible to contain the disease, efforts then move to the mitigation stage: measures are taken to slow the spread and mitigate its effects on the healthcare system and society. A combination of both containment and mitigation measures may be undertaken at the same time.[186] Suppression requires more extreme measures so as to reverse the pandemic by reducing the basic reproduction number to less than 1.[187]
