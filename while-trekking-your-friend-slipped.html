7. While trekking your friend slipped on a sloppy terrain and twisted his/her ankle


Answers:

-Rest. Avoid activities that cause pain, swelling or discomfort.-Ice. Use an ice pack or ice slush bath immediately for 15 to 20 minutes and repeat every two to three hours while you're awake. If you have vascular disease, diabetes or decreased sensation, talk with your doctor before applying ice.-Compression. To help stop swelling, compress the ankle with an elastic bandage until the swelling stops. Don't hinder circulation by wrapping too tightly. Begin wrapping at the end farthest from your heart.-Elevation. To reduce swelling, elevate your ankle above the level of your heart, especially at night. Gravity helps reduce swelling by draining excess fluid.

Your brother was setting up the tent when cuts his finger with the pole. hot 4. Your mother accidentally touches pan 5. Your friend experiences stroke. Do you think you did the right action for each situation? Do not worry. As you


Explanation:

1. While trekking, your friend slipped on a sloppy terrain and twisted his/her ankle.

Immediate action taken: I will apply RICE method (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation) to the injured ankle, and if necessary, assist my friend to the nearest health facility for medical attention.

2. While camping, one of the campers was attacked and bitten by a snake.

Immediate action taken: I will immediately move the victim from the place where the snake is still present, immobilize the affected area and try to identify the type of snake to determine the appropriate antivenom. Seek emergency medical care as soon as possible.

3. Your brother was setting up the tent when cuts his finger with the pole.

Immediate action taken: I will wash the cut with clean water, apply pressure on the wound to stop the bleeding, and cover it with a clean sterile dressing. If necessary, I will take him to the nearest health facility for medical attention.

4. Your mother accidentally touches pan.

Immediate action taken: I will cool the burn with water for several minutes to alleviate the pain, cover the burn with a clean dressing, and give her pain relief medication if needed. If it is a severe burn or if there are any complications, I will seek medical care.

5. Your friend experiences a stroke.

Immediate action taken: I will quickly call for emergency services and try to keep my friend comfortable and calm while waiting for medical help. It is crucial that I not attempt to give any medications or beverages until medical professionals arrive, as stroke requires immediate professional medical attention.

Overall, these actions are an initial useful first aid that I think would be appropriate for each situation, however, it is always essential to seek medical attention when necessary, especially in severe cases.
