18. what kind of EM wave of checking bankbook signature?


The type of Electro Magnetic Wave for checking passbook signatures is Ultraviolet Light.

Further explanation:

Ultraviolet Light

Ultraviolet light is used in banking, namely to check whether your signature on the withdrawal slip is the same as the signature on the passbook.

Ultraviolet light is an electromagnetic wave that has a frequency between ¹⁰¹⁵ Hz to ¹⁰¹⁶ Hz. The wavelength range is ¹⁰ ⁻ ⁸ m to ¹⁰ ⁻ ⁷ m. Ultraviolet light is produced by atoms and molecules in an electric flame.

Ultraviolet light is electromagnetic radiation that comes from the sun. This light cannot be seen by the eye. However, some animals such as bees, birds, and butterflies can see UV light clearly.

Types of Ultraviolet Light

Ultraviolet radiation from the sun has three types of radiation rays which are divided by wavelength.

Types of UV rays consist of :

UVA light

UVA light has a wavelength of  ³¹⁵ ⁻ ⁴⁰⁰ nm and has the longest wavelength among other UV rays. This light is considered to be the most powerful ultraviolet light and is able to penetrate clouds and glass and even remains on cloudy or rainy weather.

UVB light.

UVB light has a wavelength of ²⁸⁰ ⁻ ³¹⁵ nm. UVB rays can be absorbed by clouds and cannot penetrate the glass, but the range of exposure can only reach the epidermis layer of the skin.

UVC light.

UVC light has the shortest wavelength, which is ¹⁸⁰ ⁻ ²⁸⁰ nm. However, UVC rays cannot penetrate the ozone layer, so these rays cannot reach the earth's surface.
