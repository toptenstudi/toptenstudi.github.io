A line perpendicular to another has the negative reciprocal version of its slope. First, find the slope of the line passing through (-2, 3) and (-3, 0).

m = [tex]\frac{Y_{2}-Y_{1}}{X_{2}-X_{1}}[/tex]

m = [tex]\frac{0-3}{-3 - (-2)}[/tex]

m = -3/-1

m = 3

The negative reciprocal of 3 is m = -1/3, which is the slope of the line we're looking for. The equation of a line can be found using it's slope and a point it passes through with the point-slope formula.

m = -1/3, (4, 2)

y - Y₁ = m(x - X₁)

y - 2 = (-1/3)(x - 4)

3y - 6 = -1(x - 4)

3y - 6 = -x + 4

Another way to use the value of the slope and a point is shown below:

m = -a/b = -1/3, equation of a line: ax + by = c

1x + 3y = c or x + 3y = c

Find c by plugging in the coordinates of the point as x and y.

x + 3y = c

4 + 3(2) = c

4 + 6 = c = 10

x + 3y = 10 or x + 3y - 10 = 0

15. Find the equation of the line through (4,5) which is perpendicular to the line 7x+6y=-3​


[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

The slope of two perpendicular lines are negative reciprocal of each other. If the slope of the first line is equal to m₁, then the slope, m₂ of the second line must be equal to -1/m₁ (In other words, the product of the slope of two perpendicular lines is equal to -1).

Slope of perpendicular lines:

[tex]\mathsf{m_2=-\dfrac{1}{m_1} \ \ or \ \ m_1m_2=-1}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Set:

[tex]\mathsf{L_1:7x+6y=-3}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

To determine the slope of the first line, convert the given standard form to slope-intercept form (y = mx + b).

[tex]\mathsf{7x+6y=-3}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{6y=-7x-3}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=-\dfrac{7}{6}x-\dfrac{3}{6}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=-\dfrac{7}{6}x-\dfrac{1}{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

The slope, m₁ of our first line is equal to:

[tex]\mathsf{m_1=-\dfrac{7}{6}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Therefore, the slope, m₂ of our second is line is equal to:

[tex]\mathsf{m_2=-\dfrac{1}{m_1}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{m_2=-\dfrac{1}{-\dfrac{7}{6}}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{m_2=\dfrac{6}{7}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

The second line is passing through the point (4, 5). We have a slope and a point, Do determine the equation of the line, we can use the point-slope form of a line.

Point-slope form of a line:

[tex]\mathsf{y-y_1=m(x-x_1)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{m=m_2=\dfrac{6}{7}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{(x_1,y_1)=(4,5)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Substitute the values,

[tex]\mathsf{y-5=\dfrac{6}{7}(x-4)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y-5=\dfrac{6}{7}x-\dfrac{24}{7}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=\dfrac{6}{7}x-\dfrac{24}{7}+5}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=\dfrac{6}{7}x+\dfrac{11}{7}} \Longleftarrow \textsf{slope-intercept form}[/tex]

If you want the standard form,

[tex]\mathsf{y=\dfrac{6}{7}x+\dfrac{11}{7}} \longleftarrow \textsf{multiply both sides by 7}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{7y=6x+11}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{6x-7y=-11} \Longleftarrow \textsf{standard form}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

The equation of the line perpendicular to the line 7x + 6y = -3 and passing through point (4, 5) is:

Slope-intercept form:

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{y=\dfrac{6}{7}x+\dfrac{11}{7}}}[/tex]

Standard form:

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{6x-7y=-11}}[/tex]

=========================================

[tex]\mathbb{SLOPE \ OF \ TWO \ LINES:}[/tex]

1.) Parallel lines

[tex]\mathsf{m_1=m_2}[/tex]

2.) Perpendicular lines

[tex]\mathsf{m_2=-\dfrac{1}{m_1}}[/tex]

28. what is the slope of a line that is perpendicular to a line whose equation is -10y = 5 + 2x​


Answer:

5

Step-by-step explanation:

1. Divide both sides of the equation by -10:

[tex]-10y=5+2x[/tex]

[tex]\frac{-10y}{-10}=\frac{5+2x}{-10}\\y=\frac{5}{-10}+\frac{2x}{-10}[/tex]

[tex]y = -\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{5}x[/tex]

2. Rearrange the expression on the right-hand side of the equation:

[tex]y = -\frac{1}{5}x-\frac{1}{2}[/tex]

3. Note the slope-intercept form of the equation of the line which is:

[tex]y=mx+b[/tex]

where m is the slope and b is the y-intercept.

Therefore, for the equation [tex]y = -\frac{1}{5}x-\frac{1}{2}[/tex], the slope is -1/5 and the y-intercept is -1/2.

4. The slope of the line perpendicular to the line with a slope of -1/5 is the negative reciprocal of the slope.

5. Get the reciprocal of -1/5

-1/5 -> -5

6. Negate the number:

-5 -> -(-5) -> 5
