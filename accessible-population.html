Many experts believe that water will become increasingly valuable as a resource in the future, and there are concerns that water shortages and access to clean water could become sources of conflict.

There are a number of efforts underway to address the challenges of providing clean water to a growing population. These efforts include:

Developing new technologies for purifying and treating water, including the use of desalination and advanced filtration systems.

Improving infrastructure for the delivery of clean water, including the repair and expansion of existing water systems and the construction of new ones.

Promoting the use of water-efficient technologies and practices, such as drip irrigation and the use of drought-resistant crops.

Protecting and conserving water resources, including efforts to reduce pollution and to prevent overuse and waste of water.

It is important for governments, communities, and individuals to work together to address the challenges of providing clean water to a growing population, and to ensure that everyone has access to this vital resource.

26. Based on the data gathered from World population review (Accessed April 3, 2019) on the population of the Philippines in 2019, the top three cities in terms of population in the Philippines are Quezon City with 2, 761, 720; Manila with 1, 600, 000; and Caloocan City with 1, 500, 000. Write the scientific notation of the total population of these three cities.​


Expressing Numbers in Scientific Notation

Quezon City - 2, 761, 720

    Scientific Notation: 2.761720 x [tex]10^{6}[/tex]

Manila - 1, 600, 000

    Scientific Notation: 1.60 x [tex]10^{6}[/tex]

Caloocan City -1,  500, 000

    Scientific Notation: 1.50 x [tex]10^{6}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Scientific notation is a shorthand method to write very large or very small numbers. Numbers are expressed in terms of exponents of 10 in a scientific notation. To write a number in scientific notation, we follow the steps below.

1. Move the decimal point to the right of the first digit in the number. Take note that if the number is a whole number, the decimal point is after the last digit or number.

  For example, the number is 2, 761, 720, we move the decimal point to the right of the first digit making it 2. 761720.

2. Count the number of places n that you moved the decimal point.

  In the previous example, we moved the decimal point six times.

3. Multiply the decimal number by 10 raised to a power of n. The n here is the number of times we moved the decimal point.

  The number now based on the example is 2.7610 x  [tex]10^{6}[/tex]

4. Indicate the sign of n or the power. If the decimal point was moved to the left as in a very large number, the n is positive. If the decimal point is moved to the right as in a very small number, the n is negative.

   In the previous example, since we move the decimal to the right then n is positive, giving us 2.7610 x  [tex]10^{6}[/tex]
