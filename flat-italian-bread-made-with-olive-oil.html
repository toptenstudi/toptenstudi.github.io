1. An italian appetizer composed of meats, sea foods, and other relishes is hors d'oeuvre

This Hors D'Oeuvre is the “outside dish” 3-course meal. Types of food that can be finished in 1-2 bites (minimum / without the need for cutlery), served before the main meal, usually accompanied by a cocktail. Or it can also be served at a cocktail party when there is no main course.

2. Small food item eaten with wine or other drink is tapas

Tapas is a Spanish term or culture that means a variety of foods served in small portions.

3. A salted roe or egg of the sturgeon is caviar

Caviar is a food made from the eggs of certain fish, generally sturgeon fish, which have been processed and salted. These foods are commercially marketed as luxury foods, and are generally used as a complement to other foods.

4. Hors d'oeuvres offered to guest that complements the chef's cooking style and talent is amuse bouche

Amuse-bouche means "pleasant mouth" in French, which is food served before appetizers. If in a restaurant, this food is not listed on the menu, not ordered by customers, but served before the meal session begins.

5. Toasted italian bread flavored with garlic and olive oil is bruschetta

Bruschetta is an Italian antipasto that was first discovered in the 15th century. The main ingredient of this food is bread. The main ingredients are toasted bread, garlic, olive oil, salt and pepper. Bruschetta bread is made from flour dough which is then given butter and garlic.

21. You add 7/3 tablespoons of olive oil for every 8/6 tablespoons of balsamic vinegar to make 44 tablespoons of bread dip. How much olive oil and balsamic vinegar do you use?


Answer:

28 tablespoons of olive oil and 16 tablespoons of balsamic vinegar are used to make 44 tablespoons of bread dip.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let the common factor of tablespoons be x.

Number of tablespoons:

Olive oil: (7/3)x

Balsamic vinegar: (8/6)x

Equation:

(7/3)x + (8/6)x = 44

(7/3)x + (4/3)x = 44

(11/3)x = 44

x = 44(3/11)

x = (40(3)

x = 12

Substitute 12 to x:

Number of tablespoons:

Olive oil: (7/3)x  ⇒  (7/3)(12) = 28 tablespoons

Balsamic vinegar: (8/6)x  ⇒  (8/6)(12) = 16 tablespoons

28 tablespoons of olive oil and 16 tablespoons of balsamic vinegar are used to make 44 tablespoons of bread dip.

Check:

28 + 16 = 44

44 = 44 (True)
