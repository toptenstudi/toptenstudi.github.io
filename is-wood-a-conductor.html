23. why is it that metal is a good conductors of heat and electricity while wood is not?​


In metals, the outer electrons in its atoms are more loosely bound than in wood. Metal atoms are packed more densely and can transmit heat vibrations more readily.

[tex]\color{blue}\huge\bold{ANSWER}[/tex]

<<<<<<<<<<<<<<<<<⊕>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>

BEFORE WE ANSWER THE QUESTION, LET US FIRST KNOW WHAT A CONDUCTOR AND INSULATOR IS

What are conductors?

Conductors are materials and substances that conducts heat and electricity. In other words, conductors are the materials that allow heat and electricity to flow from one object to another. A very good example of conductors are metals.

What are insulators?

Insulators are materials and substances that conduct neither heat nor electricity. In other words, these are the materials that are incapable of transferring heat and electricity from one object through another. A  very good example of an insulator is wood, plastic, and glass.

<<<<<<<<<<<<<<<<<⊕>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>

WHY IS IT THAT METAL IS A GOOD CONDUCTORS OF HEAT AND ELECTRICITY WHILE WOOD IS NOT?

A metal is a good conductor of heat and electricity rather than wood because a good conductor consists of atoms that are loosely bound and free to move through the material and are packed more densely to transfer heat vibrations easily.

A wood is not a good conductor of heat and electricity because a wood is an insulator, and an insulator consists of atoms that do not move around easily. This prevents particles from transferring a large amount of heat and increasing the temperature. In insulators, heat can still be transferred from one object to another, although the amount of heat from the energy particles are minimal.

25. Why are some pots and pans designed with wooden handles? A. Wood is a conductor. B. Wood has a high heat capacity. C. Wood is an insulator. D. Wood is an organic substance.


Answer:

C. Wood is an insulator.

Explanation:

An electrical insulator is a material in which the electron does not flow freely or the atom of the insulator have tightly bound electrons whose internal electric charges do not flow freely; very little electric current will flow through it under the influence of an electric field.

Wood is a material or covering that electricity, heat, or sound cannot go through.

Wood is a natural insulator due to air pockets within its cellular structure, which means that it is 15 times better than masonry, 400 times better than steel, and 1,770 times better than aluminum.
