Answer:

Two angles that have the same initial side and share the same terminal side are called coterminal angles.

Explanation:

3. which of the following describes a positive angle? A. The terminal side rotates counterclockwise B. The terminal side rotates clockwise C. The initial side rotates counterclockwise D. The initial side rotates clockwise ​


Answer:

The terminal side rotates counterclockwise. (A)

Step-by-step explanation:

The terminal side always starts at the initial side and the angles only become positive if the terminal side rotates in a counterclockwise direction.

Thus, the correct answer is option A.

Reference Angles is the angle formed by a terminal side of a given angle (x°) and the x-axis. 

If the terminal side of the the given angle, x°, falls on:

Quadrant I:  Reference Angle = x°

Quadrant II:  Reference Angle = 180° - x°

Quadrant III:  Reference Angle = x° - 180°

Quadrant IV:  Reference Angle = 360° - x°
