2. non living things which is growth by external addition of substances​


In a non-living organism, The process of growth is by the accumulation of materials on the surface.

The living organism begins life as a single cell.

Unicellular organisms may stay as one cell but they grow too.

Multicellular organisms add more and more cells to form more tissues and organs as they grow. Growth and development of living organisms are not the same things.

Hence, Growth in non-living objects is external as the material is accumulated on the surface and Growth in living organisms is internal as biomass is synthesized in the cell.

30. environment is the sum of all external forces influences and condition which affect the life nature behavior and the growth development and maturation of living organism True or False​


Answer:True

Evidence:

According to Douglas and Holland (1947), the environment describes, in the aggregate, all of the extrinsic (external) forces, influences, and conditions, which affect the life, nature, behavior, and the growth, development, and maturation of living organisms.
