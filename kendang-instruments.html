14. ASSIGNMENT Give the meaning of the following musical instruments: 1.Gong 2.Kendang 3.Saron 4.Bonang 5.Kenong​


Answer:

Gongs- are made of bronze and iron. They are struck on the central bump called pencu. The largest gong in a gamelan ensemble is called the gong ageng.

Kendang- a double-ended drum beaten by hands. There are 5 sizes of kendang ranging from 10 cm to 45 cm

Saron-It normally has seven bronze bars placed on top of a resonating frame (rancak) and is struck with a wooden mallet. It is usually about 20 cm (8 in) high, and is played on the floor by a seated performer.

Bonang-It is a collection of small gongs (sometimes called "kettles" or "pots") placed horizontally onto strings in a wooden frame (rancak), either one or two rows wide. All of the kettles have a central boss, but around it the lower-pitched ones have a flattened head, while the higher ones have an arched one.

Kenong- large kettles, resting horizontally, which divide the major phrases evenly (playing simultaneously with the gong or siyem at the end of major phrases, and subdividing evenly in between).
