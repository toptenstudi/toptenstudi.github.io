1. what is drawing conclusions in practical research?​


Answer:

Drawing conclusions in practical research is the process of making a judgment or decision based on the findings of a study. It involves interpreting the data collected from the research to determine whether the research hypothesis has been supported or not.

In practical research, drawing conclusions typically involves analyzing the data using statistical methods to identify any patterns or trends that may be present. Once the data has been analyzed, the researcher can draw conclusions about the relationship between the variables being studied, and make recommendations or decisions based on the results.

It is important to draw conclusions carefully and accurately, as these conclusions can have significant implications for future research or practical applications. It is also important to acknowledge any limitations or uncertainties in the research findings and to avoid making claims that are not supported by the data.

Placing used tires in the sea, regardless of the intention, can have both positive and negative impacts on the marine environment.

One potential positive impact is that the used tires can create artificial reefs that provide a habitat for marine life. These reefs can help support the growth of fish populations and other marine organisms, and they can also provide a destination for recreational divers and fishermen.

However, there are also significant negative impacts associated with this practice. As the tires degrade, they can release chemicals and toxins into the water that are harmful to marine life. The tires can also trap and entangle marine animals, causing injury or death. Additionally, the tires can cause damage to natural reefs and other marine habitats.

While the creation of artificial reefs can have some benefits for the marine environment, the negative impacts of placing used tires in the sea outweigh any potential benefits. Therefore, this activity should be stopped and alternative methods should be explored to support the growth of marine populations and habitats. These alternatives could include the creation of artificial reefs using environmentally friendly materials, or the restoration of natural reefs and other marine habitats that have been damaged by human activity.
