14. write your future contributions to slow down climate change ​


Answer:

1.Reduce your carbon footprint: One of the most effective ways to slow down climate change is to reduce your carbon footprint. You can achieve this by using public transport, cycling or walking instead of driving alone, eating less meat, using energy-efficient appliances and reducing waste.

2.Support renewable energy: Renewable energy sources like solar, wind and hydroelectric power are clean and sustainable. Supporting these sources of energy can help reduce the amount of greenhouse gases that are released into the atmosphere.

3.Plant trees: Trees are natural carbon sinks and can absorb large amounts of carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. Planting trees in your community, your backyard or supporting reforestation efforts can help combat climate change.

4.Educate others: Raising awareness about the causes and effects of climate change is essential in encouraging people to take action. By educating others about the impact of their actions on the environment, we can inspire people to make changes that benefit the planet.

5.Support climate-friendly policies: We can support policies that promote sustainable practices and reduce greenhouse gas emissions. This can include policies that encourage the use of renewable energy, reduce carbon emissions from transportation, and incentivize businesses to adopt sustainable practices.

By implementing these steps, we can all contribute to slowing down climate change and protecting our planet for future generations

16. 5. What are some positive effects of a slow change to an ecosystem?


It can allow certain organisms to adapt better.

For example, when an ecosystem starts to spray bug repellents at a slow rate around the world, some bugs with some resistance will survive. Then the offspring of those bugs will have more resistance, being a positive effect for the adaptation of the bugs.
