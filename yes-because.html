Two organisms rely on different resources

The correct answer is D. No, because the resources are different. ​

Intraspecific competition refers to competition between members of the same species, whereas interspecific competition refers to competition between individuals of different species. Natural selection, according to evolutionary theory, is influenced by resource competition within and between species. In ecology, interspecific rivalry is a type of competition in which individuals from different species compete for the same resources in the same ecosystem (e.g. food or living space). Mutualism, a sort of symbiosis, can be compared with this. While competition is inevitable in the natural world, it does not always occur in the same way. When various animals living in the same geographic area (sympatric species) compete for the same set of resources, such as food and space, this is known as interspecific competition.
