6. Spoken poetry about fake friend​


You smile at me with words so sweet

But behind my back, you spread deceit

You act like you’re my ally

But you’re just waiting for me to die

You tell me things I want to hear

But your actions speak so insincere

You’re like a snake, so sly and cunning

Your friendship is nothing but a running

You pretend to care about my life

But you’re only here to bring me strife

You’re a master of disguise

But I see through your web of lies

You’re a fake friend, I finally realize

It’s time for me to cut those ties

I’ll move on and leave you behind

A true friend is what I’ll find

So goodbye fake friend, I’ll let you go

The toxicity you bring, I’ll no longer sow

I’ll surround myself with positivity

And leave you in your own negativity.

Answer:

What would be the most healthful way to show a friend that you are a bit angry at them?

I believe that the best thing to do would be to tell the friend what he or she may have said that caused the problem. So in this case, we should encircle the letter (d).

Explanation:

Assuming both of them are reasonable, telling the offending friend would surely clear up everything, besides, if you are truly friends, these problems shouldn't even matter much. If this friend cared about you at all, he or she would apologize and never repeat the same act that started it.

We have all been guilty of offending someone at one time, there is no exception. We’ve also been on the receiving end of uncomfortably hurtful comments from people we know.
