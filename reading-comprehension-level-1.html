2. what is interpretive level, critical level and application level in level reading comprehension?​


Explanation:

On the interpretive level, students go a bit deeper and use inferring skills to think beyond the text or make guesses about characters. On the critical level, students ask important questions about the validity of the author or text.


3. explain the three levels of comprehensive reading​


Answer:

Reading comprehension is the ability to process information that we have read and understand its meaning. This is a complex process with three levels of understanding: literal meaning, inferential meaning, and evaluative meaning. Literal meaning is what the text describes as happening in the story

21. what Is the relationship between the profile and level of reading comprehension of senior high school students​


Answer:

The profile and level of reading comprehension of senior high school students are closely related. A student's profile in terms of reading comprehension includes their background knowledge, reading habits, language proficiency, and cognitive skills. Meanwhile, the level of reading comprehension refers to how well a student can understand and analyze a text.

Students who have a strong profile in reading comprehension tend to have higher levels of reading comprehension. For instance, if a student has a broad background knowledge, they may be able to easily understand and contextualize the information presented in a text. Similarly, if a student has good language proficiency, they may be able to understand the nuances of the language and vocabulary used in a text.

Moreover, cognitive skills such as critical thinking, analysis, and synthesis play a significant role in reading comprehension. Students who have well-developed cognitive skills are better able to comprehend and analyze complex texts. On the other hand, students who lack these skills may struggle with reading comprehension, even if they have a strong profile in other areas.

In summary, a student's profile in reading comprehension is an important factor in determining their level of reading comprehension. A strong profile in terms of background knowledge, reading habits, language proficiency, and cognitive skills can lead to higher levels of reading comprehension.
